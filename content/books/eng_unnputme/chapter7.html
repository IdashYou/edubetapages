7. MOST APPROPRIATE WORD AND COMMONLY CONFUSED WORDS

JAMB likes asking students understanding on vocabulary and the use of word contextually. We advise you to take your time and memorise this section.

JAMB Expected Questions

Section 1

1. He was [A. assisted B. duped C. enjoined. D. encouraged} by the trickster.

(duped. Dupe means to deceive or trick (someone) into believing or doing something).

 

2. When the soldiers saw that resistance was [A. inadequate B. inefficient C. futile D. successful], they stopped fighting

(futile: having no result or effect: pointless or useless). 

 

3. You should read all the ... [A. brochures B. prospectus C. tickets D. handouts] carefully before you decide where to go on holiday.

(brochures: a small, thin book or magazine that usually has many pictures and information about a product, a place).

 

4. The flight...... has been postponed (a) schedule (b) timetable (c) menu (d) manifest

(schedule: a procedural plan that indicates the time and sequence of each operation).

 

5. We have time to ____ before the gallery opens (a) make (b) waste (c) conserve (d) keep

(conserve)

 

6. They all gathered to exhume the __ musician's corpse for examination (a) posthumous (b) post-mortem (c) post-natal (d) orthopaedic

(post-morteman: examination of a dead body to find out the cause of death).

 

7. The man was given degree despite the fact that he did not attend a __ university (a) an honorary (b) an honourable (c) a ceremonial (d) a ceremonious
(an honorary)
 

8. ... bomb had earlier been defused A. A leaf B. An alive C. A life D. A live
(A live)
 

9. The House and The Senate will __At noon next Wednesday to hear address by the president
A. convene. B. adjourn. C. rise. D. collude
(convene: to come together in a group for a meeting).


10. At the --- of the century many ways of doing things were introduced.
A. turn. B. event. C. birth. D. sight
(turn)

 

11. The president said that the country was not out of the.... yet.
A. forest. B. fog. C. water. D. wood
(wood)


12. He went to the restaurant to enjoy the special…. 
A. suite. B. cuisine. C. a la carte. D. chef
(cuisine: food that is cooked in a particular way).

 

13. Abike must have found the very interesting movies quite....
A. absolving. B. absorbing. C. nauseating. D. perverting
(nauseating: causing you to feel like you are going to vomit).

 

14. When the strike is over, there will probably be an increase in wages and a      increase in prices
A. sporadic. B. concordant. C. concurrent. D. chronic.
(concurrent: happening at the same time)

 

15. The unconscious man was---- after receiving first aid.
A. reawakened. B. reformed. C. restored. D. revived
(revived: to make (someone or something) strong, healthy, or active again). 

 

Section 2
16. The laughter___ his face for a moment.
A. improved. B. controlled. C. animated. D. remade
(animated: full of life and energy, produced by the creation)

 

17. It was very easy for the two political parties to form a... government
A. co-operative. B. colonial. C. collusion. D. coalition
(coalition: the action or process of joining together with another or others for a common purpose)

 

18. The continuous rain has really ... the soil.
A. melted up. B. mopped up. C. satiated. D. saturated
(saturated: completely wet)

 

19. Vacancies in the company will be notified by ___[A. bulletin B. publication C. publicity D. advertisement].

(Publication: the act or process of producing a book, magazine, etc., and making it available to the public).

 

20. The driver was short of petrol, so he __ [A. glided B. coasted C. wheeled. D. taxied] down the hills with the engine switched off.

(Glided: to move in a smooth way).

 

21. Her mother brought her some     [A. clothes B. yards C. cloth D. clothing].

(clothes)

 

22. For more productivity, the company is focusing attention on the possible ___[A. synergy B. tapping C. alignment D. arrangement] of available resources.

(tapping)

 

23. The driver died in the __ [A. fatal B. brutal C. serious D. pathetic] road accident.

(fatal: causing death: causing ruin or failure).

 

24. Bola studiously avoided… the question
(a) parrying (b) answering (c) projecting (d) destroying
(answering)
 

25. When we looked up, we……. the plane some miles away A. site B. cited C. sited D. sighted
(sighted)

 

26. Western education is one of the…. of colonial rule. A. legacies B. evidence C. remnants       D. inheritance

(legacies)

 

27. The federal government has…… child trafficking A. postulated B. projected C. prescribed D. proscribed

(proscribed)


28. The man was happy that his son confessed his guilt and so the others were…… A. Implicated B. accused C. punished D. exonerated
(exonerated)
 

29. Many people would always find reasons to……the law A. arrogate B. debase C. circumvent D. circumspect
(circumvent)
