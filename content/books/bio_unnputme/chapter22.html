REPRODUCTION

Reproduction is a process by which a living organism produces offspring. 

Sexual reproduction 

The fusion of male gamete (or sperm) and female gamete (or egg). 

Asexual reproduction 

A reproduction without gametes (sex cells), reproducing by reproductive processes (as cell division, spore formation, fission, or budding) that do not involve the union of individuals or gametes

Alternation of generations

 A form of life cycle in which the asexual reproductive form alternates with the sexual reproductive form. Both forms are different in physical appearance, habit and reproduction. Moss plant undergoes alternation from gametophytic generation, represented in the main plant body alternates with sporangium, representing sporophyte generation, which is permanently attached to gametophyte. 

Binary fission 

A form of asexual reproduction in unicellular organisms by which the parent cell divides into approximately equal daughters. If the division produces two equal daughters, it is called binary fission while more than two, it is called multiple fission. 

Budding

A type of asexual reproduction in which a small new individual splits off from the body of the parent to develop into a new individual. 

Conjugation 

A form of sexual reproduction in which two equal reproductive cells of the same species such as Spirogyra, bacteria (e.g. Escherichia coli and Ciliates e.g. Paramecium) fuse temporarily to exchange genetic materials. 

Fragmentation  

Fragmentation is a form of asexual reproduction wherein a parent organism breaks into fragments, each capable of growing independently into a new organism.

Vegetative reproduction (propagation) 

A type of asexual reproduction in plants in which a part of the plant is detached and externally develops into a complete plant. 

Oviparous reproduction (oviparity) 

Laying eggs in which the embryos hatch externally, e.g. amphibians, most fish and birds. 

Ovoviviparous reproduction (ovoviviparity) 

The production of eggs that have been developed and nourished internally without placenta and hatch before or soon after extrusion of the eggs e.g. tsetse fly, snakes, lizards, and snails. 

Viviparity

Bringing forth the young alive that have been nourished internally through placenta e.g. all placenta mammals (man, goat, sheep, eat, etc.).

Ovulation is the release of an egg from one of a woman's ovaries. After the egg is released, it travels down the fallopian tube, where fertilization by a sperm cell may occur. Ovulation typically lasts one day and occurs in the middle of a woman's menstrual cycle, about two weeks before she expects to get her period.

Fertilization means the process of combining the male gamete, or sperm, with the female gamete, or ovum. The product of fertilization is a cell called a zygote.

Human fertilization is the union of a human egg and sperm, usually occurring in the ampulla of the fallopian tube. The result of this union is the production of a zygote cell, or fertilized egg, initiating prenatal development.

Gestation, in mammals, the time between conception and birth, during which the embryo or fetus is developing in the uterus.

Placenta: A particular organ built up of material and fetal tissue forms a connection jointly is called placenta. Implantation is completed within 4-5 days after reaching the embryo in the uterus. The placenta is a disc shaped and blood vessel enriched organ composed of cells derived from the fetus and the endoderm of uterus. In this way a temporary unseparated organ is formed between the fetus and endoderm of mother’s, uterus. At the time of child birth placenta is eliminated from the body.

Placenta acts more or less tike a lung. The dissolved oxygen in the blood of the maternal blood diffuses into the fetal blood and exchange carbon di-oxide from the fetus to the mother. Placenta also acts as kidney; metabolic waste product is diffused from the embryonic blood capillaries into the mother’s blood stream.

Sperm are produced in the testicles and develop to maturity while traveling from the seminiferous tubules through the epididymis into the vas deferens.

Two types of twins exist, identical (monozygotic) and fraternal (dizygotic). Identical Twins occur when a single egg is fertilized by a single sperm to form one zygote but the zygote then divides into two separate embryos. Fraternal Twins (non-identical twins) usually occur when two fertilized eggs are implanted in the uterine wall at the same time, that is when the mother releases two eggs and both become fertilized by two different sperms. 

The ejaculatory ducts (ductus ejaculation) are paired structures in male anatomy. Each ejaculatory duct is formed by the union of the vas deferens with the duct of the seminal vesicle. They pass through the prostate, and open into the urethra at the seminal colliculus.

Menstruation, also known as a period or monthly, is the regular discharge of blood and mucosal tissue (known as menses) from the inner lining of the uterus through the vagina.

Mentstrual (oestrous) cycle 

The series of monthly changes that occur in the reproductive organs of a mature female human (including some primates) to release an egg on the average of every 28 days under the influence of hormones. 

Ovary, in botany (study of plants), enlarged basal portion of the pistil, the female organ of a flower. The ovary contains ovules, which develop into seeds upon fertilization. The ovary itself will mature into a fruit, either dry or fleshy, enclosing the seeds.

In Double Fertilization in plants embryo, the first male gamete fertilizes the egg to form zygote while the other male gamete fuses with another cell, the polar nuclei which develops into endosperm (food storing tissue).  

Bisexual or perfect flowers have both male (androecium) and female (gynoecium) reproductive structures, including stamens and an ovary. Perfect flowers can pollinate itself and does not need wind, insects or other means to transfer its pollen. Examples of crops with perfect flowers include apples, cherries and legumes.

Flowers that contain both androecium and gynoecium are called hermaphroditic.

Wattle is a fleshy and sometimes red process found in the head or neck of some birds like turkey. Can serve for sex attraction
