Beat

Beat is produced by the interference of two waves with almost identical frequencies. Beat = f1 – f2. 

The presence of beats between two notes enhances the pleasure of listening to musical notes.

A Sonometer is a device for demonstrating the relationship between the frequency of the sound produced by a plucked string, and the tension, length and mass per unit length of the string. 

The relationship between frequency and length is an inversely proportional. This means that if the length is doubled the frequency is halved.

Acoustic echo sounders are used to measure the depth of the sea by using echo of sound as measure. The instrument transmits a 10 -30KHz sound that gets reflected from the sea floor. The echo reflected from the sea floor determines the depth of the sea in consideration.

Superposition states that the amplitude caused by two or more waves traversing the same space, is the sum of the amplitudes which would have been produced by the individual waves separately. In other words, it is the combining of waves.

If both tuning forks are vibrated together, then they produce sounds with slightly different frequencies. These sounds will interfere to produce detectable beats. The closer the two frequencies, the lower the beat frequency, and this will become zero when they are perfectly in tune.

Beat frequency (f) = f2 - f1
