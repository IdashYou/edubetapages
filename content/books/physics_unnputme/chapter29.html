Reflection of Light Waves (Mirror)

 

Non luminous objects are only seen when they reflect or throw back light from a luminous object like the sun or candle light. Light shined on a non-luminous object is either absorbed, reflected or transmitted. Sometimes the combination of two.

  • If the surface of an object is opaque, the object absorbed light.
  • If it is transparent, it could transmit light.
  • 1f the body transmits light, but such that an object cannot be seen through it. the body is translucent. 

The laws of reflection.

  1. The incident ray, the reflected ray and the normal at the point of incidence all lie in the same plane.
  2. The angle of incidence (i) is equal to the angle of reflection (r).

A real image is an image that can be caught on a screen. Light rays actually pass through a real image.

A virtual image is an image that cannot be caught on a screen. It is one through which rays of light do not actually pass but which is nevertheless visible to the eye. 

Images in a plane mirror are 

  • Perverted or laterally inverted 
  • Virtual (real image can be produced if the object is virtual)
  • Have the same size as the object

Two main type of reflection 

1. Regular reflection: parallel beam is reflected as a parallel beam. It occurs in smooth surfaces

2. Diffuse reflection: although a parallel beam may be incident on a surface, the reflected beam is not parallel. This happens with rough surfaces. The reason for the diffuse reflection is that because of the rough surface, different angle of incidence are presented to the incident beam, but at each point the laws of reflection are obeyed.

Example

A ray of light is incident on a plane surface at an angle of 40°. What is the angle of reflection?

A. 40°, B. 80°, C. 200°, D. 50°

D (2014/2015, no 3).

Since angle of reflection is equal to the angle of incidence. Both angles are measured with respect to the normal to the mirror.

The angle of incidence = angle of reflection. Note that the given angle is not the angle of incidence. Given that the angle between the plane mirror and the incident ray is 40. Since the angle between the normal and the plane is 90. 

The angle of incident = 90 – 40= 50

Since the angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection, the angle of reflection = 50.
