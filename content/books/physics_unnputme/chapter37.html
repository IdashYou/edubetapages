Magnetic Fields

 

A magnetic field is said to exist in a region if a force can be exerted on a magnet. If a compass needle is deflected when it is put at a particular location, we say a magnetic field exists at that point, and the strength of the field is measured by the strength of the force of the compass needle.

Magnetic fields are oval in shape.

The term dipole means two poles, corresponding to the fact that a dipole magnet typically contains a north pole on one side and a south pole on the other side. The two poles of a magnetic dipole must always have equal and opposite strength, and the two poles cannot be separated from each other. A magnetic monopole is a hypothetical elementary particle that is an isolated magnet with only one magnetic pole (a north pole without a south pole or vice versa). A magnetic monopole would have a net "magnetic charge".

Angle of dip is the angle between the direction of the earth’s resultant magnetic field and the horizontal. 

The angle of dip is 90 degree at the magnetic poles and zero at the equator. The strength of Magnet is greatest at the pole.

The force on a current carrying conductor in a magnetic field is greatest when the conductor lies perpendicular in the magnetic field.

When a conductor carrying a current is placed in a magnetic field, the conductor experiences a magnetic force. The direction of this force is always right angles to the plane containing both the conductor and the magnetic field, and is predicted by Fleming's Left-Hand Rule.

The magnitude of the force is maximum when the magnetic field and current directions are mutually perpendicular (at right angle or 90 to each other). The force decreases when the angle between the magnetic field and current directions is smaller than 90.

Angle of declination is the angle between the geographic and magnetic meridians at a place.

The principle at work in a dynamo is to primarily convert mechanical energy into electrical energy.

The electric dynamo uses rotating coils of wire and magnetic fields to convert mechanical rotation into a pulsing direct electric current through Faraday's law of induction and Lenz's law.

Right hand Grib Rule or Clenched Fist rule and Maxwell’s Corkscrew Rule describes the field line direction. 

Right hand Grib Rule or Clenched Fist rule states that if a straight wire is grasped with the right hand so that the thumb points in the direction of the current, then the direction in which the fingers are curled indicates the direction of magnetic field.

Maxwell’s Corkscrew Rule states that if a right-handed corkscrew is turned so that its tip travels along the direction of current, the direction of the magnetic field or magnetic lines of force.

Example

A. Ohm's law, B. Ampere's law, C. Faraday's law, D. Coulomb's law

C (2005/2006, no 12).

The principle of operation of the induction coil was given in 1831 by Michael Faraday. Faraday's law of induction showed that if the magnetic field through a coil is changed an electromotive force is induced whose value depends on the time rate of change of magnetic field through the coil.
