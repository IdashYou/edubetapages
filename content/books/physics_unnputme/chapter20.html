Equlibrum of forces

 

A body is in mechanical equilibrum if the resultant force is zero. Mathematically;

 

ΣFx = 0

ΣFy = 0

 

The conditions for equilibrum include;

  • 1. The linear acceleration, a, of its centre mass must be zero
  • 2. Its angular acceleration about any axis fixed this reference frame is zero.
  • 3. The resultant force is that single force which acting alone will have the same effect in magnitude and direction as two or more forces acting together.

 

The equilibrant of two or more forces is that single force which will balance all the other forces taken together. It is equal in magnitude but opposite in direction to the resultant force.

 

The sum of all forces acting on a body is Zero. Rotational equilibrium: If the net torque acting on a body is zero, then the body is said to be in rotational equilibrium. In such a case, the angular velocity of the body remains constant.

 

Moment of a point is equal the product of the force and the perpendicular distance of its line of action from the point. 

For the body in equilibrium the sum of the clockwise moments at a point equals the sum of the anticlockwise moments about the same point. 

 

Couple:A couple consists of two equal but opposite directed parallel forces not acting in a straight line.

 

The center of gravity of a body is the point through which the line of action of the weight always acts, or it is the point at which the entire weight of the body appears to be concentrated. 

Stable equilibrium; a body is said to be in stable equilibrium when it tends to return to its original position when slightly displaced. E.g. a cone resting on its base, a ball in the middle of a bowel.

Unstable equilibrium; a body is in unstable equilibrium if when slightly displaced it tends to move further away from its original position. E.g. an egg on its apex.

Neutral equilibrium; a body is in neutral equilibrium if when slightly displaced tend to come to rest in its new position. E.g. a ball or a sphere on a smooth horizontal table.

Wide base of support and low center of gravity make for good stability of a body.

 

Example

Which of the following is in a neutral equilibrium?

A. A heavy weight suspended on a string. B. A cone resting on its slant edge. C. A heavy-based table lamp. D. The beam of a balance in use.

B (2015/2016, no 7).

 

Archimedes Principle states that when a body is partially or totally immerced in a fluid, it experiences an upthrust which is equal to the weight of the fluid displaced.

The principle of floatation states that an object will float in a float in a fluid when the upthrust exerted upon it by fluid equals the weight of the object.

Relative density has no units.

Hydrometer is an instrument used in measuring relative density, its principle is that the less the density of liquid the further the hydrometer sinks in it. The height of the hydrometer sinked is inversely proportional to the density of the liquid.

Example

B (2009/2010, no 2).

Hydrometer is different from hygrometer.

A hydrometer is an instrument used to measure the relative density (specific gravity) of different liquids. This achieved by comparing the ratio of the density of the liquid to the density of water. 

Hygrometer is an instrument used for measuring humidity, the moisture content in the air of a specific area.

 

Example

Two balls of masses 40g and 60g respectively, are attached firmly to the ends of a light m rule. The centre of gravity of the system is

A. at the mid-point of the metre rule, B. 40 cm from the lighter mass, C. 40 cm from the heavier mass, 

D. 60 cm from the heavier mass

C (2014/2015, no 8).

 

Clockwise moment = anticlockwise moment

Moment = Force  Distance

40g  Xcm = 60g  (100 – Xcm)

40X = 60  (100 – X)

40X = 6000 – 60X

Collecting like terms

100X = 6000

X = 6000/100 = 60

X = 60cm.

The heavier mass = 60g

The distance between the heavier mass and the center of gravity is given in the diagram above as 100 – Xcm. Since X = 60cm.

The distance = 100 – 60 = 40cm

 

 

Example

A 90cm uniform lever has a load of 30N suspended at 15cm from one of its ends. If the fulcrum is at the centre of gravity. The force that must be applied at its other end to keep it in horizontal equilibrium is

A. 15N, B. 20N, C. 30N, D. 60N

 

B (2007/2008, no 3)

Length of uniform lever = 90cm 

The fulcrum is at the centre of gravity (the middle), then the distance between fulcrum and the end of the lever is 90/2 = 45cm. 

Since the force x distance in the right side = the force x distance in the left side

45 x F = 30 x 30 = 900 

F = 900/45 = 20N
