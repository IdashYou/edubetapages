The Bronsted Lowry theory which is also known as protonic concept defined Acid as a proton donor and a base as a proton Acceptor.

HCl (Acid) + H2O (Base)  Cl- (conjugate Base) + H3O+ (conjugate acid).

Arrhenius theory of Acid states that, an acid is a substance which dissolve in water to produce hydrogen ion or hydroxonium ion as the only positive ion. HCl + H2O  H3O+ + Cl-.

A base is a substance which dissolve in water to produce hydroxyl ion as the only negative ion. NaOH  Na+ + OH-.

Lewis Concept is that an acid is a substance that accepts electron (AlCl3, BF3), they are electron deficient while a base is a substance that donates a pair of electrons (NH3). Lewis acids are electrophile while Lewis base is nucleophile.

Neutralization is a process in which an acid reacts completely with an appropriate amount of an alkali (or any whet base) to produce a salt and water only. 

or Neutralization is the combination of hydrogen ions H+, and hydroxide ions, OH-, to form water molecules H2O and salt.

When you combine a weak acid (Organic acid - CHCOOK) and strong base (KOH, NaOH) it gives a Basic salt.

An acidic salt is formed by neutralizing a weak base with a strong acid and thus are similar chemically with the acid. NaHCO3 is an example of acidic salt.

KHSO4 + KOH ïƒ  K2SO4 + H20

Na2SO4 on hydrolysis produces an aqueous neutral solution (pH = 7)

Na2CO3 on hydrolysis produces an aqueous alkaline solution (pH > 7), this is because of excess OH ions

K2CO3 on hydrolysis produces an aqueous alkaline solution (pH > 7), this is because of excess OH ions

CuSO4 on hydrolysis produces an aqueous acidic solution (pH < 7), this is because of excess H- ions.

NB: Normal salt is neutral to litmus but the aqueous solution of sodium carbonate is alkaline to litmus paper because it undergoes hydrolysis to give strong base and weak acid.

 

The kind of salt formed from strong acid and weak base will possess all the properties of the strong acid (eg HCl) and is thus called an acidic salt. 

pH and POH

pH can be said to mean Power of Hydrogen. It is a measure of hydrogen ion concentration; the concentration of hydrogen ions presents in a solution.

pOH on the other hand, refers to the concentration of hydroxyl ions in a solution. Just like PH, pOH measures concentration except it's the hydroxyl concentration.

Relationship B/W pH and pOH

PH = -log [H+]

POH = -log [OH–]

[H+] [OH–] = 10-14

PH + POH = 14

Where [H+] and [OH–] represent the hydrogen and hydroxyl ion concentrations respectively. 

Calculating pH

The pH of a solution is the negative logarithms of hydrogen ion concentration 

pH = -log[H+], pOH = -log [OH-]

pH + pOH = 14

Example

Given that the pH of a solution of KOH is 12, what is the concentration oh he OH- ions? (2008/09 no 5)

A. 0.01 mol/dm3 B. 1 x 10-12 mol/dm3 C. 1 x 10-14 mol/dm3 D. 1 x 10-7 mol/dm3

Option A

Solution

pH = 12 [OH] =?

pH + pOH = 14

pOH = 14 -12 = 2

pOH = 2 

NOTE

pOH = - log10[OH]

10POH = 1/ [OH]

10-POH = [OH-]

[OH-] = 10-2 = 0.01mol/dm3

TIP: (A quick way to do this is to get the concentration of OH ions is to get the pOH and then write the number of zero equal to the pOH before writing 1, for example pOH of 3 will give 0.001mol/dm3. This works when the value is whole number, but when it is in decimal it will approximately be equal to the nearest whole number).
