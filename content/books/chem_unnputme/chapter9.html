Charle’s law: 

Charles law shows the connection between the volume change with temperature of a fixed mass of gas at constant pressure.

Charles's Law states that the volume of a given mass of gas varies directly with the absolute temperature of the gas when pressure is kept constant. The absolute temperature is temperature measured with the Kelvin scale. The Kelvin scale must be used because zero on the Kelvin scale corresponds to a complete stoppage of molecular motion.

Gay-Lussac's Law

When the temperature of a sample of gas in a rigid container is increased, the pressure of the gas increases as well. The increase in kinetic energy results in the molecules of gas striking the walls of the container with more force, resulting in a greater pressure.  Pressure and temperature at constant volume are directly proportional.

Kinetic theory of gases

The gas molecules move randomly in straight lines, colliding with one another and with the walls of the container. These collisions constitute the gas pressure. 

The pressure is as a result of two factors 

(1) Molecular collision 

(2) Molecular collision with the walls of the container. 

But molecular collision is always neglected because the pressure mainly results from the molecular collision with the walls of the container.

An increase in temperature leads to an increase in the kinetic energy of the gas particles, an increase in the frequency (or number) of collisions and an increase pressure.

The temperature of the gas is proportional to the average kinetic energy of its molecules. Faster moving particles will collide with the container walls more frequently and with greater force. This causes the force on the walls of the container to increase and so the pressure increases.

Average intermolecular distance decreases when a given mass of gas is compressed relative to its initial volume leading to increase in the frequency of collision.

JAMB Examples

(1) A balanced chemical equation obeys the law of 
A. conservation of mass 
B. definite proportions 
C. multiple proportions 
D. conservation of energy 

(Every chemical equation obeys a basic law which is the conservation of mass. In its simplest term, the law of conservation of mass states that the total mass of the reacted substances will be the same as the combined mass of the products formed. 

This means that the same grams of reactants will be the same grams of products that would be formed. The rest laws like definite or multiple proportions and conservation of energy are true but only for specific reactions. 

The law of conservation of energy for instance, is only significant for nuclear reactions where both matter and energy is changed. Hence, Option A 2018)

 

 

(2)  basic postulate of the kinetic theory of gases is that the molecules of a gas move in straight lines between collisions. This implies that 

A. collisions are perfectly elastic 
B. forces of repulsion exist 
C. forces of repulsion and attraction are in equilibrium
D. collisions are inelastic

Answer C. 2018.

The assumption that molecules of gas move in straight lines means the attractive/repulsive forced between the molecules are negligible.

In other words, it implies both the repulsion and attraction forced in the molecules cancel each other (Option C)
