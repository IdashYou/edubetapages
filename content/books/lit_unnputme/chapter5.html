5. REVIEW OF GENERAL LITERARY PRINCIPLES QUESTIONS

1. A literary genre which directly imitates human action is

A. drama

B. comedy

C. prose

D. poetry (JAMB 2012/2013, No 37)

Option A is the answer. 

Drama is the representation of life. It makes use of characters that imitate real life human beings. See Shakespeare's "All the world's but a stage..." 

 

2. A fable is a story in which

A. allegations are made about characters

B. animals or things are used as characters

C. there is an important setting

D. the story is told in poetic form

(JAMB 2012/2013, No 38)

Option B is the correct answer. 

A fable is a story that makes use of animals as characters. Example is George Orwell's Animal Farm

 

3. The account of experiences of an individual during the course of a journey is known as 

A. a travelogue 

B. an autobiography 

C. a catalogue 

D. a memoir

(JAMB 2009/2010, No 39)

Option A is the correct answer. 

A travelogue is a description of someone's travels, given in the form of narrative, public lecture, slide show or motion picture. Example is Byron's The Road to Oxiana. 

 

4. Satirical writing employs _.

A. epigram 

B. synecdoche 

C. irony 

D. onomatopoeia. 

(JAMB 2009/2010, No 40)

The correct option is option C, irony. 

Satire is a literary device of writing or art which principally ridicules its subject often as an intended means of provoking or preventing change. Satire employs three strategies to achieve its intended effects and they are irony, humour and exaggeration (hyperbole).  

 

5. A formal dignified speech or writing praising a person or a thing for past or present deeds is 

A. premiere 

B. eulogy 

C. anthology 

D. lampoon

(JAMB 2010, No 33)

The answer is option B, eulogy. 

 

To eulogize simply means to praise

A eulogy is a speech or writing in praise of a person or persons or things, especially one who recently died or retired or as a term of endearment. It can be written for both the living and the dead. Elegy and dirge are particularly meant for the dead. 

 

6. The narrative style in which the hero tells his own story directly is the __.

A. objective 

B. subjective 

C. first- person 

D. third-person.

(JAMB 2010, No 34)

The correct option is option C. 

Just like in personal pronouns the first-person personal pronoun refers to the person speaking to others about something he is or did, the first-person style of narrative is the style one adopts when one wants to tell a story about oneself to others. In this style, the protagonist is same as the writer. The use of such first-person pronouns as I, me, we, us, mine, ours are used. The reflective pronouns such as myself and ourselves can also be used. 

Because the narrator narrates his own story, he may not be able to recapture the events of the story accurately as some events take place in his absence. This makes the story likely unreliable. Most naive narrators fall under this style of narrative. First person style of narrative is subjective. 

 

7. The physical, historical or cultural background of a literary work is referred to as 

A. episode 

B. plot 

C. time 

D. setting

(JAMB 2010, No 35)

Option D, setting, is the correct option. 

Setting in literapolis refers to the time, place and circumstance in which the events of a story is set. 

 

8.  A plot structure that defies chronology can be described as 

A. open-ended 

B. circular 

C. episodic 

D. organic

(JAMB 2009/2010, No 36)

Option D, organic, is the correct answer. 

Organic plots arise from the situation and actions of the characters, which is to say, it is not fixed. Organic depicts living and one of the features of the living is growth and change. This leads to complexity of the plot.

 

9. The large space above the proscenium in a theatre from which the scenes are controlled is called 

A. aside 

B. setting 

C. anachronism 

D. flies (JAMB 2011/2012, No 31)

Option D is the correct answer. 

Flies refers to the open area above a stage where scenery and equipment may be hung. Flies is the only word among the options that refers to a part of a theater.

12. A literary work in which the characters and events are used as symbols is known as

A. characterization

B. allegory

C. metaphor

D. parallelism  

(JAMB 2012/2013, No 31)

Option B, allegory, is the answer. 

The representation of abstract principles by characters or figures is known as allegory. In allegorical works, characters represent certain phenomena that exist in the real world like truth, sin, death, failure, bribery, bravery, wisdom, etc. 

 

13. In literary work, verbal irony refers to a

A. device in which the speaker means the opposite of what he says

B. situation in which a character speaks or acts against the trend of events

C. difficult situation which defies a logical or rational resolution

D. device in which the actor on stage means exactly what he says

(JAMB 2012/2013, No 33)

Option A is the answer. 

There are three types of irony: verbal, situational and dramatic ironies. Situational irony depicts where the outcome or resolution of the events of a story defies the expectation of readers or audience. Dramatic irony speaks of a happening in the story or play that the characters are not aware of but the reader or audience is aware of it. Verbal irony refers to a literary device in which the speaker means the opposite of what he says. 

 

14. The main aim of caricature is to

A. describe

B. expose

C. emphasize

D. ridicule 

(JAMB 2012/2013, No 40)

Option D is the answer. 

The main aim of caricature is to ridicule. 

In literature, a caricature is a character who is given a combination of oversimplified and exaggerated characteristics and features. Caricatures can often be used for satirical purposes, or to generalize about a group of people. Authors often choose to use caricature in their works of literature to criticize qualities they see in their own societies.

 

15. A technique by which a previous scene or action can be recalled in a play to shed light on the present action is

A. Climax

B. Flashback

C. interlude

D. Catharsis 

(JAMB 2015/2016, No 12)

Option B, flashback, is the correct answer. 

Flashback is a dramatic device in which an earlier event is inserted into the normal chronological flow of a narrative. Flashback serves as a way of facilitating suspense because an event that the reader/audience ought to be aware of is cut off and not talked about so that when this event is revealed later, the audience begins to join the bits and pieces of the events, and every confusion starts to make sense. 

 

16. Criticism is a literary activity which seeks to

A. Find faults in a literary work

B. Analyse and evaluate a literary work

C. compare and contrast novels

D. Discover the beauty of a literary work 

(JAMB 2015/2016, No 14)

Option B is the correct answer. 

Criticism is done to analyze, review and evaluate a literary work. 

Criticism goes beyond finding faults in a literary work. Literature is subjective and as such, no text can be said to be flawless in a way. Criticism is done to unravel the strengths and weaknesses of a literary text. Criticism is divided into two: constructive and destructive criticism. When a teacher marks his student's scripts, the teacher is not only interested in the questions that his students failed but is also interested in the ones he got correctly. And the ones he failed are used to determine how much the student has learnt. This is evaluation. 

 

17. A band of singers and dancers in drama who act as a link between the play and the audience is the

A. Chorus

B. Clown

C. Playwright

D. Cast (JAMB 2015/2016, No 16)

Option A is the answer. 

The chorus refers to non-individualised group of singers in a play. These people, with their music, reveal the emotional status or progress of the protagonist or the play in general. They appeal to the emotions of the audience musically. While the actors went off-stage to change clothes and costumes, and perhaps prepare for their next role, it is the chorus that sustains or entertains the audience. 

The chorus would allow the playwright to prepare the audience for certain key moments in the storyline, build up momentum or slow down the tempo; he could underline certain elements and downplay others. 

The chorus would provide commentary on actions and events that were taking place before the audience. By doing this the chorus would create a deeper and more meaningful connection between the characters and the audience.
