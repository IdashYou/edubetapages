1. STATE

State is all political authority with sovereign power over a specific geographical area. It is a politically organized body of people for the purpose of government. Simply, it is a 

State is the most important agency or institution for the organization of political power. 

Characteristics of State 

  1. Sovereignty
  2. Government
  3. Population
  4. Defined territory
  5. Permanence
  6. Monopoly of power

Difference between a State and a Government

The government is the particular group of people, the administrative bureaucracy that controls the state apparatus at a given time. That is, governments are the means through which state power is employed. States are served by a continuous succession of different governments. States are immaterial and nonphysical social objects, whereas governments are groups of people with certain coercive powers.

Difference between a Society and a Nation

A society refers to a group of individuals involved in persistent social interaction, or a large social group sharing the same geographical or social territory, typically subject to the same political authority and dominant cultural expectations. 

Societies may also be structured politically. In order of increasing size and complexity, there are bands, tribes, chiefdoms, and state societies. These structures may have varying degrees of political power, depending on the cultural, geographical, and historical environments that these societies must contend with. But a society is not well structured so as to compete effectively with other competitors. 

A nation is a large group of people having a common origin, language, and tradition and usually constituting a political entity. When a nation is coincident with a state, the term nation-state is often used. The word nation comes from a Latin language word meaning “birth” or “place of birth.” The adjective is national. A nation depicts a people with common values, common people, common history and heritage. Homogeneous population is whereby a population or group of people have the same history, language etc. in a place.

A polity is an organized society, such as a nation, city, or church, together with its government and administration.

Political Socialization/Education 

Political socialization is the internalization of political culture or the way in which political values are formed and the political culture transmitted from one generation to the next. It is a process of acquiring new political orientations, norms and values of a political system. Political socialization preserves political culture of society. 

The dominant agent of political socialization is the family. In fact, political socialization begins in the family. Other agents of political socialization include: schools, market, mosque, church, government agencies, media, and political party. Socialization is direct and formal when the internalization of the new political behavior is consciously done but indirect and informal when the involved person unconsciously acquires the new orientation. 

The primary agent of political socialization is family, while secondary agents include schools, political party, mass media, church etc. Agents of socialization, sometimes referred to as institutions, work together to influence and shape people’s political and economic norms and values. Such institutions include: 

1. Families

2. Media

3. Peers

4. Schools

5. Religious bodies

6. work 

7. Legal systems.

Political socialization exposes the citizens to roles and expectations. It makes them to be patriotic, loyal and nationalistic. Political socialization also promotes stability in society. 

Type of Socialization

1. Indirect socialization/informal: This is when individuals unconsciously learn roles, skills and activities; that is without being aware of it. This can take the form of interpersonal transfer where orientation learned in other social bodies like the family, church or peer group are transferred into political roles. 

2. Direct socialization: This is a formal method of political socialization in which individuals consciously learn political behavior; that is being aware of it. It can take place through one’s imitation of behavior of others.

Political Culture 

Political culture refers to the political way of life of a given people. It is the political norms, values, attitude, orientations and morals. Political culture is the public attitude to politics and their roles within the political system. A nation’s political culture includes its citizens’ orientation to political system, the political and policy making process, and policy output and outcome. The political culture of a society determines the political behavior of the people Nigeria political culture include: democracy; rule of law; respect for constituted authority; respect for national symbols and values; constitutionalism; accountability; transparency, popular participation, etc. 

Example

A fundamental component of political culture is ---------

A. social values, B. family values, C. community structure, D. economic values.

Option A. social values

 

Political Participation 

Political participation refers to direct and indirect involvements and actions of the citizens in the process of governance. It refers to those lawful activities of citizens and groups that are aimed at selection of government officials and the activities they undertake. Some citizens are active participants that engage in law making and enforcement, party financing and campaigning as well as contesting for political position. Some participate indirectly by sponsoring candidates for an election. 

How Citizens Participate in Politics /Forms of Political Participation 

Voting, Criticism, Public opinion, political rally, Demonstration, party agents etc.

Public Opinion

Public opinion is an aggregate of the individual views, attitudes, and beliefs about a particular topic, expressed by a significant proportion of a community. According to G. A. I. Ofoegbu (1999), public opinion is the believes, values and attitudes which are commonly held and expressed by majority of the people on a given issue.

In some cases, public opinion are the views of infinitesimal (very small) number of members of the public which means it does not represent the opinion of majority members of the public, and this is a major disadvantage of public opinion. 

Methods of measuring public opinion are:

1. Direct Observation

2. Survey or Opinion Poll

3. Secondary Sources e.g Information from libraries

4. Election

5. Through Mass Media.

6. Referendum

Referendum is the submission to the electorate for approval or rejection of some aspects of legislation. It is a nice tool for measuring public opinion. This is so due to the fact that it gives out an aggregate view of the people's opinion on a particular public policy
