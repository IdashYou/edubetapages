1. PUBLIC CORPORATIONS

Public corporations are statutory organizations set up by the government through an Act of Parliament, decree or edict to provide essential service to the people at affordable rates. Public Corporation (state owned) is sometimes referred to as public enterprise or parastatals. It is state owned, non. profit oriented and financially autonomous. Public corporations or enterprises are also called parastatals, and they are established to render social services.

Structure of Public cooperation 

The minister: he is at the apex of the ranking of authority in public cooperation, he appoints the members of the board

The board of director:  they are appointed by government, serves as policy makers for public corporation and enterprise. The policy of the board must be in line with the government policies and priority, the board of directors approves the budget of the public corporation, monitor its performance and protect the interest of the corporation. 

General manager: directly below the board of directors in the authority structure is the general manager otherwise called the Chief Executive or managing director who is responsible for day-to-day administration of the corporation. 

Secretary to the board: he is not a management member of the staff but records the board transactions and decisions. He is normally a lawyer and gives legal advice to the cooperation

JAMB Examples

1. The main purpose of establishing public enterprise in Nigeria is to

A. increase government revenue

B. enrich the title

C. provide essential services

D. compete with the private sector

Option C. 2010

Explanation

The main purpose of establishing public enterprise in Nigeria is to provide essential services.

Public Corporations was set-up to provide essential services to the masses at a cheaper rate than would have been possible if left in the hands of private entrepreneurs. This also prevents exploitation and discrimination.
