1. INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS

OPEC

OPEC means the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries. 

OPEC was initially formed in September 1960 by the following countries Saudi Arabia, Venezuela, Iraq, Iran, and Kuwait. 

Then after a while some other countries joined which includes; Qatar, Libya, Indonesia, Abu, Dhabi, Algeria, Nigeria, Ecuador and Gabon joined the organization.

The major objective of OPEC is to devise ways and means of ensuring the stabilization of prices in international oil markets with a view to eliminating harmful and unnecessary

OAU

The Organization of African Unity was formed on May 25, 1963 in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia under the chairmanship of Emperor Haile Sellasia of Ethiopia. The OAU was founded on the principles of respect and the recognition of the sovereignty, equality and territorial integrity of member states and non-interference in the internal affairs of member states. Thabo Mbeki of South Africa was the leader of the defunct OAU who spearheaded the transformation of OAU to AU.

Organs of OAU 

1. Assembly of Heads of States and Government 

2. Council of Ministers 

3. General Secretariat 

4. Commission of Mediation, Conciliation and Arbitration

The OAU was formed for the promotion of African States’ unity and solidarity; the defence of sovereignty, independence and territorial integrity of African states as well as the advancement of Africa economic welfare. 

UNO

The UNO charter of 1945 aims at ensuring world peace and security and to take a collective measure for the prevention and removal of threats to the peace. 

The president of the United Nations General Assembly is a position that is voted by representatives in the united Nations General Assembly (UNGA) on a yearly basis. He is the chair presiding officer of the General Assembly.

The President of the United Nations Security Council is the presiding officer of that body. The President is the head of the delegation from the United Nations Security Council member state that holds the rotating presidency for a tenure of One Month. the presidency rotates monthly among the state members of the Security Council. The rotation takes place in alphabetical order of the member states' official United Nations names in English.

The Security Council is one of the organs of U.N.O (United Nations Organization) and it is assumed to be the most powerful of all the organs, making up the organization. 

Initially, it was made up of 5 permanent members also known as the Permanent Five, The Big Five (P5) and 6 non-permanent members, but it was enlarged in 1965, bringing 6 non-permanent members to 10, making it 15 members. The permanent members are: United States of America (USA), China, Britain, France and Russia. 

Commonwealth of Nations

The Commonwealth of Nations is a voluntary association of independent states most of which were once colonized by the United Kingdom and are former colonies. The Commonwealth is a successor of the British Empire in 1884. It is made up of 53 member states with headquarters at Marlborough House, London. The founding members of Commonwealth of Nations were United Kingdom, Australia, New Zealand, Canada, and South Africa. The head of Nigeria's foreign mission in a Commonwealth nation is known as high commissioner.

The appointment and exchange of High Commissioners among member nations of Commonwealth is one of the factors that bind member - nations of Commonwealth together.

ECOWAS

The idea of West African community came through the President of Liberia, who made the call in 1964 but later failed. The Economics Community of West African States (ECOWAS) emanated from a bilateral meeting between Nigeria and Togo under the leadership of General Yakubu Gown and General Gnasingbe Eyadema respectively. This very meeting resulted in trade agreements between the two countries in 1972. The aim of ECOWAS is to promote co-operation and integration in economic and cultural activity. In 1973, the Council of Ministers of the defunct Economic Integration. This noble idea of the Council of Ministers of defunct O.A.U later metamorphosed into the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS). The ECOWAS treaty was signed in Lagos on May 28, 1975 by fifteen West African States. Nigeria spearheaded the formation of ECOWAS during the regime of Yakubu Gowon. 

Nigeria participates in conflict resolution in Africa, one of which is seen in the formation of ECOWAS Monitoring Group (ECOMOG). ECOMOG was formed by the member-nations of ECOWAS- Nigeria. Ghana, Gambia, Sierra Leone and Guinea as a peace keeping force to mediate in Liberia's crisis. Most people believe that Nigeria single handedly set up the ECOWAS Monitoring Group (ECOMOG) to end the crisis in Liberia, and thus, was classified as a front-line state for helping to end the crisis in Liberia. Basically, Nigeria is in the forefront as far as ECOMOG and resolution of crisis in Liberia is concerned.
