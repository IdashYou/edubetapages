1. FORMS OF GOVERNMENT

Dictatorship: a form of government in which absolute power is concentrated in a dictator or a small clique

Monarchy is a government having a hereditary chief of state with life tenure and powers varying from nominal to absolute. In Britain monarchy power is transferred within the family from one generation to another and it is being headed by a king, queen or emperor. Usually, a monarch claims the rights to the title by way of hereditary succession or as a result of some sort of divine appointment or calling.

The small, oil-rich nation of Oman, for instance, is an example of an absolute monarchy. In this nation, Sultan Qaboos bin Said Al Said has ruled since the 1970s.

Tyranny: This exists where the government uses force or the threat of force to rule the people by suppressing them and preventing them from exercising their rights. 

Authoritarianism: This is the belief in or practice of absolute government in which authority is exercised regardless of popular consent. 

Aristocracy: this is a government in which power is vested in a minority consisting of those believed to be best qualified

Oligarchy: This refers to government by few privilege individuals who rule for their selfish needs. It is government control by few people for their own selfish interest. The Iron law of oligarchy was developed by Robert Mitchel. 

Autocracy: Autocracy is a style of government in which powers are concentrated in the hand of an individual or individuals who exercise the power without regard to the people and constitution. 

Republic/Republicanism: Republic or republicanism refers to a form of government is that which the head of state is elected by the people through periodic election. This means that a sovereign state is referred to as a republic if the country has no traditional ruler of his representative as its head of state but by the one chosen by the people with a fixed term of office. Such head of state is known as a President in a republic. The President may be an Executive of Ceremonial President. Republicanism is a perverted form of democracy. 

Democratic government: it’s a government of the people by the people, and for the people: a form of government in which people choose leaders by voting; an organization or situation in which everyone is treated equally and has equal rights.

Democracy is a system of government by the whole population or all the eligible members of a state, typically through elected representatives.

(i) Democracy is a form of government in which rulers are elected by the people on the principle of one person, one vote.

(ii)  Democracy must be based on free and fair elections, where those who are currently in power have a fair chance of losing. Voters should have a choice of candidates.

(iii)In a democracy, the final decision-making power must rest with those elected by the people.

Legal sovereignty shouldn't be unquestionable because in a democratic system, the powers of the legislature should be subject to necessary executive and judicial review respectively for the sale of checks and balance and accountability.
