1. LOCAL GOVERNMENT

Local government may be defined as a government established at through an Act at the local level to deal with specific matters as it affects them. Under the 1963 Republican Constitution, local government authorities were created mainly to deal with matters of local concern, e.g. markets, roads, motor parks, etc. 

Local governments are created through a document called ‘Instrument’. They are created like corporate bodies because they have legal existence (they can sue and be sued).

The purpose of local government creation is to involve local participation in the affairs of the country and to bring them into the realms of politics at the grassroots.

Functions of Local Government

1. Provision of Health Centres: Provision of primary Health centres such as maternity centres and dispensaries are provided at the grassroots.

2. Provision of Water: This involves the building of boreholes and wells are also constructed for the people.

3. Maintenance of Law and Order: Local government is capable of making bye-laws for the maintenance of law and order within it's territory.

4. Provision of Recreational Centres: Local governments provide and maintain centres like amusement parks for recreation purpose.

5. Naming of Streets: The Local Government are involved in the naming and numbering of streets and roads.

Sources of Revenue for Local Governments

1. Allocation from the Federation Account: The Local governments receive allocation of money from the federal government.

2. Grants: Another source of revenue is the special grants they receive from both the central and state governments, and it is mostly for specific projects needed by the Local Government.

3. Imposition of Levy: They also generate funds through the imposition of levies on the people. The levy may be for a special project meant to improve the welfare of the people.

4. Collection of Rates: Revenue is equally generated through the collection of rates on the radios and televisions, market shops, motor parks, etc.

Local government Reforms

The first major local government reform in Nigeria was carried out in 1976, by the government of Olusegun Obasanjo. Traditional rulers under the 1976 were assigned ceremonial and advisory roles.

7. The equivalent of the commissioner at the local government level is the

A. Executive chairman

B. Secretary

C. Councilor

D. Supervisory councillor.

Option D

Explanation

The equivalent of a commissioner at the local government level is the supervisory Councillor. 

In the state level of government, a commissioner is the highest or completely in-charge of a particular ministry. For instance, the commissioner for Education. He does not come in through election but through the appointment by the government of the state. In the Local government level, the supervisory Councillor for education as well as any other supervisory Councillor is appointed to the Executive Chairman of the local government subject to the approved of the Councillors.
