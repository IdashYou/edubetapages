1. SYSTEMS OF GOVERNMENT

Presidential System 

presidential system means a system of government in which a single individual known as president is the Head of State and Head of Government. Here the president plays dual functions of ceremonial and real executive matter. Thus, there is fusion of ceremonial and executive power. 

Nigeria, in 1979, first practiced a presidential system of government and Alhaji Shehu Shagari of National Party of Nigeria (NPN) was elected the first indigenous Executive President. Countries like USA, Brazil, Bolivia, Colombia, and Tanzania practice presidential system of government.

Features of Presidential System of Government 

  1. Unification of powers of Head of state and government
  2. Separation of power
  3. Supremacy of the constitution
  4. Presidential veto over law
  5. Appointment of ministers from nonparty members
  6. Checks and balances are in operation

Merits of Presidential System

  1. Supremacy of the constitution
  2. Appointment of qualified and competent ministers
  3. Separation of power
  4. Stability of government
  5. Accountability to electorate

Demerits of Presidential System

  1. Expensive cost
  2. Executive-legislative conflict
  3. Authoritarianism

Parliamentary System 

Parliamentary System or cabinet system of government exists where the functions of the Head of State are separated from that of the Head of Government and are carried and are carried out by two individuals, the president and the Prime minister respectively. 

The parliamentary system is marked for their lack of strict separation of powers between the executive and the legislature but in the presidential system there is total separation of powers.

A Prorogation means a proclamation by the head of state ending a session of parliament, it is the discontinuation of the meeting of a legislative body without dissolving it.

The parliamentary system is marked for their lack of strict separation of powers between the executive and the legislature but in the presidential system there is total separation of powers.

In a presidential and cabinet system of government, the ministers are individually responsible to the president, but the legislature are collectively responsible and thus they share both blame and praise. 

A presidential system of government is one where the Head of State possesses real powers.

Features of Parliamentary System

  1. Dual executive (head of states and head of government)
  2. Collective responsibility
  3. Ministers are chosen from the ruling party
  4. Absence of separation of powers
  5. Parliamentary supremacy

Merits of Parliamentary system

  1. Reduced executive Burden
  2. Less expensive to operate
  3. Quick passage of law
  4. Promotes accountability in government
  5. Better executive and legislature cooperation.

Demerits of Parliamentary system

  1. Political instability
  2. Distraction of government by opposition party or shadow government in the form of critism and distraction.
  3. Dispute between President and Prime minister
  4. Lack of separation of power

The Military Government 

The military protect the sovereignty and territorial integrity of a country from external invasion. The military in Nigeria include the army, the Air force and the Navy Military government is the type of government under the control of the armed forces of a state that comes to power through military coup or force. 

Military coup is forceful overthrow of government by the armed forces. Coup is a sudden and forcible seizure of government power through illegal and unconstitutional means. The military claim to be a corrective regime. They believe that they come to power to correct the misconducts of politicians. 

However, experience has shown that the military are not less corrupt than civilians. Because military rule is unconstitutional, the first thing that a military government does after successful coup over a civilian government is to suspend the constitution and rule by decree and edict. In military regime, there is fusion of executive and legislative power.

Revolution

Revolution is not the same thing with coup d’état. Revolution is a fundamental or radical change in government as well as socio-economic structure of a state while coup d’état is forceful change in government only. Revolution involves mass political action.

Features of Military Rule

  1. Coup d’etat
  2. Undemocratic
  3. Rule by decree and edict
  4. Citizens right can be violated
  5. Accountability is very poor
  6. Centralized structure and based on command system.

Merits of Military Rule

  1. Quick decision making
  2. Defense of sovereignty
  3. Development
  4. Easy state creation

Demerits of Military Rule/Government

  1. Undemocratic
  2. Unconstitutional
  3. Abuse of human Rights
  4. No rule of law
  5. Poor Accountability and absence of separation of power
