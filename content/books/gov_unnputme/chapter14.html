1. CIVIL CERVICE

The civil service or public bureaucracy is a permanent institution of government that has the primary responsibility of implementing government policy on day to day basis. The structure of the civil service is based on hierarchical organization. 

Structure of the civil service / bureaucracy 

Administrative Cadre

Executive Cadre

Clerical Cadre

Manipulative/ Auxiliary Cadre

Administrative Cadre

The administrative grade comprises the most senior civil servants who organize and coordinate the activities of the ministries, executes high level administrative works, He advises ministers in the formulation and implementation of policies, and as communication link between the minister and other civil servants under him. 

To qualify for absorption into the administrative cadre in Nigeria, an applicant must be a holder of a university degree inorder to qualify for it. Their recruitment is the therefore based on university education or equivalent educational qualification and long service as it was in the past.

Characteristics of a civil servant includes; 

Comprehensiveness, Anonymity Nonpartisan/ Political neutrality, Permanence, Professionalism/ expertise, Impartiality, Meritocracy, Bureaucracy, and Hierarchy.

Anonymity: The civil service or bureaucracy is unseen and no blame or praise go to it rather every blame and praise goes to the minister representing the government. The civil service do not serve a particular government.

Red tapism: is a disadvantage of Civil service in a bureaucratic organization and this causes delay in decision making. There is laid down procedure in making decision causing administrative delay.

The parliament and the courts control the Civil Service. The parliament has the power to monitor and control the performance of civil servants and also question any minister who misbehaves. 

In the other a citizen through law courts can question a civil servant misbehaving.
