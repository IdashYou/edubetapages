Summary of John 10: 1 – 18

Jesus again figuratively compared himself to the door and the good shepherd. According to him, anyone who does not enter the sheepfold by the door but climbs in through another means is a thief and a robber; but he who enters by the door is the shepherd of the sheep. To such a person, the gate keeper opens the gate for. 

The sheep hear his voice and he call his sheep by name and leads them out because they know him. On the contrary, the sheep would run away from the hireling because they do not know him and cannot recognize his voice. According to him, the thief would come through the window because he is not the owner and the sheep would not stand his sight. 

Jesus equally presented himself as the door of the sheep, insisting that all who came before him referring to the false prophets were thieves and robbers. He maintained that as the door, anyone who enters through him would be saved, and would go in and out to find pasture. He said that the thief only came to steal, kill and destroy, while he (Jesus) came that his flock may have abundant life. 

As the good shepherd, Jesus maintained that he came to lay down his life for the sake of his sheep, unlike a hireling who is not a good shepherd and therefore does not care for the sheep. 

According to Jesus, a hireling runs away whenever he sees a wolf, leaving the sheep because 

they do not belong to him. He further noted that there are other of his sheep that are not within 

the fold, but as a good shepherd, he would bring them back to the fold, so as to have one flock and one shepherd. The sheep outside the fold refer to the unbelievers, who would eventually be brought into the fold of which Christ is the head.
