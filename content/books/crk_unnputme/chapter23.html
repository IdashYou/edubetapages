23. JOSHUA AS A LEADER 

Why and how Joshua succeeded Moses (Numbers 13; Joshua 1)

Joshua the son of Nun was one of the twelve spies representing the tribe of Ephraim sent from the wilderness of Paran to spy the land of Canaan in order to find out its nature. He was originally known as Hoshea before Moses gave him the name Joshua. 

When the ten spies presented their frightening report to the congregation of Israel, it was Joshua and Caleb who comforted the Israelites with the message of hope of victory over the inhabitants of the land of Canaan, as already narrated. 

When Moses could no longer bear the task of leadership as a result of his old age, Joshua became a possible replacement. It was however noted that the Lord led him up into the mountain of Abarim to see the promised land and to die thereafter. 

Moses then prayed to God to appoint a man over the congregation to lead them in and out, that they may not be left like sheep without shepherd. Having made the request, the lord instructed Moses to take Joshua the son of Nun, a man in whom is the spirit and lay his hand upon him to commission him before Eleazar the priest and all the congregation. 

Moses was also directed by God to invest Joshua with some of his authority so that the people will obey him. After that Joshua will be made to stand before Eleazar the priest to inquire for him by the judgment of the Urim before the lord. Moses did as God directed him and thus Joshua became the leader of the Israelites after Moses. 

Following the death of Moses, God called on Joshua to take up the baton of leadership to lead the people of Israel to the promise land. God assured him that no man shall be able to stand before him all the days of his life and to be with him just as he was with Moses, and never to forsake or fail him.

He however encouraged him to be strong and courageous, and to be careful in keeping the law which Moses commanded him. God also encouraged him not to allow the book of the law depart from his mouth but to meditate on it day and night; for his success and prosperity depend on it. God finally assured him of his presence wherever he goes. He later died at the age of one hundred and ten.
