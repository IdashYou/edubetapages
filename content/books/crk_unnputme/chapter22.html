Summary of the Social conditions of the Hebrews at the birth of Moses

The number of the offspring of Jacob who moved down to Egypt where seventy. Following the death of Joseph, his brothers and all that generation, the descendants of Israel were fruitful and greatly increased; there came on the throne a new king in Egypt who did not know Joseph. 

The new king was disturbed by the increasing number of the Hebrews that he said to his people, “Behold, the people of Israel are too many and might for us. 

Come, let us deal shrewdly with them, lest they multiply, and if war befall us; they join our enemies and fight against us and escape from the land. ”As a result, taskmasters were set over the Hebrews to afflict them with heavy burdens. 

They were made to build for Pharaoh store-cities, Pithom' and Raamses. However, the more the Hebrews are Oppressed the more they multiply and spread, which the Egyptians to subject them to severe rigour and their lives were made bitter 'with hard services. This did not in any way affect their number as they kept increasing. 

In order to check the increasing number, ”Pharaoh instructed the two Hebrew midwives, Shiphrah and Puah to kilt upon the birth stool every male Child of the Hebrews, but to leave the male children, The two midwives who were God-fearing did not heed to the directives which made the king to summon them for cautioning. 

They replied the king that the Hebrew women have exceptional strength and deliver before they come unlike the Egyptain women. Because of the land gesture of the midwives God dealt well with them and gave them families. 

The final onslaught on the Hebrew was the command by the King that every son of the Hebrews should be cast into the Nile. It was during this offensive attack that Moses was born.

Summary of Exodus 2 and 3

Moses was born and being a male child, he was hidden by his mother for three months, but When she could no longer hide him, she put him in a basket and placed it among the reeds at the bank of river Nile. 

The daughter of Pharaoh came down to the river for her bathe in the Company of her maidens, she saw the basket and sent her maid to fetch it. 

When she opened it, she saw the child had pity on him and affirmed that the child was one of the Hebrews’ children. Immediately the sister of Moses who was hiding in the nearby offered to call a Hebrew woman to nurse the child, which the princess accepted. That was how Moses was brought up by his own mother and he came from the tribe of Levi. 

After nursing the child, the mother brought him back to the Pharaoh’s daughter who named him Moses, for she said, “Because I draw him out of the water.” One day when Moses had grown up, he was out to see the burden of his people when he saw an Egyptian beating a fellow Hebrew. 

He killed and hid the Egyptian in the sand. When he went out the next day, he saw two Hebrews struggling together and said to the man that did the wrong, “Why do you strike your fellow?” He answered, "Who made you a prince and a judge over us? Do you mean to kill me as you killed the Egyptian?" Moses was afraid and thought that Pharaoh would kill him if the news gets to him. 

Being uncomfortable he fled from Pharaoh to the land of Median where he sat down' by a well. There he helped the daughters of the priest of Median (Jethro) in watering their flock and delivered them from the hands of the shepherds. 

They reported this kind gesture to their father who instructed them to call Moses that he may eat bread in their house. Moses was contented to live with Jethro who gave him his daughter Zipporah in marriage. She gave birth to a son whom Moses called Gershom; for he said, “I have been a sojourner in a foreign land.

In those days, the people of Israel groaned under bondage in Egypt, they cried for help and their cry under bondage got to God who seeing their plight and knowing their condition remembered his covenant with Abraham, Isaac and Jacob their fathers. 

In response to the plight of the Israelites, God through his angel appeared to Moses at mount Horeb, the mountain of God where he was attending to the flock of his father-in-law in a flame of tire out of the midst of a bush. 

When Moses looked up, he saw a bush burning without consuming he said, “I will turn aside and see this great sight, why the bush is not burnt.” While making the move, God called him out of the bush, “Moses, Moses” And he said, “Here am I.” the voice then said, “Do not come near; put off your shoes from your feet, for the place on which you are standing is holy ground.” 

The voice again said, “I am the god of your father, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob.” On hearing this, Moses hid his face for he was afraid to look at God. 

The Lord told Moses that he has seen the affliction of his people in the land of Egypt and that their cry has come up to him, and he is therefore set to bring them out of the land to a comfortable land that is flowing with milk and honey, to the place of the Canaanites, the Hittites, the Amorites, the Perinites, the Hivites and the Jebusites. 

He called on Moses that he might go to Pharaoh to bring the people of Israel out of Egypt. But Moses being afraid said to him, “Who am l that l should go to Pharaoh, and bring the sons of Israel out of Egypt?” 

God promised to be with him and that he shall serve God on the mountain as a sign when the people come out of Egypt.

Moses asked God what he shall say to the people if they should asked for his name, and God said to him, “I AM WHO l AM.” God further asked Moses to tell the people of Israel that the God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob sent him to them. God directed Moses to meet the king with the elders of Israel to inform the king of their intention to go on a three-day journey into the wilderness to worship the Lord and offer sacrifice to their God. 

God however knew that the king will not grant the request unless compelled by a mighty hand. He then vowed to strike Egypt with so many wonders that at the end the people of Israel will be allowed to go. He promised to grant the Israelites favour in the sight of the Egyptians and advised them not to go empty but to go with their neighbors and belongings, so as to rob the Egyptians of great valuables. God equips and sends Moses to lead the Israelites out of Egypt.

Moses was still not convinced that the Israelites will believe or listen to him. In order to make the people believe Moses God first asked Moses what he was having in his hand and Moses responded “A rod.” He asked Moses to cast it on the ground and it turned to serpent and Moses fled from it. But the lord ordered him again to take it by the tail and it became rod in his hand. 

Secondly, God asked him to put his hand into his bosom and when he took it out, his hand was leprous, as white as snow. God again asked him to put his hand back into his bosom and when he did that his hand was restored to its normal state. 

God expressed assurance that the people will believe Moses but further maintained that if they fail to heed to the two signs that Moses should take some water from the Nile and pour it on the dry ground and it shall become blood upon the dry ground. 

Moses again complained of his lack of eloquence which God responded to by making Aaron his, spokesman. While Aaron was made his spokesman, Moses shall be to him as God. 

When Moses and Aaron met Pharaoh, the king of Egypt with the request to let the people go on three days journey into the wilderness to sacrifice to their Lord. A request which the king turned down with caution on Moses and Aaron to allow the people do their work. 

Following the unwillingness of Pharaoh to allow the people go but instead increased their burden, Moses turned to the Lord in disappointment and questioned why he sent him to speak to Pharaoh who has done evil to the people of Israel without the Lord delivering them. Moses was eighty years old and Aaron eighty-three years old when they spoke to Pharaoh.

Summary of their Exit 

Pharaoh finally allowed the Israelites to go after God had inflicted them with plagues, the last of it was the killing of the firstborn of both man and cattle. On their way out of Egypt, God instructed Moses to ask the people to encamp at Pi-ha-hi’roth. 

When Pharaoh heard that the people had fled, he regretted his action of letting the people go, as a result he set his army in pursuit of the Israelite just as the Lord hardened his heart. The Egyptians overtook them encamped at the sea by Pi-ha-hi’roth. 

When the people of Israel lifted up their eyes and saw Pharaoh drawing near, they were afraid Ind cried out to the Lord and blamed Moses for bringing them out into the wilderness to die, Insisting that it would have been better for them to die in Egypt while serving the Egyptians than to face the shameful death in the wilderness where there are no graves. 

Moses however said to them. “Fear not, stand firm, and see the salvation of the Lord, which he will work for you today; Moses asked God what he shall say to the people if they should asked for his name, and God said to him, “I AM WHO l AM.” God further asked Moses to tell the people of Israel that the God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob sent him to them. 

God directed Moses to meet the king with the elders of Israel to inform the king of their intention to go on a three-day journey into the wilderness to worship the Lord and offer sacrifice to their God. God however knew that the king will not grant the request unless compelled by a mighty hand. 

He then vowed to strike Egypt with so many wonders that at the end the people of Israel will be allowed to go. He promised to grant the Israelites favour in the sight of the Egyptians and advised them not to go empty but to go with their neighbors and belongings, so as to rob the Egyptians of great valuables. 

God equips and sends Moses to lead the Israelites out of Egypt. Moses was still not convinced that the Israelites will believe or listen to him. In order to make the people believe Moses God first asked Moses what he was having in his hand and Moses responded “A rod.” He asked Moses to cast it on the ground and it turned to serpent and Moses fled from it. 

But the lord ordered him again to take it by the tail and it became rod in his hand. Secondly, God asked him to put his hand into his bosom and when he took it out, his hand was leprous, as white as snow. God again asked him to put his hand back into his bosom and when he did that his hand was restored to its normal state. 

God expressed assurance that the people will believe Moses but further maintained that if they fail to heed to the two signs that Moses should take some water from the Nile and pour it on the dry ground and it shall become blood upon the dry ground. 

Moses again complained of his lack of eloquence which God responded to by making Aaron his, spokesman. While Aaron was made his spokesman, Moses shall be to him as God. 

When Moses and Aaron met Pharaoh, the king of Egypt with the request to let the people go on three days journey into the wilderness to sacrifice to their Lord. 

A request which the king turned down with caution on Moses and Aaron to allow the people do their work. Following the unwillingness of Pharaoh to allow the people go but instead increased their burden, Moses turned to the Lord in disappointment and questioned why he sent him to speak to Pharaoh who has done evil to the people of Israel without the Lord delivering them. 

Moses was eighty years old and Aaron eighty-three years old when they spoke to Pharaoh. 

God adopted the following means to save the fleeing Israelites from the hands of the Egyptians who were closely pursuing them: 

1. The angel of the Lord went before the Israelites and behind them. 

2. A pillar of cloud from before them and stood behind them, thereby barricading the Israelites from the Egyptians. 

3. Moses was instructed by God to stretch out his hand over the sea and the Lord drove the sea back by a strong east wind, and the sea became dry land for the Israelite to pass. 

4. The wheels of the chariots were clogged by God thereby making it difficult for them to drive easily. This measure made the pursuing Egyptians confess that the LORD fights for the Israelites. 

5. The final onslaught on the Egyptians was when God instructed Moses to stretch out his hand over the sea which came back and drowned the Egyptians, their chariots and horsemen. 

The effect of the encounter was that having seen the marvelous works of the Lord, the Israelites feared the Lord; and believed in the Lord and in his servant Moses.
