14. LABOUR MARKET

Labour Market 

Labour Market refers to a market where the wages and other conditions that determines employment are determined and negotiated. It is a market where factors of production are baught and sold.

Labour force

Labour force refers to the total number of people who fall within the age bracket working population, both those thatare working and those that are willing but have not gotten opportunity to work. Labour force is the number of the working population. It normally covers people fall in the range of 18 to 65years.

Supply of Labour (Supply of Effort)

This refers to the total number of people offered for employment at a given wage rate over a period of time. 

Factors that affect the size of Labour Force and Supply

  1. The Total size of population of a country
  2. The official school leaving age,
  3. Trade Union Activities
  4. Age Distribution
  5. The retirement age,
  6. The pursuit of higher education age
  7. Population structure
  8. The role of women in the society
  9. The number of working hours and working days, the number of disabled, the number of people willing to work, migration.
  10. Immigration

Efficiency of Labour

Efficiency of labour is the ability of labour to utilize a given input to produce greater output within a period of time. By 'efficiency of labour', we mean the productive capacity of A WORKER to do more or better work or both during a specified period of time. 

It can also be defined as the increase in production output as a result of combination of labour and other factors of production. 

Factors that Improve Efficiency of Labour

  1. Education and training of workers
  2. Promotion
  3. Improved working condition
  4. Appropriate Incentives
  5. Efficiency of other factors of production
  6. Technological improvement
  7. Job security
  8. Improved health facilities

There other factors that decrease efficiency of labour which includes, Red-tapism indiscipline of workers and lack of the factors mentioned above.

Mobility of Labour

Mobility of labour is the movement of labour from one place or job to another. 

Causes of Mobility of Labour

1. Unfavourable working condition

2. Marriage 

3. Irregular Payment of Salary

4. Bad Management 

5. Lack of Incentive

6. Unfavourable Climate

7. Government Policy

Trade Union

Trade unions are groups of employees who join together to maintain and improve their conditions of employment.

Weapons of Trade Union Used During Trade Disputes

  1. Negotiation: the representatives of the union meet the employers for collective bargaining
  2. Threat of strike
  3. Work to Rule: Deliberately slowing down work
  4. Picket Line: striking workers parading
  5. Strike: this involves staying away from work.

Employers Association

Employers Association means an association of employers whose principal purpose includes the regulation of relations between employers and workers or trade unions. It is formed to enable members to adopt a common policy in labour negotiation. In Nigeria we have Nigerian Employers’ Consultative Association (NECA).

Nigeria Employers' Consultative Association (NECA) is the umbrella organization of employers in the Organised Private Sector of Nigeria. It was formed in 1957 to provide the forum for the Government to consult with private sector employers on socio-economic and labour policy issues.

Weapons of Employers Association Used During Trade Disputes

  1. Negotiation
  2. Strike Breakers: this involves using some workers to operate in plant during workers strike
  3. Blacklist: a list of workers dismissed for union activities
  4. Closing down the factory until the dispute is resolved.
