4. ECONOMIC SYSTEMS

An economic system is an arrangement specifying how production and consumption are controlled in a society. Examples of different economic systems are capitalism, socialism and mixed economy.  

Market Economy (Capitalism)

A market economy is defined as a system where the production of goods and services are set according to the changing desires and abilities of the market players. It allows the market to operate freely in accordance with the law of supply and demand, set by individuals and corporations, as opposed to governments.

Features of Capitalist economy

1. There is private ownership and control of the production processes, and the government contribution is very small.

2. There are many producers and many buyers in the economy.

3. The main objective of the producers are to make profit.

4. There is high level of competition which leads to economic rivalry.

5. It has Aristocratic structure by placing strength in the hands of a small, privileged ruling class, the aristocrats.

6. The consumers have the freedom of choice in deciding on what to buy. 

7. There is freedom of Enterprise, anyone can start-up a private business.

8. It is a market economy because all the production is targeted towards the market, which determines the decision of the producers. 

9. It possess highly developed occupational specialization. 

10. It is believed that labour is a commodity, and that a commodity is a good or service. If the definition of a commodity as a good or service that can be bought and sold in the open market is anything to go by, then labour should fall within it.

Some countries that adopt a market economy include the USA, Canada, Hong Kong, Singapore, the UK, Denmark, etc.

Advantages of Capitalism

1. It gives each citizen freedom of enterprises.

2. It encourages and pays hardworking.

3. It fastens economic development.

4. It gives room for utilisation of talent and potentials.

5. There is increased efficiency in the production of goods and services due to business competition among business establishments. 

6. It helps a giving economy to be self-reliant and not to depend on foreign goods. 

7. It creates an environment conducive for carrying out inventions. 

8. Decreased state bureaucracy, as some public sector activities can be taken over by private entities

Disadvantages of Capitalism

1. Few people are in control of the economy and this gives room for manipulation.

2. There is inadequate distribution of income.

3. The big gap between the rich and the poor increases crime rate. 

4. There is rivalry in the economy. In Market economy there is inevitable periods of economic crisis due to the usual business cycle (as some business are rising others are failing).

5. Profit making is favored over social welfare

6. It creates a room for job insecurity and unemployment.

Socialist Economy (Controlled, Command or Centrally planned economy)

A centrally-planned or controlled or socialist economic system can be defined as a system where the means of production and distribution of goods and services are controlled by the government. The goods and services are produced with the main aim of satisfying the wants of the whole citizens. There is no private ownership of the means of production and distribution of goods and services.

The founder of modern socialism is Karl Marx (1818 – 1883). Currently, some countries are still practicing socialism as seen on some African countries like Angola, Ethiopia, Tanzania, Guinea, Burkina Faso and Mozambique.

The opposite of a market economy is a socialist economy, which is centrally controlled by the government.

The socialist economy is a type of system where the government plays the principal role in planning and regulating goods and services produced in the country. The state authority determines the types of goods and services to be produced and provided, as well as the quantity and prices that will be offered in the marketplace.

Characteristics Socialist economy

  1. The state (government) owns the majority of the means of production and distribution of goods and services.
  2. The goods and services are produced according to needs and distributed according to individual contribution and wants.
  3. Economic plans are centrally created by the government for the majority, if not all, sectors and regions.
  4. The private firms play little or no role in the production and distribution of goods and services.
  5. There is equitable distribution of income among citizens.
  6. There is an absence of economic rivalry.
  7. The government distributes the nation’s capital, labor, and natural resources in the means it deems most efficient.
  8. Production and prices are dictated by the government.
  9. Businesses in the finance, utilities, and automotive industries are owned and monopolized by state authorities.
  10. Government policies are created to carry out the centralized economic plan.
  11. Citizens work collectively towards the advancement of the country and government.

This type of economic system is a key feature in communist or socialist countries, such as modern-day North Korea.

Advantages of a Socialist Economy

If done properly and with adequate resources, a socialist economy offers the following benefits:

  1. Society favors social welfare and equity rather than profiteering
  2. Prevents monopolies by private businesses in identified crucial industries, such as health and energy
  3. Reduction of unemployment
  4. Ensures access to basic necessities

Disadvantages of Socialist Economy

On the other hand, even when done “properly”, a pure command economy has significant drawbacks.

  1. Shortage of goods and services which due to fixed prices and quantity of production. Natural equilibrium is more difficult to achieve when price and quantity are not floating.
  2. Inefficient pricing of goods in relation to supply and demand
  3. No response or attention to consumer preferences
  4. It makes people lazy.
  5. Limits liberties and personal rights to pursue financial stability, in favor of social equality
  6. Highly bureaucratic; all planning and execution carried out by the government
  7. Not being able to know and respond to consumer preferences or complaints
  8. It does not lead to inventions and innovation due to lack of competition.

Mixed Economic System

A mixed Economic System is one in which the private and public (government) ownership of the means of production and distribution exist side by side. In this kind of system, the government mainly participate in the regulation, management and supervision of all the economic activities in the country. Mixed economy is the result of the combination of the features capitalism and socialism. Most of the developing countries practice mixed economy. Nigeria practice mixed economic system. 

Features of Mixed Economic System

1. The Government (state) mainly participate in the regulation, management and supervision of all economic activities in the country.

2. Both the government and private sectors jointly make the decision on what and how much of the products and services are to be produced. 

3. There is economic freedom to carry out production processes.

4. The private sectors main aim of business is to make profit.

5. It encourages occupational specialisation.

Advantages of Mixed Economic System

1. It encourages entrepreneurship among citizens.

2. It combines the advantages of both Capitalism and socialism.

3. It creates room for innovation in the society.

4. It creates room for both government and private monopoly.

5. It promotes hard work and economic development.

6. Governments regulation prevents the exploitation of citizens by the private sectors.

7. It promotes job security.

Disadvantages of Mixed Economic System

1. Problems arising from the combination of socialism and capitalism are complicated and hard to solve. 

2. Capitalism is promoted more by both the government and private sectors. 

3. It causes both economic and political instability in the country.

4. It widens the gap between the rich and the poor.

5. There is no equitable distribution of fund.

6. It gives room for corrupt practices and immoral activities.
