13. POPULATION

Meaning of Population 

Population refers to the number of people living in a geographical area or country. 

The main way of determining the total number of people living in a geographical area is through population census. 

Population Census

This is a regular and organized program conducted by the government or its agencies, that involves house-to-house counting of the number of people (both male and female, the disabled and enabled, children and adult) who live in a given area at a particular time. 

Features of Good Population Census

  1. It is organized by the government and its agencies
  2. It must be specific to a defined area and time
  3. It must be done regularly with year intervals like 5years, 10years, 20years, etc.
  4. Only people that are seen should be counted

Two forms of Population Census

1. De-Facto Population Census: it is a form of census that involves counting only those that are physically present during census

2. De-jure Population Census: this is a form of census that involves counting people who are residents of an area irrespective of their physical presence during the census. This is not an ideal type of census.

The Purpose and Importance of Population Census

  1. It determines the size of Population of an Area.
  2. It enables the government to make Future Forecast.
  3. It shows the number of unemployed citizens in a nation.
  4. It is used to estimate government revenues that can be realized from tax payment.
  5. It is important in making economic policies
  6. It is used to determine the standard of living of the citizens
  7. It shows the rate of Migration.
  8. It determines the rate of migration

 

Determinants of population 

These are the main factors that determine population.

(a) Birth rate: this is the rate at which babies are born in a country. High birth rate increases the population and vice versa.

(b) Death rate: this is the rate at which people die in a country. High death rate decreases the population and vice versa.

(c) Migration: this is the movement of people into and out of a country at a specific period of time.

NB. Natural Growth means the difference between birth rate and death rate of people at a particular area and time.

Migration

Migration is the movement of people from one place or country to another at a specific period.

There are two types of Migration: 

Emigration (E)

Immigration. (I)

Emigration is the movement of people out of a place or country. Those people are called emigrants. Emigration has the effect of decreasing the population of the country from which people moved out from. 

Immigration is the movement of people into a place or country. The people are called immigrants. It increases the population of the country where they come in. In a particular country, if immigration is greater than emigration, the population of the country will increase and vice versa. 

The difference between immigration and emigration is Net migration.

NB: The natural growth rate of population is the difference between birth rate and death rate.

Natural growth rate = Birth rate - Death rate 

These three factors that affect the growth of the population can be summarized thus: 

Growth rate = Birth rate - Death rate + Net Migration

Population Size

Apart from regular head counting of all the people, the current population size of the people can be determined anytime from the previous population size using the formular below

Current Population size = The figure of the Previous year population + Death rate – Death rate + immigration – Emigration.

Large Population (Increasing Population)

Large Population also means increasing population in which the number of people dying is lower than the number of babies being born

Factors Responsible For Increase In Population

  1. Increase in birth rate
  2. Decrease in Death rate
  3. Immigration
  4. Early Marriage
  5. The culture and believe of the people
  6. Illiteracy
  7. Improvement in Medical Sector which can reduce death rate
  8. Religions like Christianity and Islam promote population growth by discouraging population control measures like abortion and the use of contraceptives

Advantages of Large Population

  1. Increase in the working population
  2. Encourages rapid urbanization
  3. Diversity of skills and talents
  4. Increase in defense capacity
  5. Encourages expansion of local market

Disadvantages of Large Population

  1. Results in overpopulation
  2. Demands for Large Government expenditure
  3. It leads to congestion in housing, traffic, schooling etc
  4. Increases taxation
  5. Poor standard of living
  6. Unemployment
  7. Increase in dependency ratio
  8. Inadequate social amenities

Static Population (Ageing or Declining Population)

This is a condition where the population of a nation is decreasing. It is also the population with increasing number of old people. It is also known as a stationary population.

Factors Responsible For Declining Population

  1. Increase in death rate
  2. Decrease in birth rate
  3. War
  4. Natural disaster
  5. Deadly Diseases
  6. Excessive Family Planning

Optimum Population 

This is a moderate population that is neither too large nor small with regards to the available resources and level of technology to secure maximum return per head.

Optimum population is not static but dynamic and varies from country to country. It is determined by the available resources and factors of production available in a country at a given time. It is the best type of population that provides the citizens with the highest standard of living and good economic management.

Malthusian Population Theory

Rev. Thomas R. Malthus in the 18th Century talked about the prevailing Social and economic conditions in Britain. He wrote essay to criticize his Father and two philosophers (William Godwin and Jean Condecent) about science, food production and population growth. In his first essay titled “Essay on Population 1798” he talked about population.

These are the Summary of Malthusian Theory

1. that nothing could be done to wipe out poverty in a given society

2. Poverty cannot be related to a good or bad government but as a result of human tendency.

3. he disregarded equality of citizens as seen in equalitarian society claiming that in a giving society everybody cannot be equal.

4. he stated that human population can grow above food production

5. if human population are not checked in their natural form, human population will grow at a geometrical progression while food production will grow at an arithmetic progression

6. in his view population at the rate of 1, 2, 4, 16, 32, 64…. (geometric rate), while food production will grow at the arithmetic rate of 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7…. (arithmetic rate).

7. increase in food will lead to increase in population while lack of food will decrease population

8. Human nature and impulses undergo changes over time.

Malthus Concept of Population Control

 He identified two ways to check rapid growing population. The checks are divided into two, which are the positive checks and preventive checks.

The positive checks consist of factors that brings about death of people living in the area like wars, pestilence, child exposure, infanticide, vicious custom etc. 

The preventive checks include voluntary abstinence and postponement of marriage. 

In summary he stated that the two checks mentioned above can only control population growth but cannot stop it because of the issue of poverty, always having more person to feed will remain the law of nature.

The Appraisal of Malthusian Theory of Population

Malthusian essay was controversial and attracted different views.

The Politicians accepted his concept that poverty is a natural phenomenon which can never be attributed to good or bad government. The politicians took it as their excuse for inability to perform their duties effectively.

Karl Marx group opposed and rejected his concept and called Malthus a prophet of doom, they saw him as a myopic and conservative capitalist.

Malthus Proved Wrong

The discovery of new world like U.S.A., New Zealand, Canada etc. which provided new settlement for the population which Malthus suggested it will grow geometrically if not checked (by positive or preventive checks). Instead of using those checks they applied science and made it possible for the production of food in abundance.

Secondly, the industrial revolution in Europe which reached its Peak by the second half of the 19th Century. This boosted food production as a result of thousands of industries set up to the extent that the food produced was enough to be exported to other countries. 

Moreover, improvement in medical sector discouraged the use of positive checks of population as suggested by Malthus. Good medical treatment prolonged human lives.

Welfare shames were also set up to support the poor masses.

The Agrarian Revolution also provided food for all parts of Europe. The abundance of food showed that Malthusian theory was Myopic. 

Areas that Supported Malthus Theory

African Countries which are basically Third World Country of the 20th Century are said to have aligned with Malthus ideas. This is due to the existence of Taboos which makes the deliberate prevention of pregnancy or abortion an evil act, and this can lead to ostracisation of any one who involve himself in those acts. 

This attitude of Africans has contributed to the high population of the people and poor economic support to carry the growing population.

Some countries like U.S.A and U.K are applying the Malthus ideas in the aspect of legalization of abortion in order to check the rapid growth of population. 

In summary, a journal of United Nation Fund for population activities called the “Populi” stated that the world population stability is not only desirable but absolutely imperative. There is need for economic and social equilibrium. 

 

Demographic Transition Theory

This is the most current theory of population. The theory was meant to provide the explanation for the observed decline in the population of the advanced industrialized European countries. 

Demographic transition means the changes a country’s population structure undergoes as a result of economic development. This theory also proves that a population is not a static phenomenon but a dynamic phenomenon as was earlier noted by Malthus.

According to this theory, three stages of demographic transition occurs.

First Stage (Pre-industrialization Stage):  this is a stage that is characterised by high Birth rate (Fertility rate) and high Death rate (Mortality rate). During this stage population is either static/stationary or increasing or decreasing at a low rate.

Second Stage (Transitional Stage):  

This stage is the transitional stage which is has three subdivisions

  1. Early Transitional stage: this stage is where Death rate start dropping while the birth rate remains high or keep growing. This where developing countries like Nigeria falls in.
  2. Middle transitional stage: at this stage, the birth rate starts to decrease while death rate keeps falling.
  3.  Late Transitional Stage: at this stage the death rate has reached low level while the birth rate continues to decrease

Third Stage (Post Transitional Stage): during this stage both the birth rate and death rate have leveled down to low level. Therefore, the population is said to be in stationary or static as seen in developed nations.

Working Population

Working population may refer to the population employed in a specified occupation or to the total population of a country who are employed and looking for employment. 

Working population also known as labour force, it is the total number of people of working age in a country who are able and willing by law to work.

Dependency Ratio in Nigeria

The dependency ratio is the number of dependents in a population divided by the number of working-age people. Dependency ratios reveal the population breakdown of a country and how well its dependents can be taken care of.

Dependency ratio = Dependent: Independent

The dependency ratio relates the number of children (0-14 years old) and older persons (65 years or over) to the working-age population (15-64 years old).

Population Density

This is the total number of inhabitants per unit area of land in a giving period of time. When a small area of land is being crowded with people as seen in Lagos state, it is said to be densely populated, while an area with few people in a large area of land is said to be sparsely populated. 

Population density =  (persons per sq km)

Reasons for High Population Density

  1. Friendly Climatic Factors
  2. Good Fertility and Topography of soil
  3. Availability of industries Job Opportunities
  4. Presence of Railway Line, Seaports and Airports
  5. Presence of commercial Centers
  6. Availability of Mineral Resources
  7. Presence of Social Amenities
