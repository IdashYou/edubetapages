19. THE CONCEPT OF UTILITY 

Utility is the satisfaction that a consumer derives from consuming a commodity at any particular time. Utility differs from usefulness because a commodity may be useful to human being but does not satisfy an individual’s want. For example, a healthy person derives no satisfaction from the use of drug. Again, utility differs from one individual to the other and is dependent on time of consumption.

It was introduced by Daniel Bernoulli. The Utility of a product determines its Demand by the customers 

Types of Utility 

1. Place Utility: Place utility is the satisfaction derived from the consumption of a commodity due to its geographical location. For instance, when a commodity is moved from an area where it is not needed to a place where there is high demand for it, it is said to possess place utility. 

2. Time Utility: This refers to the satisfaction derived from a commodity during the time of need. For example, a hungry man from his place of work will derive more satisfaction from his food than another man that is not hungry. So, the time of consumption determines the amounts of satisfaction a commodity will give. 

3. Form Utility: This is the satisfaction derived from a commodity when it has been turned from its original state to another form through manufacturing. This increases its satisfaction. Example, turning of limestone into cement will give more satisfaction to the consumer. 

Concept of Total, Average and Marginal Utility 

Total Utility 

Total Utility is the total amount of satisfaction derived from the consumption of all the units of a commodity at a particular time. In other words, it is the sum of all the satisfaction from the different units of the commodity at a given period. It is obtained by multiplying average utility with the quantity of the commodity consumed. 

Thus, TU = AU X Q or TU = EMU. 

Total utility increases as the units of the commodity consumed increases but at a decreasing rate. Total utility increases at a decreasing rate because as more units of a commodity is consumed, the satisfaction derived from it decreases. 

Average Utility

This is the amount of satisfaction derived by a consumer per unit of the commodity consumed. That means the satisfaction for one unit of the commodity. It is derived by dividing the total utility with the number of units consumed or the quantity. Average utility decreases as more units are consumed. 

Marginal Utility

Marginal utility is the additional satisfaction derived by consuming an extra Unit of commodity. It is obtained by dividing the change in total utility with the change in quantity consumed. Marginal utility falls as the consumer consumes more units of a commodity. When the total utility reaches its maximum, marginal utility is at zero. This is the point where the consumer will no longer consume any unit of the commodity. 

MU = ΔTU/ΔQty

Or 

MU = ΔTU/ ATU

Where MU = Marginal utility 

ATU = Change in total utility 

AQ = Change in quantity

The sum of all marginal utilities is equal to total utility for the product (EMU = TU). Economists are much interested in marginal utility because it determines whether “consumer will increase or decrease his consumption of a commodity.

The Law of Diminishing Marginal Utility 

The law of diminishing marginal utility states that the satisfaction derived from consuming additional successive units of a commodity Will diminish as the total consumption of the commodity increases. Total utility increases as more of a commodity is consumed but increases at a diminishing rate. Diminishing marginal utility starts after a point called ‘origin’. Origin is the maximum quantity of a commodity that must be consumed before the consumer can derive any satisfaction. 

Utility Maximization 

A consumer’s utility is maximized when the marginal utility per amount spent on a product is equal to the marginal utility per amount spent on any other product.
