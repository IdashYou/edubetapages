26. INTERNATIONAL ECONOMIC ORGANIZATION

These are organizations formed in order to encourage trade, economic cooperation and development among sovereign nation’s of the world.

Examples of international economic organizations are;

IMF – International Monetary Fund.

IBRD – InternationalBank for Reconstruction and Development.

ADB – African Development Bank

EEC – European Economic Community

OPEC – Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries.

UNCTAD – United Nations Conference on Trade and Development.

ECA – Economic Commission for Africa.

WACH – West African Clearing House

ECOWAS – Economic Community of West African States

IMF

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) is an organization of 190 countries, working to foster global monetary cooperation, secure financial stability, facilitate international trade, promote high employment and sustainable economic growth, and reduce poverty around the world.

It was established in 1944 towards the end of the Second World War, and came into operation in 1947 with it’s headquarters in Washington D. C., United States.

Aims and Functions of IMF

1. To end exchange control.

2. To serve as Clearing house for it’s member Nations. 

3. To help developed Nations with excess products sell their products.

4. To promote foreign exchange by ensuring free convertibility of the currencies of member Nations.

5. To give advice and financial aid to member Nations experiencing balance of payment difficulties. 

6. To promote international monetary cooperation and the expansion of international trade.

7. To standardize currencies by ensuring currency stability and preventing competition by depression of the rate of exchange. 

IBRD

The International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD) is a global development cooperative owned by 189 member countries. As the largest development bank in the world, it supports the World Bank Group’s mission by providing loans, guarantees, risk management products, and advisory services to middle-income and creditworthy low-income countries, as well as by coordinating responses to regional and global challenges. 

Created in 1944 to help Europe rebuild after World War II, IBRD joins with IDA, our fund for the poorest countries, to form the World Bank.  They work closely with all institutions of the World Bank Group and the public and private sectors in developing countries to reduce poverty and build shared prosperity.

Aims and Objectives of IBRD

1. To assist in the Reconstruction and Development of territories of member Nations that were devastated by the Second World War.

2. To provide technical assistance to member Nations 

3. Promotion of the standard of living of developing countries by provision of financial assistance.

4. To promote international trade participation by private sectors

5. To maintain equilibrium of balance of payments among Nations.

ADB

African Development Bank (ADB) is a financial institution comprising 54 African and 26 non-African countries that promote economic and social progress in Africa through loans, equity investments, and technical assistance. Structurally, the ADB Group includes the African Development Bank, the African Development Fund, and the Nigeria Trust Fund. Established in 1964 and headquartered in Tunisia. 

It was established in 1964 with the help of O.A.U and E.C.A. 

 

Aims and Objectives of ADB

1. To put and poverty in all its forms everywhere, and to end hunger, by achieving food security and improved nutrition and promote sustainable agriculture.

2. To ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages.

3. To Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.

4. To build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization and foster innovation

5. To promote Economic integration of African Countries.

6. To grant loans to member states to promote economic growth.

EEC

The European Economic Community (EEC), informally known as European Economic Union, was a regional organization that aimed to bring about economic integration among its member states. It was created by the Treaty of Rome of 1957. In 2009, the EC formally ceased to exist and its institutions were directly absorbed by the EU. 

The main aim of the EEC, as stated in its preamble, was to "preserve peace and liberty and to lay the foundations of an ever-closer union among the peoples of Europe". Calling for balanced economic growth, this was to be accomplished through: 

1. The establishment of a customs union with a common external tariff

2. Common policies for agriculture, transport and trade, including standardization (for example, the CE marking designates standards compliance)

3. Enlargement of the EEC to the rest of Europe

4. To promote close union among the European peoples.

5. To strengthen their safeguards of peace and liberty.

OPEC

The Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) is a permanent, intergovernmental Organization, created at the historic Baghdad Conference, September 10–14, 1960.  It aims to coordinate the petroleum policies of its member countries and determine the best means of securing stable oil supplies.

OPEC’s first Summit of Heads of State and Government in Algiers in 1975 led to the establishment of the OPEC Fund for International Development in 1976. 

Aims and Objectives of OPEC

1. Stabilization of the prize of oil in the World market.

2. Coordination of oil policies with respect to it’s standards.

3. To ensure steady supply of oil to consuming Nations.

4. To negotiate for the participation of member Nations in the oil exploitations and exploration.

5. To fix and allocate oil production quotas to member Nations.

6. To protect the interest of it’s members in the oil market.

Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS)

ECOWAS is a sub-regional organization formed by West African Countries with the main aim of promoting cooperation and development of all economic field and of contributing to the progress and development of African continent.

ECOWAS was established on 28th May, 1975, when 15 head of states in West Africa met in Lagos and signed the Treaty and thereby Formerly establishing ECOWAS. 

The headquarters is in Abuja but was formerly in Lagos.

The seat of operations of the fund for co-operation, compensation and development in in Line, Togo. 

ECOWAS now has 16 states after the inclusion of Cape Verde.

Organs of ECOWAS

The following organs (Institution) exists in ECOWAS 

1. The Authority of Head of State: This is composed of State leaders from the 16 member states. They serve as the supreme authority.

2. The Council of Ministers: This organ is composed of two ministers (representatives) from each member state. They monitor the activities of the organization and make recommendations to the supreme authority of the organization.

3. The Executive Secretariat: this organ exists in Abuja, where it performs administrative functions to the community.

4. The Tribunal of the Community: The main function of this organ is to interpret the treaty and ensure that law and justice are maintained. 

5. Technical and Specialized Commission: This is composed of several subunits which includes

1. The Defense Commission

2. The Social and Cultural Affairs Commission.

3. The Transport, Communication and Energy Commission.

4. The Trade, Customs, Immigration, Monetary and Payments Commission.

5. The industry, Agriculture and Natural Resources Commission.

The Aims and Objectives of ECOWAS

1. To promote cooperation and development among member Nation.

2. Elimination of Custom Duties and abolition of Trade Restrictions

4. Elimination of Trade Restriction among the member states

5. To harmonize monetary policy of member nations for more effective relations.

6. To harmonize agricultural policies.

7. Establishment of infrastructures that will promote transport, communication and trade among mber nation.

1. The Economic Commission for Africa was set up by the_________

A. UNO

B. ECOWAS

C. OAU

D. IMF

Option A. 2018

Explanation

The economic commission for Africa (ECA) also known as the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa.

ECA was established by the Economic and Social Council of the United Nations (UN) in 1958 as one of the UN's five regional commissions. ECA's mandate is to promote the economic and social development of its member States, foster intra-regional integration, and promote international cooperation for Africa's development.

It has its headquarters in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. It is an organ of the United Nations Organization (UNO). Its major aim is to promote the social and economic development of Africa.

Explanation

NNPC stands for Nigerian National Petroleum Corporation. 

The Nigerian National Petroleum Corporation is the oil corporation through which the federal government of Nigeria regulates and participates in the country's petroleum industry.
