17. MONEY

Money is an economic unit that functions as a generally recognized medium of exchange for transactional purposes (making payments, settlement of depts it other business obligations) in a particular economy. Money provides the service of reducing transaction cost, namely the double coincidence of wants.

Trade by Barter

Trade by barter means a system of exchange where people directly exchange goods or services for other goods or services during a transaction, without using a medium of exchange, such as money.

Origin of Money

Money originated from the receipts which a Goldsmith issued people in exchange for gold and other valuable which people kept with him. These receipts given in exchange for goods were letter modernized into money.

Disadvantages of Trade by Barter

1. The difficulty of double coincidence of wants: it was difficult to find people who have what you want and needs what you have in exchange.

2. Wastage of time and energy.

3. Problem of establishing the value of goods and services

4. It Discourages the selling of goods in large quantities.

5. It Discourages large scale production.

6. It Discourages deferred payment.

7. Miscellaneous problem which includes lack of divisibility, durability, homogeneity etc.

8. It Discourages lending and borrowing.

9. Discourages instalment payment.

10. It does not encourage division of labour.

Characteristics of Money (Qualities of Money).

1. General Acceptability: the people on the country should accept the money as a medium of exchange.

2. Absolute Homogeneity: all the money used in a particular country should be uniform and the same everywhere. 

3. Easily Recognizable: this prevents the spread of counterfeit.

4. Divisibility: it should be easily dividable to enhance and fasten exchange. 

5. Durability: of Money should be made with a durable material.

6. No intrinsic value: the material make-up of money should be very cheap when compared with the value it represents. 

7. Money should have controllable supply: when the supply of money is easily controllable the government can check and monitor it's circulation.

8. Stability: the value of money should be relatively stable so as to encourage borrowing and lending.

Types of Money 

1. Coins: this a type of money made with metals, that is issued and stamped by the government. Example is the use of coins in Nigeria.

2. Paper Money: this is a type of money made with paper notes. It originated from the receipts issued by the Goldsmiths issued to people in exchange for goods.

3. Bank Money: these are money people save in the bank in the form of deposits for security purposes.

4. Foreign Money: these are money of other countries used in foreign exchange e.g. Dollars, Pounds Sterling, Deutschmark, etc.

5. Token Money: Token money is a form of money that represents a greater value than its intrinsic value. The intrinsic worth is less than it's nominal or face value. An example of this is seen in Britain's silver coins. At a point there was a rise in the value of silver which endangered their silver coins, because people could start melting them to extract silver. As a result of this Britain replaced the silver coin with curpo-nickel coins

6. Commodity Money: some commodities are used as medium of exchange in different parts of the world. Examples of commodity Money includes; Gold, Silver, Diamond, Shark teeth, cowries etc.

7. Gold Backed Money: This is the money that can be easily converted or changed into Gold by the central authority. It also originated from the Goldsmith receipts for gold's and other goods.

8. Legal Tender: A legal Tender refers to money which is backed with the force of law in the country making it a generally accepted medium of exchange, example is Naira and Kobo.

9. Fiduciary Note Issue: this is a form of money that is neither backed by Gold nor any Foreign currency.

10. Representative Money: This are documents or instruments that are used in place of legal Tender but not fully and freely acceptable.

Representative Money and Means of Payment

These are articles that serve as money and removes the burden of carrying physical cash around during business transaction. It is important to know that they are not Legal Tender, they are not backed up by the law and therefore not generally accepted.

1. Cheques: A cheque is a written order on a paper to a bank to pay a specified sum of money to a named person or bearer at a certain date.

2. Postal Order: it is issued by the post office and serves as a means of transferring money from one part of the country to another. A commission known as poundage is charged by the post office, the cost depends on the amount involved.

3. Money Order: this is a representative of money offered by postal office and used in sending large amount of money to a recipient whose name and address are indicated. It must be paid into a bank account.

4. Postal Stamp: it involves using stamps which is a representative of money for the settlement of Dept. It is applyed in postal office also.

5. Bank Order (Standing order): it is an order from the owner of the account to his bank, requesting the bank to pay a given amount of money to the person indicated in the account.

6. Bank Draft: this is issued by commercial banks and are specially used in settling dept.

Other representative of money includes

Certified chaque, Mall and telegraphic transfers, primary note and Bill of exchange.
