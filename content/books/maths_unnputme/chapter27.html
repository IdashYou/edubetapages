Probability

Probability is the measure of likelihood of occurrence of an event.

The probability of an event always lies between 0 and 1. You cannot get negative probability or greater than 1.

The probability of an event that cannot happen is zero (0), while the probability of an event that must happen is one (1).

The probability of success plus the probability of a failure is always equal to one (1). 

P (success) + P (failure) = 1

P (success) = 1 – P (failure) 

P (failure) = 1 - P (success)

The Either/ or rule: if two or more events A and B are mutually exclusive then the probability of A or B happening is given by adding the separate probability

P (A or B) = P(A)  P(B)

The And rule: if two or more events A, B, are independent, then the probability of both occurring together is given by multiplying their separate probability

P (A and B) = P(A)  P(B)

 

Mutually exclusive means In statistics and probability theory, two events are mutually exclusive if they cannot occur at the same time. 

 For example, you can't run backwards and forwards at the same time, or you can’t get and head and tail in a single toss of a coin.

An independent event is an event that has no connection to another event's chances of happening (or not happening). In other words, the event has no effect on the probability of another event occurring.

Example,

Your team winning a match and scoring a goal, because winning a match doesn’t affect them scoring and scoring a goal doesn’t guarantee them a win and it can happens in all possible way you think of if provided there must be a winner in the match.

 Landing on heads after tossing a coin AND rolling a 5 on a single 6-sided die.

 

The difference between mutually exclusive and independent events is: a mutually exclusive event can simply be defined as a situation when two events cannot occur at same time whereas independent event occurs when one event remains unaffected by the occurrence of the other event.
