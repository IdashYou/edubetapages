 Plane Mensuration

A plane shape is any shape that is drawn on a paper or wall. It is a 2D figure without depth. Circle, rectangle, triangle etc are all plane 2D shapes. A plane shape has an outline and therefore, encloses an area. This is called the area of the plane shape. 

The area of a plane shape is defined as the product of the length, l and the side length (breadth), b ie A = l × b. Notice the use of the word "defined". There is nothing inherent about the area of a plane shape that it should go by l × b. We only chose this definition for the area and work with it.

Solid Mensuration

Unlike plane shapes which have no depth, solid shapes are 3D shapes with a length, side and depth. They are real life shapes that you can touch and feel eg a cup, baseball, bucket etc are all solid 3D shapes. Solid shapes have volume and NOT area. While plane shapes enclose an area/surface, solid shapes rather take up actual space/volume. 

The volume of a uniform solid shape is defined as V = A × h where A represents the uniform cross-section/base of the solid shape and h is the depth. We emphasize again that this is just a convention that was adopted. There is no inherent reason why the volume of a solid shape should be A × h. We only work with this as a standard for measuring volume, but that's all.

Q1: A trapezium has two parallel sides of length 6cm and 8cm. If the area of the trapezium is 42cm2, find the distance between the parallel sides.

Solution

A = 42cm2, a = 6cm, b = 8cm, h = ?

A = 1/2 (a + b)h  => h = 2A/(a + b)

h = (2 × 42)/ (6 + 8) = 84/14

:. h = 6cm

 

Q2: A pyramid 12cm high stands on a rectangular base of length 7cm and width 5cm. Calculate the volume of the pyramid.

Solution

Given l=7cm, w=5cm and h=12cm.

The base of the pyramid a rectangle. Therefore, the base area, A of the pyramid is given by 

A = l × w

=> A=7×5=35cm2

V=1/3 Ah = 1/3 × 35 × 12 = 35 × 4

:. V = 140cm3

 

Q3: What is the volume of a cone with base diameter of 6cm and height 5cm? [Leave your answer in terms of π]

Q4: How many 30cm square tiles would be enough to cover a 7.2m by 4.2m rectangular floor?

Solution

The area of a square tile is 30 × 30 = 900cm2

The floor measures 7.2m (7.2 × 100 = 720cm) by 4.2m (4.2 × 100 = 420cm)

:. The floor spans 720 × 420 = 302,400cm2

The question could then be rephrased thus: "how many 900cm2

tiles would be required to cover a 302,400cm2 rectangular floor?"

If the number be x,

900x=302,400

:. x = 336 square tiles

Notice how we converted the dimensions of the floor to cm. The units have to be unity. If we wanted, we could have converted cm to m and work in m. Either way, we will still arrive at the same answer.

Q6: How many 3cm sugar cubes will fit perfectly in a carton measuring 5cm×3cm×1cm?

Solution

If the number of sugar cubes be x,

27x = 15

:. x = 15/27 = 5/9 sugar cubes

Well, it means that not even a single cube can contain inside the carton. The reason is quite obvious from the measurements given above. Instead, take a cube and divide it into 9 equal parts. Only 5 parts out of the 9 will fit inside the carton.

Example

Two triangles have the same area if 

A. The three angles of one are equal to the three angles of the other, B. if the three sides of one are equal to the three sides of the other, C. they lie between the same parallels, D. they are similar

B. (2015/2015, no 10)

Solution

Two triangles have the same area, if the three sides of one are equal to the three sides of the other. The three sides being equal means that they also have equal three angles. Note that two triangles can have equal angles but different length in sides and therefore have different area.

Two triangles are said to be congruent when they are equal in all respect (their area, 3 sides and 3 internal angles).
