Raider Of The Treasure Trove By Lade Wosornu

But what can be worthy of your life?

What dearer than the gems or your dreams;

The reason you are here? Always strive

To fly flags of joy, and, sail upstreams

Powered by the breeze of love, your course

Chattered in the ink of compassion.

And, fling roses wherever you pause

Heaven-on-earth your destination.

Of things which would blot out that brief

Or, breach your sails with arrows unseen

No! Rob you of life, Rage is chief.

Rage drags rags after you. Of charity,

Laughter, sweetness and joy, Rage is thief

Enemy of equanimity,

Rage spreads toxic fumes on every scene.

In essence, Rage spells calamity.

Its cause is your perception of storms

Breaking around, not upon, your head.

There are either snakes, deviants nor norms:

As you think, so you feel. Watch your mind.

Rage sets sail. Can ruin lag far behind?

I’ll fling roses wherever I berth.

My destination is heaven-on-earth.

SUMMARY OF THE POEM

The poem is organized into 5 stanzas of 23 lines.

Lade’s Raider of the Treasure Trove is a didactic poem about man’s life. From its title, which depicts man’s pursuit of life treasures or the value of life, to its opening line, which asks a rhetorical question about the value of life: “But what can be worthy of your life?”

The word raider is used by the poet to represent humans. Every human being comes into the world with the sole purpose of discovering their destiny. The treasure trove is this destiny. The poet metaphorically presents two concepts in the poem that will enable every human being to achieve or lose this “Treasure Trove.” They are affectionate and rage. He stated that “Rage,” as personified in line 11, is a human weakness that has the snarky power to limit humans’ journeys in life. 

The poet metaphorically creates an imagery of the sea and a sailor on a voyage in stanza four of “The Raider of the Treasure Trove.” He compares life to a destination or path, with pain and gain.

Finally, the poet issued a warning against the mind that harbours Rage, stating that only a heart filled with love, compassion, and joy can triumph over rage.
