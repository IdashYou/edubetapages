6. REVIEW OF LITERARY APPRECIATION QUESTIONS

1. ‘Basha: You dumb skull of a bone head . . . you will face court martial for this. You look everywhere? You search inside toilet bowl?

 Wole Soyinka: King Baabu

The person being addressed above is a 

A. soldier 

B. student 

C. domestic servant 

D. lawyer

(JAMB 2009/2010, No 41)

Option A, soldier, is the correct option. 

The phrase that sold out the answer to this question is court martial. Court martial is a military term used to designate a court where cases of military law are heard. When a soldier misbehaves, he faces court martial. 

 

2. From the tone of the speech above, the speaker is obviously 

A. enraged 

B. lackadaisical 

C. elated 

D. happy. 

(JAMB 2009/2010, No 42)

Option A bears the correct answer, enraged. 

The speaker is obviously enraged, angered, made furious by the actions of this soldier to whom he speaks. Perhaps, the speaker is a superior officer to this soldier. 

 

3. 'That year the harvest was sad, like a funeral, and many farmers wept as they dug up the miserable yams. One man tied his cloth to a tree branch and hanged himself'. 

Chinua Achebe: Things Fall Apart

The mood conveyed in the excerpt above is one of 

A. sadness 

B. frustration 

C. sympathy 

D. dilemma.

(JAMB 2009/2010, No 43)

Option A, sadness, is the correct answer. 

The mood pictured in the above excerpt is that of sadness. The farmers are sad that they put out a poor yield of their farm work.

 

4. But the towering earth was tired 

sitting in one position. She moved, 

suddenly, and the houses crumbled, 

the mountains heaved horribly, and 

the work of a million years was lost. 

The subject matter of the extract above is 

A. storm 

B. sea waves 

C. house movement 

D. earthquake. 

(JAMB 2009/2010, No 45)

Option D, earthquake, is the correct answer. 

But the towering earth was tired. It was the earth that moved and when it did, houses collapsed and mountains heaved and the work of a million years was lost. This is exactly the way earthquake happens. 

 

5. Don't panic. Be calm, If you are somehow upset ...try to regain your exposure.

The speaker in the excerpt above is 

A. hopeless  

B. uncertain 

C. afraid 

D. confident. 

(JAMB 2009/2010, No 47)

Option D bears the correct answer. 

The speaker is obviously advising someone to not panic. He has to be confident; he has to believe in himself first before advising someone else to do so.

 

6. Theseus: Now, fairHippolyta, our nuptial hour. 

Draws on space four happy days bring in. 

Another moon. But 0, me thinks how slow 

This old moon wanes, she lingers my desires, 

Like to a step-dame or a dowager, 

Long withering out a young man's revenue.

William Shakespear. A midsummer Night's Dream

The literary devices used in the excerpt above are 

A. personification and smile 

B. irony and suspense 

C. alliteration and synecdoche 

D. rhyme and refrain.  

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 41)

Option A, personification and simile, is the correct answer. 

An object is said to be personified if it borrows the attributes of human beings that inanimate objects can never possess. The moon is personified in the excerpt above. "This old moon wanes, she lingers my desires." On the other hand, simile is indirect comparison of two things that are dissimilar. What the moon does in the poem is likened to a dame, a dowager. "Like to a step-dame or a dowager". 

 

7. 'You are the silent code of pleasure locked in wordless wonder. 

You are the hive of treasure; no dragon can plunder'

 Gbemisola Adeoti: Dream Code.

The excerpt above achieves its rhetorical effect through the use of 

A. repetition and meiosis 

B. metaphor and rhyme 

C. caesura and hyperbole 

D. alliteration and irony 

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 42)

Option B bears the correct answer, metaphor and rhyme. 

"Silent code of pleasure locked in wordless wonder" which stands for dream is a metaphor. "The hive of treasure" is also metaphorical. Hive is borrowed from bees and honey to display how protected the person spoken to is. Wherever there's something valuable, people (both who deserve it and those who don't) go after it to tap it or harness it. The person spoken to is a treasure hidden in a place where no gold digger can reach it. 

On the other hand, wonder /wʌndə/ and plunder /plʌndə/ rhyme together. 

 

8. It was not yet closing time, but already most staff were trooping out of their offices. The lift was working now and he squeezed himself into it, breathing with difficulty the body odour emitted by one of the passengers. He sighed with relief when they got to the ground floor and tumbled out of the lift.'

Ken Saro-Wiwa: A Forest of Flowers

In the excerpt above, the subject's experience in the lift is 

A. timely. 

B. comfortable. 

C. unpleasant 

D. amusing  

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 43)

Option C is the correct answer. 

The subject is obviously faces discomfort. 

First of all, he squeezed himself into the lift because it is overcrowded. Secondly, he is breathing with difficulty the body odour emitted by one of the passengers. And finally, he sighed with relief when they got to the ground floor and tumbled out of the lift.

The emboldened words in the illustration above show that the experience of the subject in the lift is highly unpleasant and uncomfortable. 

 

9. 'Do not thank me, instead, let me ask you one question, now you have all come here sprawling, vomiting, rubbing tears on one another begging me to do my duty and help you. But what about you yourselves? What have you done to help yourselves? Answer. Or is the land at peace? Are not people ailing and dying?' 

OIa Rotimi: The Gods Are Not To Blame

In the excerpt above, the land is not at peace because of 

A. chieftaincy tussle 

B. famine and war 

C. political unrest 

D. sickness and death  

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 44)

The correct option is option D, sickness and death. 

Odewale suffers the fate of killing a king whom he does not know is his father and marrying the queen whom he does not know is his mother and the wife of the king he killed. He becomes king and the land suffers sickness and death. 

 

 10. ‘In those days. When civilization kicked us in the 

face, when holy water slapped brows. The 

vultures built in the shadow of their talons.'

David Diop: The Vulture

The dominant literary device used in the lines above is 

A. pun 

B. metaphor 

C. personification 

D. simile 

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 45)

Option C, personification, is the correct answer. 

Students should note the use of dominant in the question asking. This means that more than one literary device occurred in the excerpt, but which one occurred most frequently? Metaphor and personification manifested but personification manifested more. 

Remember that personification is the portrayal of animals or inanimate objects as though they are human beings by lending them such human attributes. From the excerpt, When civilization kicked us in the face/ when holy water slapped brows, civilization and holy water are personified. The only metaphor there is "vultures built in the shadow of their talons". 

 

11. I am not afraid of anything; he told them. I have done almost everything in this world. I have you can think of an been committed all c y jailed for most of them. I have been in prison more hours than I have been out of it within the last five years. 

In recounting his criminal life, the speaker's tone is 

A. regretful 

B. boastful 

C. subdued 

D. repentant  

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 46)

Option B. boastful 

The speaker's tone is boastful. His first two statements affirm this. "I am not afraid of anything; he told them. I have done almost everything in this world". He speech shows that he recounts his life achievements. Going to prison no longer scares him. 

 

12. 'I have said too much unto a heart of stone, And 

laid my honour too unchary on it', There's 

something in me that reproves my fault, But such 

a headstrong potent fault it is That it but mocks 

reproof.' 

William Shakespeare: Twelfth Night

A heart of stone in the lines above is an example of 

A. metonymy 

B. litotes 

C. assonance 

D. metaphor  

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 47)

Option D is the correct option. 

Heart of stone is metaphorical. It stands for strong heart. The attribute of stone which is being thick or hard or rocky is borrowed and lent to heart which should be soft and delicate. 

 

13. 'Blood was prove no solace to the king. The rejection he had suffered at Idama's hands pushed his spirit into a comfortless hole in which, alone with himself, he searched in vain for ways to run from his inner emptiness.' Ayi Kwei Armah: 

Two Thousand Seasons

The narrator’s attitude to the king is one of 

A. envy 

B. sympathy 

C. suspicion 

D. contempt  

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 48)

The correct option is option D, contempt. The speaker's speech lacks regard for the king. He speaks as if it was not a king he speaks of. 

Contempt is the state or act of contemning; the feeling or attitude of regarding someone or something as inferior, base, or worthless; scorn, disdain. 

 

14. 'Homage to Peregede the triumphant mother of morning radiant in Chameleon's velvet. Let today's dawn bring on its rails trains of good tidings.'

Gbemisola Adeoti: Salutation to the gods 

The excerpt above is an example of 

A. invocation 

B. limerick 

C. ode 

D. elegy  

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 49)

Option A, invocation, is the correct answer. 

Invocation is the act or form of calling for the assistance or presence of some superior being, especially prayer offered to a divine being. The speaker in the above excerpt does not just praise the goddess, Peregrede, the triumphant mother of morning who is radiant in Chameleon's velvet but also prays that the day's dawn brings on its rails trains of good tidings.

 

15. The wood decay, the woods decay and fall, The vapour weep their burthen to the ground, Man comes and fills the field and lies beneath, And after many a summer dies the swan.

The subject matter of the lines above is 

A. death

B. rainfall

C. famine

D. storm  

(JAMB 2011/2012, No 50)

Option A, death, is the correct answer. 

Wood falling and decaying, man coming and filling the field and lying beneath; these are all motifs of death. 

 

16. ‘We all make decisions. Sometimes it is wrong, sometimes it is right.' The speaker in the lines above is 

A. Afraid

B. Excited

C. pessimistic 

D. Reassuring (JAMB 2015/2016, No 21)

Option D is the answer. 

The speaker is reassuring. He encourages his listener that the world is not always perfect. He builds the courage and confidence of his listener in the face of flaws. It's not a flawless world. One learns from his mistakes and moves on. 

 

17. 'Her neck is rope-like thin, long and 

skinny and her face sickly pale.' Okot 

p' Bitek: Song of Lawino. The style used 

in the lines is 

A. Narrative 

B. Argumentative 

C. dramatic 

D. Descriptive 

(JAMB 2015/2016, No 22)

The correct option is D. 

The lines are descriptive as every detail is reflected. This was achieved by the use of such adjectives and adverb-modified adjectives as rope-like thin, long and skinny, sickly pale. The description, however, reveals that the person being described is not a fleshy one perhaps not healthy or worn out, starving. 

 

18. 'Once upon a time son, they used to 

laugh with their eyes; but now they 

only laugh with their teeth, while 

their ice-block-cold eyes search 

behind my shadow' G. Okara: Once 

upon a time, 

The lines above are expressive of 

A. Friendliness 

B. Insincerity 

C. jealousy 

D. Sympathy 

(JAMB 2015/2016, No 23)

The correct option is B. 

Any laughter that is done with the eyes or the teeth alone is not true laughter; it does not get to the heart. That laughter is only done in your presence but in your absence, the same people who laughed when you were there will mock you. This is pretence, insincerity in play.

20. Bigger kills Mary due to 

A. fear 

B. envy 

C. hatred 

D. distrust

Option A, fear, is the correct answer. 

Drunk and high on drugs, Mary is driven back home from party by Biggy and plays outdoors without her clothes on. Seeing this from inside the building, Biggy takes her to her room. Mary discloses to Biggy her feelings for him and tries to seduce him but Biggy tries to contain her and put her to sleep seeing she is high. Mary's mum who happens to be blind comes to the corridor leading to Mary's room and calls out her name to know if she is back. The more Biggy struggles to control Mary, the more she insists on kissing Biggy. This struggle makes both to be in a romantic position, both lain on the bed with Biggy on top of Mary, facing each other. 

This time, Mary's mother approaches and comes into her room. 

Out of fear of being asked what he is doing in Mary's room at night, Biggy covers Mary's face with pillow to make her silent so the blind mother leaves. Doing this, he continues to look behind to make sure she hears no sound and leaves. Meanwhile, Mary struggles and gasps for breath and gives up the ghost unknowingly to Biggy.

 

21. 'This thing you are doing is too heavy for you, he 

said. I went to school only a little but I have killed 

many many more years in this world than you 

have'. G. Okara: The voice

It can be inferred from the passage above that the

A. listener is wise

B. speaker is a porter

C. listener is more experienced

D. speaker is more experienced.

The correct option is Option C, the listener is more experienced. 

The listener is more experienced than the speaker is. For the speaker to say that he has killed many more years in his life than the listener has means that he has a lot of wasted years spent on perhaps inexperience or idleness.
