Gasification means a process that converts organic/fossil fuel-based carbonaceous materials into carbon monoxide, hydrogen and carbon dioxide.

Synthesis gas, composed principally of carbon monoxide and hydrogen, and are used as the major building block in the production of chemicals and fuels. It is prepared by reacting coke with air and/or steam. There are two types of synthesis gas, Producer gas is produced when air is used in the gasification of coke while water gas is produced when water is used in gasification of coke. 

Producer gas (Air gas) is a combustible mixture of nitrogen, carbon monoxide and hydrogen. It is used as a fuel and prepared by passing air with steam over burning coke or coal in a furnace.

The producer gas is a mixture of CO (from coke) and N2 (from the air).

 

(1)  Which of the following pairs of substances will react further with oxygen to form a higher oxide? 

A. CO2 and H2O
B. NO and H2O
C. CO and CO2 
D. SO2 and NO

Answer D. 2018

Solution

Higher oxides are oxides which are already in their most complete form. This implies these oxides will not form new oxides when they burn in air eg CO2, SO3, PbO2

However, there are oxides that can still burn further in the presence of oxygen to form higher oxides eg CO, NO, SO2, PbO etc.

SO2 will burn further to form SO3 while NO can still form NO2. Water (H2O) cannot form an oxide. Hence, Option D

Common characteristics of Nonmetals:

  • Rarely have metallic luster (shine)
  • Generally, gases at room temperature
  • Neither malleable nor ductile
  • Poor conductors of heat and electricity
  • Usually exist as molecules in their elemental form
  • Combine with other nonmetals to form covalent
  • Generally, form negative ions, e.g.  Cl-, SO42-, and N3-

The differences in the characteristics of metals and nonmetals can be explained by the following:

  • Metals have relatively few electrons in their valence shells.

  • Metals have smaller electron affinities than nonmetals.
  • Metals have larger atoms than nonmetals.

A. strength of the core

C. source of the core

D. position of the metal in the periodic table

B. position of the metal in the electrochemical series. 2018

Explanation

The method used to extract a metal from its ore depends upon the stability of its compound in the ore, which in turn depends upon the reactivity of the metal:
