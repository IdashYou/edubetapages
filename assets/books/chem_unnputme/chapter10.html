B. 20sec 

C. 14sec 

D. 7sec 

Answer C. 2016

Solution

Molar mass of O2 = 16 × 2 = 32g/mol

Molar mass of CH4 = 12 + (1×4) = 16g/mol

Rate of diffusion of Oxygen = Volume diffused/time taken

RO2 = 200/50

Rate for O2 = 4cm³/sec

Using Graham's law of diffusion, rate of diffusion of gas is inversely proportional to the molar mass.

Let RO2 and RCH4 represent their rates of diffusion.

RO2/RCH4 = √(MCH4/MO2)

4/RCH4 = √(16/32)

RCH4 = 4√2 cm³/sec
