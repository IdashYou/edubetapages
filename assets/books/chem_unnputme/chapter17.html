1. Ionization Energy

Ionization energy is the energy required to remove the outermost (highest energy) electron from a neutral atom in its ground state.

1. As you move down a group, ionization energy decreases.

2. As you move across a period, ionization energy increases.

Reason

As we move down the group, Electrons are further from the nucleus and thus easier to remove the outermost one (lesser ionization energy will be needed). "SHIELDING" - Inner electrons at lower energy levels essentially block the protons' force of attraction toward the nucleus.  It therefore becomes easier to remove the outer electron.

Moreover, as you move across a period (from left to right), the atomic radius decreases, that is, the atom is smaller.  The outer electrons are closer to the nucleus and more strongly attracted to the center.  Therefore, it becomes more difficult to remove the outermost electron (more and more ionization energy will be needed.

Ionization energy is affected by the following factors

  1. Screening effect
  2. Orbital penetration
  3. Size of the nuclear charge
  4. Atomic radius

2. Electron Affinity

Electron affinity is the energy change that occurs when a gaseous atom acquires an electron to form a negative ion. It can be defined as the energy given off when a neutral atom in the gas phase gains an extra electron to form a negatively charged ion.

a. As you move down a group, electron affinity decreases.

b. As you move across a period, electron affinity increases.

3. Atomic Radii

a. As you move down a group, atomic radius increases.

b. As you move across a period, atomic radius decreases.

Reason

As you move down the group the number of energy levels increases while the number of electrons increases.  Each subsequent energy level is further from the nucleus than the last.  Therefore, the atomic radius increases as the group and energy levels increase.

Moreover, as you move across a period (from left to right), electrons are added to the same energy level.  At the same time, protons are being added to the nucleus.  The concentration of more protons in the nucleus creates a higher effective nuclear charge."  As a result, there will be a stronger force of attraction pulling the electrons closer to the nucleus and thereby reducing the atomic radius.

4. Ionic Radii

a. Anions (negative ions) as found in non-metals have a larger atomic radius when compared to their unionized state.

b. Cations (positive ions) as found in metals have a smaller atomic radius when compared to their unionized state. 

Reason

Generally, the ionic radius of metals is less than their atomic radii. This is as due to the fact that metals loose electrons to achieve the octet state. This creates a larger positive charge in the nucleus than the negative charge in the electron cloud, causing the electron cloud to be drawn a little closer to the nucleus as an ion. 

In Non-metals - the atomic radius of a non-metal is generally smaller than the ionic radius of the same element. Why? Generally, non-metals loose electrons to achieve the octet. This creates a larger negative charge in the electron cloud than positive charge in the nucleus, causing the electron cloud to 'puff out' a little bit as an ion.

5. Lattice Energy

Lattice energy is the energy given off when oppositely charged ions in the gas phase come together to form a solid.

1. As you move down a group, lattice energy decreases.

2. As you increase the magnitude of the charge (becomes more positive or more negative), lattice energy increases.

Reason

As we move down the group the atomic radius increases.  To better understand this you need to know the Coulomb’s law (Coulomb's Law - The force of attraction between oppositely charged particles is directly proportional to the product of the charges of the particles (q1 and q2) and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between the particles).

Therefore, since the square of the distance is inversely proportional to the force of attraction, lattice energy decreases as the atomic radius increases.

Moreover, an increase in magnitude of charge increase lattice energy because the product of the charges of the particles is directly proportional to the force of attraction. Therefore, lattice energy increases as the charges increase.

Metallic character

1. As you move across a period, metallic character decreases and nonmetallic character increases.

2. As you move down a group, metallic character increases and nonmetallic character decreases.

Semimetals (Metalloids)

A class of 8 elements that have properties of both metals and nonmetals.

B Si Ge As Sb Te Po At

Common characteristics of semimetals:

Physically, they look metallic but are brittle (not malleable or ductile) they do not serve as good conductors or insulators; instead they are semiconductors.

In Summary

Once a compound or element has the tendency to attain noble gas structure, the compound will undergo chemical reaction.

As you move down a column or group, the ionic radius increases. This is because each row adds a new electron shell. Ionic radius decreases moving from left to right across a row or period.

Ionization potential increases across the period, from left to right and decreases down the group.

Electronegativity also increases across the period, from left to right and decreases down the group.

Electron affinity also across the period, from left to right and decreases down the group.

But the atomic radius decreases across the period and increases down the group because of the increase in the number of shells down the group.

Cations or anions of different elements are said to be isoelectronic if they both have the same electronic configuration e.g. K+ and Ca 2+ For isoelectronic cations, the more positive the ionic charge, the smaller the ionic radius. For isoelectronic anions, the more negative the ionic charge, the larger the ionic radius.
