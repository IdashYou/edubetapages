Separation Techniques

 

In our Previous lesson we talk about physical changes and chemical changes, now in this topic we want to know the know seperation techniques for seperating mixture.

 

 

Before we proceed lets see some terms

 

  1. Mixture
  2. Compound

 

A mixture can be defined as a substance (material) made up of two or more different elements or compounds but are not chemically combined.  They are physically combined and can be separated by physical means 

 

A compound on the other hand is made up two or more element or another compound that  are chemcally combine.

 

We can say that the result of physical change give birth to mixture and that of chemical change give birth to compound.

 

But in reality some substance or matter exist in nature as mixture we actually did not mix them our self to course a physical change but just find out that someone of something did it for us, and we are like how can we get back the individual compound or element in the mixture which bring us to seperation technique.

 

Example of such mixture in nature include Petroleum, 
Salt Water(Mixture of salt and water especially in sea),
Alloys (Mixture of two or more compund), Air(Mixture of Oxygen, Nitrogen,Water Vapour, Carbon(IV)oxide e.t.c. and many more mixture.

 

WhIle separating techniques is used to seperate mixtures using physical method, compound can also be splitted to his individual element or compunds using chemical process such as double decomposition, Electolysis and so on but that are not our target.

 

Homogenous and Heterogenous Mixtures

When mixture are mix that they form a single phase it called Homogenous mixture like sugar solution but when there form layers it is called heterogenous mixture like crude oil (Petroleum).

 

 

The Different Sepearting Technique

 

  1.   Separating funnel is used to separate immiscible liquids,  (e.g. oil and water). The lower dense layer is tapped off leaving the upper less dense.
  2.   Sieving is a separation technique used in separating solid particles of different sizes.
  3.   Evaporation to dryness is used to recover a solid solute from a solution.A salt that is stable to heat, can be separated from salt water by evaporation. Solvent is sacrificed. E.g Salt is seperated from its solution using this method
  4.   Distillation is the process of separating components of a mixture based on different boiling points. Example purification of water, since water has a boiling point of 100 degree celsius anyother liquid with a different boiling point is removed in  another column while water is sent to water column.
  5.   Fractional Distilation, similar to normal distilation but has more fraction column for different liquid that is being separated and deals with more range of liquid and shorter range of boiling point, fractional distilation is used in seperating crude oil.
  6.   Crystallization from solution is a separation technique where a solid phase is separated from a mother liquor.Crystallization is used to separate salts, which decompose easily on heating, from their solutions.   An impure solid is completely dissolved in a minimal amount of hot, boiling solvent, and the hot solution is allowed to slowly cool, thereby forming crystal which can be collected.E.g Salt is seperated from its solution using this methos
  7.   Precipitate is used to separate two miscible liquid by adding a substance that will make one of the liquid insoluble (A preciptate)  in the presence of the new substance, the insoluble compound is them collected. Example is adding alum to a dirty water to remove the dirt, the dirt is remove as a precipitate
  8.   Decantation is used to separate insoluble solid from a liquid, after allowing the mixture to settle by standing it for a while.
  9.   Chromatography is used in separating mixtures with different rate of migration and possibly distinct colours.
  10. Sublimation is the process of transition of a substance from the solid to the gaseous state without passing through the liquid state. For example a mixture contain iodine and another solid can be separated using sublimation because on heating iodine will sublime without turning to liquid while the the other solid will turn to liuid first.
