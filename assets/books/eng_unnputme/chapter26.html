26. QUESTION TAG

A tag question is a question added to a declarative sentence, usually at the end, to engage the listener, verify that something has been understood, or confirm that an action has occurred. Also known as a question tag.

Steps involved

  1. Repeat the auxiliary verb, if there is one
  2. Use “do” if there’s no auxiliary verb
  3. If the main statement is positive, the question tag will be negative, and if the main statement is negative, then the question tag will be negative.
  4. Maintain the tense

 

  1. Repeat the auxiliary verb, if there is one

The auxiliary verbs are “is, “was,” “are,” “were,” “am,” “being,” “has,” “have,” “having,” “had,” “do,” “does,” “did,” “doing,” “may,” “might,” “can,” “could,” “shall,” “should,” “must,” “will,”  “would” and “ought”.

Just repeat the auxiliary verb in the question tag.

He is lovely, isn’t he?

The Nigerian economy ought to have been diversified

Wrong: isn’t it?

Correct: oughtn’t it?

In the correct example above, ought in the statement is repeated in the question tag in its negative form, “oughtn’t” (ought not).

 

  1. Use “do” if there’s no auxiliary verb

If the main verb in the statement is singular, “do” changes to “doesn’t”.  If it’s plural, “do” changes to “don’t”. If the main verb is in the past tense, “do” changes to “didn’t”.

And yes, these variants of “do” appear in the negative (i.e. with the addition of “n’t”) because of rule #3 below.

He passed the exam, didn’t he

Online business gives work freedom

Wrong: isn’t it?

Correct: doesn’t it?

There’s no auxiliary verb in the statement. The main verb is “gives” which is singular. So “doesn’t” is the right variant of “do” to use in the question tag.

 

  1. If the main statement is positive, the question tag will be negative, and if the main statement is negative, then the question tag will be negative.

Examples

He is a Doctor, isn’t he?

Giving you more examples will not hurt, will it?

You won the essay contest

Wrong: did you?

Correct: didn’t you?

‘Not' or -n’t shows that a statement is negative and requires negative tag. 

 

  1. Maintain the tense

The statement could be in the present tense, the past tense or the past participle. Whatever tense is used in the statement, maintain it in the question tag.

Example

He is afraid, isn’t he?

He killed the snake, didn’t he?

You were planning to be an engineer,

Wrong: aren’t you? 

Correct: weren’t you?

“Were” in the statement is a past tense and should be maintained in the question tag. Do you grasp that?

 

Other important points:

When ‘I am’ is used as the subject and the verb in the main sentence, use aren’t I as you tag.

I am getting it, aren’t I?

I am the boss here, aren’t I?

If you create a question tag out of a statement that contains broad negative (never, seldom, hardly, scarcely, rarely), the tag is normally positive.

The royal family hardly go to the market, do they?

Akpan seldom joins his mate for practice, does he?

We scarcely eat bitter leaf soup when Daddy is around, do we?

The school principal rarely discusses her problem with our community leader, does she?

In furtherance, where we have a complex sentence, the tag stems from the main clause:

He was dancing before we came, was he?

It seems that the boys have left their positions, doesn’t it?

 

Expected JAMB Examples

2. We ought to have visited the Governor... [A. isn't it B, oughtn't we C. shouldn't we D. haven't}? 

(oughtn't we)

 

3. He didn't sense Obi's presence in the room, did he?... [A. yes, he did B. No, he did C. Yes, he didn't D. No, he didn't]

(No, he didn't, Note: positive > Negative > Positive)

 

4. You live in the city now, ___? (a) are you (b) don't you (c) didn't you (d) haven't you
(don't you)

 

5. The Flying Eagles of Nigeria couldn't have won the match if they hadn't prepared well, __? (a) can't they (b) couldn't they (c) could they (d) can they
(could they)

 

6. Oloyede always sleeps like a baby, ___? (a) does he (b) could he (c) doesn't he (d) did he

(doesn't he)

 

7. We stood up when the principal came in __ [A. isn’t it B. didn’t we C. not so D. did us]?

(didn’t we)
