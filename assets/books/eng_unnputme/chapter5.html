5. WORDS SIMILAR IN MEANING (SYNONYMS)

What is a synonym?

A Synonym may be defined as a word that has the same meaning or the same general meaning as a particular word in the same Language or is in some applications a more or less satisfactory application of it. Thus, the synonym of a word can be an exact equivalent in meaning or should at least convey its meaning in one way or the other or should show in what sense it can best be used.

Context Clues

The Good news is that you can even predict accurately, the synonym of a word in a sentence even without knowing the accurate meaning of the word.  Understanding vocabulary, can be easily mastered using a tool always available to you; context. 

You can understand any new vocabulary term using only the context around it. By looking at the elements of a passage, an unknown vocabulary word reveals its meaning. 

Most English words are ambiguous, they have multiple meanings that vary in how much they overlap. Many words have multiple semantically-unrelated meanings.

Examples

Watch - a time piece

Watch - to look

Rose - a flower

Rose - past tense of rise

Others have multiple semantically-related senses 

Example 

twist an ankle

twist the truth

So, to determine the meaning of a word in a sentence you must relate it with the words before and after it in that particular sentence.

This is how the questions are framed - In each of questions 6-9, choose the option nearest in meaning to the word or phrase in italics.

Steps in Selecting Option Most Similar in Meaning

1. Always re-read the instruction at the top bar each time you enter a new question, most times due to the way the CBT platform is designed. Students normally assume they are still answering questions on options similar in meaning without knowing that they have entered antonyms. Be very careful.

2. Try to understand the central meaning of the given sentence as a whole.

2. Eliminate the obviously wrong answers like the options opposite in meaning to the underlined word.

3. Make a habit of reading all the options that are provided to you before answering the questions to be sure you are not fooled by nearly correct choices. 

4. Select an answer that is the most similar and in context to the underlined or italicised word.
