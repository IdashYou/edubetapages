6. WORDS OPPOSITE IN MEANING (ANTONYMS)

Antonyms are words, which means the very opposite or nearly the opposite of each other in a particular sentence. Please take note that both synonyms and antonyms are often given in the Lexis and Structure section of UNN PUTME examination.

As we discussed earlier in the synonyms section, the issue of context is very important in determining the right opposite option. 

When we say context, we mean that the words or ideas expressed before and after the underlined word, provides us with the information we need to fully understand, evaluate or interpret the ideas or word in the passage.

Let’s take for example

Chimma was still single when she made her first Million.

In this sentence the opposite in meaning can’t be double because we are talking about her relationship status. So, a suitable option will be married.

So, to determine the meaning of a word in a sentence you must relate it with the words before and after it in that particular sentence. This can help you guess the answer when you don’t know the meaning of the word.

This is how the questions are presented - In question 9-11, choose the option opposite in meaning to the word(s) in italics.

There are some rules that will help you to select the right options. 

Steps in selecting the option opposite in meaning

1. Always re-read the instruction at the top bar each time you enter a new question, most times due to the way the CBT platform is designed. Students normally assume they are still answering questions on options similar in meaning without knowing that they have entered antonyms. Be very careful.

2. Try to understand the central meaning of the given sentence as a whole.

2. Eliminate the obviously wrong answers, like the options similar in meaning to the word underlined.

3. Make a habit of reading all the options that are provided to you before answering the questions, to be sure you are not fooled by nearly correct choices. 

4. Select an answer that is opposite in meaning, and in context to the underlined or italicised word.
