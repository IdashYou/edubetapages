MOVEMENT

Tropisms are natural responses of organisms to external stimuli. Thigmotropism is a response that is often seen in plants in response to touch or contact. 

Nastic movement are directional responses to stimuli (e.g. temperature, humidity, light irradiance), and are usually associated with plants. The movement can be due to changes in turgor or changes in growth

Photropism (Heliotropism) means the unilateral response of plants parts to the stimulus of light.

Hydrotropism means the response of roots to the stimulus of water in the soil. 

Thigmotropism (Haptotropism) means the response of the aerial parts of some plants to touch. 

Geotropism (Gravitropism) means the response of plant parts to the stimulus of gravity. 

Hydrotropism is a plant's growth response in which the direction of growth is determined by a stimulus or gradient in water concentration. A common example is a plant root growing in humid air bending toward a higher relative humidity level.

Taxis (tactic movement): this is the locomotory movement of an entire organism or cell (e.g. gamate) in response to a diectional stimulus. If the locomotry movement is towards the stimulus, it is positive taxis, if it is away it is negative taxis.

Animals respond to stimulus of 

  • Light (phototaxis)
  • Water (hydrotaxis)
  • Chemicals (chemotaxis)
  • Current (rheotaxis)

1. The movement of a plant in response to external stimulus of no particular direction is____

A. taxism B. tropism C. haptotropic movement D. nastic movement E. phototropism

(D. Nastic movement are directional responses to stimuli (e.g. temperature, humidity, light irradiance), and are usually associated with plants. The movement can be due to changes in turgor or changes in growth, 2015/2016, no 5)

 

2. The response of parts of contact or touch is termed

A. thigmotropism B. traumatropism C. rheptropism D. galvanotropism 

(A. Tropisms are natural responses of organisms to external stimuli. Thigmotropbm (Haptotropism) means the response of the aerial parts of some plants to touch, 2017/2018 II, no 5)

 

3. Phototropism is

A. a unilateral response to light B. a bilateral response to light C. both unilateral and bilateral response to light D. a hormonal gradient created within the organ

(A. Photropism (Heliotropism) means the unilateral response of plants parts to the stimulus of light, 2008/2009, no 5)

 

4. The process by which a plant grows towards the direction of water is_____

A. geotropism B. phototropism C. hydrotropism D. chemotropism

(C. Hydrotropism is a plant's growth response in which the direction of growth is determined by a stimulus or gradient in water concentration. A common example is a plant root growing in humid air bending toward a higher relative humidity level, 2014/2015, no 9)
