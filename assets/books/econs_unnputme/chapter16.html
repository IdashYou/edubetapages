16. INDUSTRIALIZATION

What is an Industry? An industry refers to a group of firms that produce similar products.

A firm is different from an industry. A firm is a business unit or organisation that carries out production while an industry is a group of different firms that produce similar products and under separate management. Therefore, the firms that makes up an industry have different management. A firm is a type of business whereas an industry is a sub sector of an economy.

Types of Industry

1. Processing industry: this is a type of industry that specializes in the extraction of raw materials and converting them into semi-finished goods, examples include timber and rubber industries.

2. Manufacturing Industry: unlike processing industries, manufacturing industry specializes in the transformation of raw materials into finished goods which can be either be a consumer goods or producer goods. An example is an industry that converts rubber into plastic cups.

3. Mining Industry: these are industries that deals on the extraction of Minerals and other natural resources from the ground. An example is the Quarrying industries that extract stones. Minerals extracted includes, coal, crude oil, diamond, etc.

Other types of industries include,

Construction industries like road and house construction industries

Transport industries like Nigerian airway lines industries

Electricity industries like EEDC, NEPA etc.

Industrialization

Industrialization means the policy of establishing many industries in a nation with the aim of increasing the economic development of the nation.

Strategies (Measures) Adopted for Industrialization

1. Import Substitution: this involves the promotion of domestic product usage in a country by the government, rather than relying on foreign products, produced in another country. By doing this more industries will be set up in the country.

2. Export Promotion: this involves the promotion and support by the government to local industries that export their products to other countries. In Nigeria we have the National Export Promotion board NEPB). 

3. Small Scale versus Large Scale Industrial Development Strategy: These are policies made to favour either the large-scale industries or the small-scale industries, depending on the economic condition and goal of a nation. 

4. Balanced Growth Strategy: this is a strategy that supports the development of all sectors of the economy and areas of the country. It is aimed at correcting all the deficiencies encountered during industrialization.

Factors that Slows Down Industrialization

1. insufficient Capital

2. overpopulation

3. Lack of true Entrepreneurship

4. Political Instability

5. Inadequate raw materials

6. Inadequate power supply

7. Absence of developed market

Advantages of Industrialization

1. Creation of Job Opportunities 

2. Promotion of economic development

3. Favourable Balance of payments and terms of trade

4. increased productivity

5. Diversification of economy

Location of Industry

The location of industry is not randomly decided it involves the establishment of industry in an area either by the government or an entrepreneur after considering many important factors

Factors that determines the location of an industry

  1. Availability of raw materials (inputs)
  2. The size of Capital
  3. The Climate
  4. Political Stability
  5. Government policy
  6. Proximity to the market
  7. Government policy
  8. Proximity to source of power

Proximity to the source of raw materials is an important factor in locating a business site of production. Therefore, the presence of limestone which is the raw material for cement factory is an important factor. 

Localization of Industries

The term localization of industry means the concentration of many firms of an industry in a given specific area. The firms produce commodities which belong to the same industrial group and this results in structural unemployment. Structural unemployment is an unemployment due to skills gap as a result of changes in production techniques

Advantages of Localization of Industries

  1. Creation of employment opportunities
  2. Development of subsidiary firms that assist the firms in production.
  3. Creation of an organized market
  4. Inter-dependence of firms
  5. Attracts of Social Amenities
  6. Conduction of research
  7. Increase in invention and innovation

Disadvantages of Localization of Industries

When the same companies are crowded together in a particular area, it will result in an unfavourable competition among the firms, which will decrease the demand of goods of some firms. For the company to survive they will change their economy and production technique which will reduce the number of workers leading to structural unemployment.

  1. It leads to structural unemployment
  2. It causes environmental pollution (air pollution, water pollution, etc.)
  3. Unfavorauble competition
  4. Depletion (over usage) of Social amenities due to high demand from industries.
