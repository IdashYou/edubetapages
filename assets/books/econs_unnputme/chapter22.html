22. DEFLATION

Deflation is a persistent fall in the general price level. It IS the Opposite of inflation. During deflation, there is a fall in the aggregate demand as a result of scarcity of money in the economy. The value of money rises because of decrease in the prices of goods and services. This is a period of economic recession. Business and industrial activities are very slow. During deflation, there is increase in aggregate supply without the corresponding increase in aggregate demand. 

Causes of deflation

  1. Excess supply over demand
  2. Excessive use of fiscal policy by the government
  3. Restriction on bank lending
  4. Excessive use of budget surplus

Control of deflation

  1. Expansionary monetary policy
  2. Use of fiscal policy, like increasing aggregate demand to lead to increase in prices
  3. Increase in government expenditure
