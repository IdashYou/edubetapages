25. AGRICULTURE

Agriculture is the science and art of cultivating plants and livestock. It involves the production of animals, fishes, crops, and forest resources for different purposes like consumption, research and commercial purposes. Agriculture was the key development in the rise of sedentary human civilization, whereby farming of domesticated species created food surpluses that enabled people to live in cities.

Components of Agriculture

Agriculture has many components which includes

1. Crop Production: This is an aspect of agriculture that deals with the production of different kinds of crops like food crops (cassava, maize, beans, etc.) and cash crops (cotton, rubber, groundnuts, palm oil, etc.).

2. Livestock: This involves the rearing of domestic animals like chicken, goat, pig, etc.

3. Forestry: This involves the preservation and maintenance of forest resources which includes plants and wild animals. It also involves the regulated extraction of resources associated with forest.

4. Fishing: This involves the breeding and catching of form different water bodies for the use of man. 

Systems of Agriculture

There are different systems of Agriculture which includes

1. Peasant Farming (subsistence agriculture): Peasant farming refers to a type of small scale agriculture. Peasant farmers grow crops and often rear some livestock on a small scale. It is done on a small piece of land with rudimentary equipment such as hoes, axe, cutlass, etc.

2. Plantation farming: This refers to the use of large estate of land to grow economic and commercial crops. It involves the use of modern machines for large scale production.

3. Mechanized farming (Mechanization): This is a system of agriculture that involves the extensive use of machines and other advanced mechanical devices in agriculture for large scale production. It is highly capital intensive.

4. Co-operative Farming: This is an organization formed by the coming together of farmers with the common interest of increasing the production of it’s members. This makes it easier for them to obtain loan and hire farm machines.

Importance of Agriculture

1. Provision of food and feed.

2. Provision of raw materials

3. Source of income for farmers

4. Reduction of unemployment

5. Promotes economic growth

6. Raises the standard of living.

7. Guarantees food security.

Problem of Agriculture

1. Lack Capital

2. Lack of credit facilities

3. Poor transportation network

4. Problem of land tenure system

5. Unfavorable climatic condition.

6. Poor marketing system.

7. Pests and diseases

8. Poor marketing system.

Solution to the Problem of Agriculture

1. Mechanisation of Agriculture

2. Granting of credit facilities

3. Effective control pests and diseases.

4. Formation of cooperative societies among farmers

5. Provision of good transport network

6. Provision of good storage facilities.
