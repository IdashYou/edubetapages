3. BASIC ECONOMIC PROBLEM

An economic system is an arrangement specifying how production and consumption are controlled in a society. Examples of different economic systems are capitalism, socialism and mixed economy.  The society must make choice from the scarce resources which, have alternative uses. The problem of scarcity brings economic problems of the society.

The basic economic problems include

  1. What to produce?
  2. How to produce?
  3. For whom to produce?
  4. Efficiency of resource use
  5. How much to consume and save?

What to produce?

What to produce primarily depends on consumers in free market. The consumers choose the product they like and thus their choices direct the types of production that should be carried out. The firms will follow this because this is the most profit maximizing combination.

Sometimes the government too can decide what to produce. The government may decide to produce an essential good or service which everyone ought to have.

How to produce?

This question will be answered by those supplying the goods and services. If the supplier is a private firm, it will seek to use the method which will give the maximum profit. For example, production can be done using labour intensive method and capital-intensive method. The private firm will decide on the method which will give lowest average costs.

If the government is the supplier, it may try to use the method which promotes welfare of the society rather than maximising the profit.

For whom to produce?

For whom to produce will also depend on the suppliers (government and private firms). The consumers are the target of production, but the kind of consumers the firm or the government wants to target is the question. The government usually produces for the general public whereas the private firms can seek to maximize profit by producing for the high and rich level customers as well as the general public. In simple words, the production is done for those who are willing to pay.

Note: among the suppliers, there will also be private individuals (sole traders). Their objective in production is the same as that of the private firms – that is, to maximise profit.

Efficiency of resources use 

It is a fact that resources are scarce and as a result, all the available resources should be used without allowing anyone to lie idle. This is the problem of getting the best result from any approach we adopt in employing our scarce resources (that is technical efficiency). If all the resources are used, maximum output will be produced and this can only be achieved through the use technical efficiency.

How much to commit? and save? 

In the society, all the outputs produced are not to be consumed. Some could be kept for future consumption or for export which will generate some funds to the society. This fund can later be used for further production of goods and services.

Different economic system

This includes;

capitalism, socialism and mixed economy 

Capitalism is an economic system that allows private ownership of property or means of production and distribution. This is also called market economy. 

Socialism refers to economic system in which the state owns and controls the means of production and distribution.

Mixed Economy is an economic system where both the private individuals and the government are allowed to own and control the means of production and distribution. 

Market Economy (Capitalism)

A market economy is defined as a system where the production of goods and services are set according to the changing desires and abilities of the market players. It allows the market to operate freely in accordance with the law of supply and demand, set by individuals and corporations, as opposed to governments.

How the basic problems are solved in a Capitalist or free market economy 

What to produce: 

The question as to what to produce and at What quantities in a free market economy is answered by the forces of demand and supply. Goods that use in high demand attract high prices and this encourages producers to produce more while goods that are in low demand attract low prices and this discourages production. 

How to Produce: In a free market system, where labour is cheap and available in abundance, a wise decision is to use a labour intensive method or to use a capital-intensive method where capital is relatively cheap. 

For whom to produce: It is a question of how to distribute the goods produced Under a free market economy, it is decided by the forces of demand and supply. 

Efficient use of resources: This is the problem of getting the best result from any approach we adopt in employing our scarce resources. The problem is solved by application of technical efficiency which is making use of the best production method to produce the highest production level at the least possible cost. 

How much to consume and save (for investment)? In a free enterprise, all output produced are not to be consumed, some could be kept for future consumption or for export which will generate some funds for the society.

Socialist Economy

A socialist (centrally-planned) economic system can be defined as a system where the means of production and distribution of goods and services are controlled by the government. The goods and services are produced with the main aim of satisfying the wants of the whole citizens.

How the basic problems are solved in Socialist Economy

What to Produce: The decision of what to produce and at what quantities is carried out based on national priorities. The state estimates the needs of the people through its agencies. 

How to Produce: The main objective in a socialist economy is to ensure full utilization of resources. The planning authority therefore, chooses a technique of production that is in line with this objective. For Whom to Produce: Production is controlled by the state or government. Therefore, goods are distributed equitably by government agencies according to the desires of the consumers. 

How the basic problems are solved in a Mixed Economy 

This is a combination of the features of both capitalism and socialism. Most economic activity in countries around the world exists on a spectrum that ranges from a pure free market economy to an extreme command economy.

What to Produce: What to produce by private sector is controlled by the market forces of demand and supply while the planning agency decides what to produce in the public sector and administer prices.

How to Produce: In the private sector, the profit motive of firms decides how to ptoduce. However, in the public sector, the decision is made on the basis of the welfare motive. 

For Whom to Produce: Some of the goods that are produced are distributed in the market on the basis of price mechanism while some are distributed by the government through the public distribution.

NB: In capitalism, basic problems are solved through the price mechanism while in socialism the state solves the problems through its agencies.

8. The condition for equilibrium price and quantity under perfect competition is?

A. MC = AR = TR

B. TC =AR = P

C. MC = AR = P

D. MC = AR = TC 

Option C. 2000

A competitive firm always operates at the lowest point of its AC curve. This means that the firm produces at an optimum cost. In the long run, as a competitive firm earns only normal profit (because of free entry and exit), P = minimum AC. That is to say, in the long run, competitive equilibrium occurs when P = AR = MR, MC = minimum AC. This means optimal utilization of resources.
