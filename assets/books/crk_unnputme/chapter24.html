24. DEBORAH AS A LEADER

The leadership of Deborah 

After the death of Joshua and Ehud one of the elders who lived after him, the people of Israel did what was evil in the sight of the Lord by going after other gods and the Lord sold them into the hands of Jabin the king of Canaan, who reigned in Hazor. 

The king of Canaan oppressed the people of Israel using Sisera, who was his army commander with nine hundred chariots of iron for twenty years. Following the oppression of the king, they cried out to the Lord for help and the lord came to their rescue through the instrumentality of a woman called Deborah at that time, Deborah, a prophetess and wife of Lappidoth was judging Israel. 

She summoned Barak the son of Abinoam who came from the tribe of Naphtali and instructed him as the lord directed her to gather his men at mount Tabor, taking with himself ten thousand men from the tribe of Naphtali and the tribe of Zebulun in a battle against Siserah, the commander of Jabin’s army by the river Kishon, with the promise of giving Jabin into his hand. 

Barak however declined going except Deborah goes with him. Deborah accepted going with him but pointed out to him that the lord will sell Siserah into the hand of a woman. 

When they got to the battle field, the Canaanites were defeated prompting Siserah to alight from his chariot and fled on foot to the house of Jael, the wife of Heber the Kenite, owing to the long-standing peace between Jabin the king and Heber the kenite. 

Jael lured him into her tent, covered him with rug. When Siserah asked for water, Jael opened a skin of milk, served him a drink and covered him again. Being so tired he slept off and Jael took a tent peg with hammer and nailed him on the ground leading to his death thereby fulfilling the prophecy of Deborah that the glow would go to a woman instead of Barak. 

As Barak was pursuing Siserah Jael went out to meet him calling inviting him to her tent to see Siserah whom he was seeking. Low and behold, Barak went in and saw Siserah laying dead, with the tent peg in his temple. Thus, the lord granted victory to the Israelites and Jabin the king 0f Canaan was destroyed. g
