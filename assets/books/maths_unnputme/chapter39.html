BINARY OPERATIONS

A binary operation is any rule of combination of any two elements of a given non-empty set. It is usually denoted by an asterisk (*).

Operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication and division of real numbers are all binary operations. 

However, the problems that we'll encounter in the study of binary operations don't normally focus on these basic operations.

Binary operation problems are normally contrived from new sets of rules that define the operations. Below, we look at some of the examples of these operations.

Example 1

A binary operation on the set of real numbers is defined by x*y = (x+y) / 2 for all x,y ∈ R. 

Find 7 * 5

  1. 8
  2. 9
  3. 5
  4. 6

UTME, 2017, D

Solution

What we are going to do is follow the rule of the operation. It says that 

x * y = (x + y) / 2

Therefore, 

7 * 5 = (7+5) /2

7 * 5 = 12 / 2

ie 6

 

Example 2

If a binary operation * is defined by x * y = x + 2y, find 2 * (3 * 4)

  1. 26
  2. 24
  3. 16
  4. 14

UTME, 2013, B

Solution

First, we evaluate the operation in the bracket by following the rule for the operation,

(3 * 4) = 3 + 2 × 4

(3 * 4) = 11

2 * (3 * 4) = 2 * 11

Applying the same rule to the above,

2 * 11 = 2 + 2 × 11

2 * 11 = 24

: . 2 * (3 * 4) = 24

Identity Element

Giving a non-empty set S which is closed under a binary operation *, if there exist an element e such that: 

a * e = e * a = a      for all a € S! 

Then, e is called an identity element in S under the given operation *

Example 1

A binary operation on the set of real numbers excluding -1 is such that for all m, n ε R, m Δ n = m + n + mn. Find the identity element of the operation.

  1. 1
  2. 0
  3. -1/2
  4. -1

UTME, 2008, B.

Solution

The condition for the identity element, e is

m * e = e * m = m --- #

The rule for combining elements in our case is

m ∆ n = m + n + mn

This implies,

m ∆ e = m + e + me

m ∆ e = e (1+m) + m 

Also,

e ∆ m = e + m + em

e ∆ m = e (1+m) + m 

Upon careful observation, we see that

m ∆ e = e ∆ m as required.

Finally, we take either of m ∆ e or e ∆ m and equate to m. See eqn # if you are confused!

: . e (1+m) + m = m 

=> e (1+m) = 0

:. e = 0

An operation ∆ is defined on the set of real numbers by:

a ∆ b = ab / 3 for all a, b € R. Find the identity element.

Solution

The condition for the identity element, e is

a ∆ e = e ∆ a = a

Following the rule for the operation above,

a ∆ e = ae /3 

Also,

e ∆ a = ea /3 

This implies

a ∆ e = e ∆ a as required!

Finally, we take either of a ∆ e or e ∆ a and equate to a.

=> ae / 3 = a

e /3 = 1

Finally, e = 3

Inverse Element

Consider a non-empty set S, which is closed under a binary operation ∆, if x € S and we can find an element x-1 such that 

x ∆ x-1 = x-1 ∆ x = e

where e is the identity element, then x-1 is called the inverse of x in A. It is quite usual to denote the inverse of x as x-1. 

x-1 does not necessarily mean ¹/x unless the operation ∆ is multiplication.

Example 1

A binary operation on the set of real numbers is defined by m*n = mn / 2 for all m, n ∈ R. If the identity element is 2, find the inverse of -5

  1. -1/5
  2. -1/4
  3. 45
  4. -⅘

UTME, 2012, D

Solution

Let the inverse of -5 be z

The rule for the inverse is given by:

- 5 * z = z * - 5 = e --- (#)

So, we need to find the identity element, e under this operation.

m * e = me / 2 according to the rule of the operation.

Finally, we write 

 m * e = m

i.e. me / 2 = m

e / 2 = 1

: . e = 2

Going back to # and following the rule for the operation again,

-5 * z = -5z / 2

Finally, we write -5 * z = e

ie. -5z / 2 = 2

z = -4/5

Example 2

A binary operation ⊗ defined on the set of integers is such that m⊗n = m + n + mn for all intergers m and n. Find the inverse of -5 under this operation, if the identity element is 0.

  1. - 5/4
  2. 5/6
  3. 0
  4. 5

UTME, 2009, A

Solution

Let the inverse of -5 be z. The inverse element is given by

-5 ∆ z = e ---# (e = 0 in our case)

-5 ∆ z = -5 + z -5z

-5 ∆ z = -5 -4z

Finally, from #

-5 - 4z = 0

4z = - 5

z = - 5/4 

Example 3

A binary operation Θ defined on the set of real numbers is such that xΘy = xy/6 for all x. y ∈ R, Find the inverse of 20 under this operation when the identity element is 6

  1. 10/3
  2. 9/5
  3. 1/20
  4. 1/12

UTME, 2006, B

Solution

We reiterate the same processes as before. If the inverse of 20 be z,

20 ∆ z = 20z / 6

Since we want 20 ∆ z = e which in our case is 6,

20z / 6 = 6

20z = 36

z = 36 / 20

z = 9/5
