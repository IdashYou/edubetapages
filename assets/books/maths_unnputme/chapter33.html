Money is Not Free to Borrow

People can always find a use for money, so it costs to borrow money.

How Much does it Cost to Borrow Money?

Different places charge different amounts at different times!

But they usually charge this way:

As a percent (per year) of the amount borrowed


It is called Interest

Example: Borrow $1,000 from the Bank

Tejiri wants to borrow N100,000. The local bank says "10% Interest". So to borrow the N100,000 for 1 year will cost:

N100,000 × 10% = N10,000

So Tejiri will pay back N100,000 at the end of the end.

What happen if Tejiri pay back in two years time, that means that she will pay back interest of two years plus the initial money she borrow which is 2 * N10,000 + N100,000 =N120,000.

While some bank may disagree and say that if she can’t pay back in one year she was suppose to pay is as if she is borrowing N110,000 from the bank again to pay her old debt and  the process continue

10% of N110,000 which is N11,000, so she will pay, N110,000 + N11,000 = N121,000 at the end of the second year.

Don’t be confused, the first case is a case of simple interest and the later a case of compound interest.

Lets Understand some terms before we proceeds.

  1. Principal the money you borrow.
  2. Rate the percentage of that money you borrow that you are to pay as the cost to borrow money.
  3. Time The duration it took you to pay back the money our standard duration is year (Per Annun), but in reality it could be monthly, quaterly.
  4. Amount Total amount payable with interest.

Why Borrow?

Well ... you may want to buy something you like. Paying it back will end up costing you more though.

But a business may be able to use the money to make even more money.

Investment is when you put money where it can grow, such as a bank, or a business.

If you invest your money at a good interest rate it can grow very nicely.

Investment is opposite of loan, so just the same way you take loan to and pay simple or compound interest you can invest and be paid simple and compound interest but you need to be careful when collecting loan or investing so you may not enter into trouble.

For Investment we use the term ROI return on Investment rather than simple or compound intesrest.

Calculation of Return on investment is similar to that of compound interest or simple interest except a special sequence is used. Like the example below.

Example
Mr Johnson N10,000 Invested in a poultry business and his return of investment is a fixed 10% for the first 100 chicken and 5% for the rest chicken sold per annum, calculate his R.O.I if 350 chicken was sold.

Solution

Since 350 chicken was sold.

For the first 100 chicken

R.O.I = (PXRXT)/100 = (10000 x 10 x 1)/100= N1000

For the remaining 250 chicken

R.O.I = (PXRXT)/100 = (10000 x 5 x 1)/100= N500

That means is total R.O.I is N10,000 + N500 = N15,000.

 

Each investment has their different condition so the ability for you to calculate Return of Investment will make you not to be cheated upon when you eventually invest in someone business.

 

Annuity
Annuities are insurance contracts that provide a fixed income stream for a person's lifetime or a specified period of time. An annuity can be purchased with a lump sum or a series of payments and begin paying out almost immediately or at some point in the future. Annuities are often used as a way to fund retirement.

Just like investment annuity can be calculated like simple or compund interest or base on special formula.

Annuity is known as future money, pension,Insurance or other name associated with it.

You can ask your local pension and insuarance company about annuity, annunity is another form of investment also.

 

Tax
Money that the government collects based on income, sales, and other activities.

Example: Alex earned N300,000 but had to pay N42,000 of that to the government as tax.

When calculating tax we consider the following,

 

Tax free income and tax band.

 

For example if you earn N100,000

Your tax free income could be N25,000 and your tax band could be as follows,

First N10,000 tax to be paid 10%

Next N30,000 tax to be paid 20%

And the next amount tax to be paid is 25%

 

This method is called PAYE (Pay as you Earn) and its practice by most country.

 

Lets solved the tax the person above will pay.

 

 

Tax free income = N25,000

So taxable income = N100,000 - N25,000= N75,000

 

Tax for the first N10,000 = 

 

10/100 x N10,000 =N1,000

 

We are left with N75,000 - N10,000 = N65,000.

 

Tax for the next N20,000

 

20/100 X N20,000 = N4,000

 

We are left with N65,000 -N20,000 = N45,000

 

Tax for the rest N45,000

 

25/100 x N45,000 =  N11,250

 

So total tax = N1,000 + N4,000 + N11,250 = N16,250.

 

You see how simple it is to calculate income tax.
