Basic Integral Calculus

 

The derivative of a function, written dy/dx or f'(x) is the rate of change of the function at any given point. We can evaluate derivatives up to an infinite number of times, provided the function remains differentiable. And so, we can write d2y/dx2 or f''(x) for the second derivative or differential of the function. Similarly, we can take the third, fourth, fifth derivative etc.….

17. CURVES AND TURNING POINTS

Maxima and Minima Points Including Inflexion points

Given a curve, f(x), which simultaneously increases and also decreases in some neighborhood of x, there exists at least a turning point such that f(x) switches from increasing to decreasing or vice versa. 

This turning point could be a cup (U-shaped) or cap (inverted U). These are what we refer to as the minimum and maximum values respectively. At those values, f'(x) changes signs, from positive to negative derivative or vice versa.

The terms, "maximum" and "minimum" can be misleading. In general, maximum or minimum values do not mean absolute maximum or minimum. They only indicate the values of f(x) which mark the turning points of the curve. 

A curve may turn at a point but later fall far below the (minimum) turning point in some other neighborhood of x, likewise for the maximum. In this case, the turning points cannot be the absolute minimum/maximum for the entire curve, only their neighborhood. 

It helps to think of maximum and minimum values as maximum/minimum turning point values, which states clearly there may still be higher/lower values of f(x) than the former. This does not however, rule out the possibility of having a turning point that is also absolute maximum/minimum.

Test for Turning Points

  1. At a turning point, dy/dx = 0
  2. For the maximum turning point, the sign of dy/dx changes from +ve to -ve just before and after this point.

 => max turning point, d2y/dx2 is -ve.

  1. For the minimum turning point, the sign of dy/dx changes from -ve to +ve just before and after this point.

=> min turning point, d2y/dx2 is +ve.

  1. Finally, there is also a different kind of turning point, which is neither a cup or cap. This is slender S-shaped turning point. This point is called a point of inflexion. 
    • At this point, d2y/dx2 = 0

 

TANGENTS AND NORMAL TO A CURVE

A tangent to a curve is a line that touches or brushes the curve at a given point. A tangent does not cut/cross a curve, rather it caresses the curve at the tangent point. 

A normal to a curve is a line which cuts through the curve at right angles to the tangent point. It passes perpendicular to the given point through the curve.

Steps involved in finding the equation of a tangent or normal to a curve at a point

  1. Find the slope/derivative, m of the curve at the required point.
  2. Using the eqn of a line, (y - y1) = m (x - x1), write down the eqn of the tangent line for the given point (x1, y1).
  3. If it's a normal you are required to find, follow step 1 and use m' = - 1/m for the slope of the line (normal).
  4. Follow step 2 above and complete the eqn of the normal with slope, m' = - 1/m in place of m.

Let us see some examples

Solution

dy/dx = 3x2 - 18x + 24

At the turning points, dy/dx = 0

:. 3x2 - 18x + 24 = 0

Solving for x, x = 4 or x = 2

To determine the nature of these points, we take the second derivative.

d2y/dx2 = 6x – 18

Substitute x = 4 and x = 2 respectively

d2y/dx2|x=4 = 6(4) -18 = 24 - 18 = +6 > 0

d2y/dx2|x=2 = 6(2) - 18 = 12 - 18 = - 6 < 0

We therefore conclude,

 x = 2 is a max point and x = 4 is a minimum point
