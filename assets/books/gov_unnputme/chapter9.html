The administrative structures/ institution of the emirate system.

The Executive: He was in charge of all the officials within the emirate. That was the head of all officials. He was the highest official. He was the political head of state. He consulted and recommended all political appointees to the Emir before ratification by council of state 

Madawaki: The commander of the army/cavalry. He advised the Emir on matter of peace and war. 

Galadima: The administrator of the Emirate capital. He maintained orderliness within the capital or city. He deputize the chief palace administrator, Serkin Fada Serkin Fada: The head of the palace workers. He is the chief palace administrator. He maintained order at the king's court. 

Dogari/ Serkin Yan Doka: The chief of the police responsible for internal security Maajz’: The officer in charge of treasury, a kind of minister of finance. He was the Chancellor of the Exchequer. He formulated fiscal policies; that is policies on revenues 

Hakim: The head of district 

Serkin Ruwa: The head of water resources. He regulated fishery ceremonies. 

Serkin Pawn: The head of butchers. He ensured that quality animals were killed. Yuri: Chief Superintendent of prisons. 

Yari: Chief Superintendent of prisons.

The Igbo Political System and Yoruba System

The Igbo political organization was segmented because there was no central government headed by a king. The administrative system was highly decentralized with diffused power. There was no monarch or kingship except in Onitsha where there was Obi title and red cap chiefs as a result of contact between Onitsha and Edo. 

The executive functions were performed by the village Assembly, the Council of Elders, the Age Grade, and the Masquerade Cult. The Council of elders comprises of the heads of different families (Okpara). 

The Yorubas had a centralized administration in pre-colonial Nigeria.

In the Yoruba kingdom the Ogboni cult was one of the political institutions and it was a secret society which is comprised of prominent elders and headed by the Oluwa. 

This fraternity plays a judiciary role in the Yoruba kingdom administration by checking the excesses of the kingmakers in the area of dethronement of the oba and mediated between the oba and the king makers whenever there is a disagreement. In the Yoruba political system, they use suicide symbol (occultic calabash) for checking and controlling the possible dictatorial rule of the king (oba).

Colonial Administration

The British administration used two principles in their administration, which are the crown colony and the protectorate.

In the crown colony the territory acquired is governed directly by the British govemment. The protectorate is an indirect rule in which the territory is protected by the British government in order to prevent any other colonial power from occupying and governing the territory. Due to the need for both human and financial resources, the British colonial government introduced the indirect rule system to effectively control Nigeria. The British colonization of Nigeria was fueled by their economic interest in the area, in search of cheap natural and human resources for the development of their economy.
