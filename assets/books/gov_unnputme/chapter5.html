1. STRUCTURE OF GOVERNMENT

Unitary system is a structure of governance with a single unit of authority known as the federal government. A unitary state is a state governed as a single power in which the central government is ultimately supreme. The central government may create administrative divisions. Such units exercise only the powers that the central government chooses to delegate.  It is a governance structure where there is concentration of powers on the strong central government. In unitary structure of governance, the constitution recognizes only a single authority, that is, the federal government. Thus, there is only one source of authority. Local units have no constitutional provision. 

Where local authorities exist, they are creation of central government and derive their powers from the federal authority. In other words, the central government derives its power from the constitution, while the local authorities derive their power from the central government. A large majority of the world's states have a unitary system of government and adopt a unicameral legislature (of a legislative body having a single legislative chamber).

Advantages of Unitary Structure 

  1. National unity and integration: It promote unity, integration, and nation building among the people of a given nation.
  2. Quick decision taking: Decision making is easier in unitary system because the central authority need not consult other units of administration before taking decision on national issues.
  3. Balanced development.
  4. Easy location of sovereignty: Sovereignty in a unitary state can be identified and easily located at the central government since it is the only source of authority.
  5. Elimination of jurisdictional conflict: Conflicts of jurisdiction between tiers of government are eliminated because the central government is the sole source of authority.
  6. Economy of scale: Unitary system generates economy of scale and thus promotes judicious utilization of resources.
  7. Suitability for homogeneous society: It is suitable for country with homogeneous or similar culture and small population.

Disadvantages of Unitary Structure 

  1. Manipulative constitution
  2. Despotism
  3. Lack of Local freedom
  4. Unsuitable for diverse society
  5. Lack of popular participation

France and Britain are a good example of Unitary structured government.

Features of Unitary system

  1. Concentration of power
  2. Single constitutional authority
  3. Flexible and unwritten constitution
  4. Homogeneity of culture
  5. Unitary constitution

Unitarism: A system of government that concentrate power at the central government. The central government is the sole constitutional authority.

Federal Structure 

The idea of federalism was first associated with K.C Whare. Federalism exists where there is constitutional sharing of powers between the federal and state government. The first example of federation in western world was Achaen League in ancient Greece. The first federal state in modern history was the Dutch Republic of United Province. 

Federalism can take the form of co-operative or dual system. In cooperatives federalism, federal and state government share powers and consequently are required to co-operate closely with each other e.g. Switzerland and Germany. 

However, in dual federalism there is supposed to be a clear separation of function with each level of government having its own sphere of competent e.g. USA and Australia. Nevertheless, this distinction is only in theory, in practice, the federal and state government co-operate closely on domestic policy. In federalism, both the state and federal government derive their ‘powers from the constitution. However, the central authority is superior to the component units or authority. 

The constitution in a federal structure is usually written, rigid and supreme unlike in a unitary structure where the constitution is usually unwritten and flexible. The USA is a good example of a country that practices federalism. Also, Canada, Germany, Switzerland and Australia are federal states. Nigeria became a true federation in 1954 under the Lyttleton Constitution. The upper house in most federal systems is created to prevent excesses of the executive. Bicameralism makes it difficult for the executive to dominate the two chambers.

Merits of Federalism

  1. Written and rigid constitution
  2. Competition
  3. Division of labour
  4. Local participation
  5. Unity in diversity
  6. Cultural Preservation
  7. Protection of the minority
  8. Equality of state before the law.

Demerits of Federalism

  1. Conflict over power
  2. Ethnic politics
  3. Delayed decision making
  4. Rigidity of constitution
  5. Dual loyalty of citizens
  6. Disunity
  7. Unbalanced development
  8. Unhealthy competition

Features of Federalism

  1. Supremacy of constitution
  2.  Division of power
  3. Ridgid and written constitution
  4. Supreme court interprets constitution
  5. Shared Federal revenue
  6. Strong central Government.

Confederalism 

Confederalism is a loose association of sovereign states. Confederalism is a structure of governance in which independent states come together to form a union with weak central government and strong state government. The earliest form of Confederalism was Swiss confedcracy dating back to 1315. The USA had a cunfederation 1776 - 1788. 

Confederalism is most suitable in a society with diverse culture (multi ethnic). In a confederation, the citizens are under direct control of the state government. Loyalty of the citizens, in a confederation, is to the state government not the Central Union Government. In other words, the citizens pay allegiance to their various state governments. 

There is right of secession of state in confederal structure. A state/country that is no longer satisfied with the association has the right to break away. [Examples of confederations are African Union, ECOWAS, European Union, UNO and other International association of sovereign states. 

Advantages of Confederalism 

1. Unity and cooperation: It promotes socio-economic co-operation among nations with diverse culture. They cooperate to improve their standard of living 

2. Competition: It enhances competition for development among component states. 

3. Non autocratic central government: It prevents despotic central government because the component units are strong. The central government has lesser powers 

4. Right of secession: It encourages the right of secession by any state that wishes to terminate its membership. A state or nation has right to withdraw its membership to a country any time it wants.

5. Suitable for multi-cultural society: It is most suitable for states with people of different culture and ethnic groups because it preserves the diverse culture. 

6. Strong military: It makes it possible for weak states to form a union for strong military gains. They unite their human and material resources to form strong military force such as Army, Navy and Air force.

7. Protection of the minority. It protects the rights and identity of the minority states/groups. The small ethnic groups retain their culture and liberty.

Disadvantages of Confederalism 

1. Political instability: The right of secession of states weakens the association and may cause political instability. If a state breaks away it affects union’s strength 

2. Delay in decision taking: There is delay in decision making because member states have to agree on issues before implementation. 

3. Weak central government: The central authority is weak and has less pewer over states. This affects the capacity of the central government to provide unity 

4. Political crisis

5. Unequal development among member states

6. Lack of national unity because of weak central government.

Features of a Confederation 

1. Voluntary association: It is a loose and voluntary association of sovereign states. 

2. Strong state government: There is a strong component units and weak center. The component states are stronger than the central government. The center is less powerful than the states.

3. Authority vested in states: The source of authority rests with the component states. Sovereignty is vested with the component units that is the states or regions.

4. Secession right (freedom of secession): The confederating states have the right of secession that is right to withdraw membership to a country.

5. Loyalty to state government: Individuals and citizens are under the direct control of their states not central government. 

6. Decentralization: Powers are decentralized and shared between the tiers of government. 

7, The central government can only exercise power with the consent of the confederating units.

Difference between Federalism and Confederalism 

1. In federalism authority is shared between the centre and regional/state government while in confederalism it rests with the component states or regions. 

2. Sovereignty rest with the central government in federalism but in confederalism it lies with the component states/regions.

3. In federalism there is a strong central authority but in confederal state the central government is weak. 

4. The constitution of a federal state is rigid but that of the confederal is flexible.

Decentralization 

Decentralization is the transfer of power from the central government to lower tiers of government in a political system. It is transfer of decision making from centre to lower level which may be regional or local. 

Models/Types of Decentralization 

1. Deconcentration: 

Deconcentration is the transfer of administrative functions from the central administrative bodies to different geographical areas. It is the transfer of power from central administrative authorities to lower administrative authorities that are answerable to the central authority. It is a transfer of administrative roles from the federal authority or government to administrative units or agents of the federal government at the local or regional areas. It can also be defined as the delegation of administrative power to local units with the central supervision and control. Deconcentration, therefore involves redistribution of authority and responsibility among different levels the central government.

2. Devolution of power: 

This is the transfer of power from higher level of government to independent lower level of government. Devolution of power occurs when the federal government transfers any of its power to state or local government or communities. It involves the transfer of power, rights and resources from central government to autonomous local government, communities or lower tier of government. Local government is an example of devolution of power. Devolution of power exists in federal system of government.

3. Delegation: 

This is the transfer of managerial and regulatory function to other bodies or agencies which may be local, regional or national bodies such as quasi-autonomous non-governmental organization. Under this form of decentralization, the central government transfer responsibility for decision making and administrative functions to semi-autonomous organizations such as public corporations, housing authorities and agricultural development authorities. Examples of delegated decentralization in Nigeria include the Nigeria Airways, FRCN. 

4. Privatization: 

Under privatization, permission is granted to community groups, cooperatives, voluntary association, businesses and nongovernmental organizations to provide service either alone or in partnership with public agencies. 

NB: Deconcentration, Devolution and delegation are collectively called administrative decentralisation.

Merits of Decentraralisation

  1. Encourage interstate competition
  2. Democratic participation of people
  3. Efficiency
  4. Protection of minority
  5. Suitable for vast territory

Centralization 

Centralization is the concentration of powers at central level or government. This is predominant in a unitary system in which the central government is the only constitutional source of authority. 

Merits of Centralization

  1. Equality
  2. National unity and national integration
  3. Quick decision making
  4. Protection of local minorities

Demerits of Centralization

  1. Discourages interstate competition
  2. Tyranny
  3. Distance government from the people
  4. Unsuitable and unstable for vast territory
  5. Discourages interdependent of local authority
