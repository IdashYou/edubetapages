1. PRESSURE GROUP

A group of people with common objective of influencing government decisions.

Types of Pressure Group 

Institutional Pressure Group: Nigerian Union of teachers, Nigerian Labour congres.

Non-Institutional Pressure Group: Nigerian Bar Association, Nigerian Union of Journalist.

Anomic Pressure group: This group is informal and unorganized; Rioters and demonstrators.

Professional or occupational / Labour pressure group: Nigerian Medical association, Nigerian Bar Association, ASUU

Economic / Business Pressure Groups: Nigerian Labour Congress, Trade union Congress, Nigerian Chamber of commerce 

Social Pressure Group: Arewa Consultative Forum, MOSOB, MASSOB, Ohaneze ndi Igbo. 

Educational pressure group: ASUU, NANS

Religious Pressure Group: Christian Association of Nigeria 

A Promotional pressure group is one that promotes a particular cause that its members may not be direct beneficiaries like advocating for the less privileged; Society of the deaf and dumb, civil liberty organization. Example Civil Liberties Organization 

 

JAMB Examples

1. An example of promotional pressure group is the____.

A. Supreme Council of Islamic Affairs, B. Nigerian Medical Association

C. Civil Liberties Organization, D. Manufacturers Association of Nigeria

Option C (2017/2018, no 5).

Civil Liberties Organization

 

2. Pressure groups are formed mainly to__________

A. capture state power

B. replace bad governments

C. compete with the military for power

D. satisfy the needs and interests of members

Option D. 2018

Explanation

The main objective of pressure groups are to meet the needs and interests of their members.

 

2. Associational interest groups are organized to

A. further the interest of members

B. specifically lobby the government

C. support the government

D. achieve goals affecting other associations.

Option A. 2018

Explanation

Associational interest groups are organized to further the interests of members.

 

3. A major issue that distinguishes pressure groups from political parties is

A. membership drive

B. the objective

C. the voting pattern

D. idealogy.

Option B

Explanation

Their objectives differ.

Political parties aim at contesting and winning elections and controlling government while pressure groups aim at pursing the interest of their members but do not attempt to control government machinery directly by contesting elections.

Pressure group seek to influence government policy and also protect the interest of their members.
