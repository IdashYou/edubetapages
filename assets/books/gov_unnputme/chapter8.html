8. POLITICAL IDEOLOGIES

Ideology is a systematic set of thought or ideas that provide basis for organized political action. It is a set of political philosophy on how to organize or transform the society to achieve a set out goal. 

Communalism 

It is primitive and the earliest mode of production. In communal state, land was the major means of production. Communalism emphasizes community-oriented development where the members of the community work together and share the produce together. Individuals in communalism do not own privately the means of production or use communal property such as land for personal purpose. 

Communalism is not suitable in contemporary society where the society is individualistic. It is conservative and discourages individual initiative and enterprise. Communalism existed in pre-colonial African society e.g. Igbo. According to Karl Marx, society undergoes some pattern of development. The development patterns are as follows: Communalism, Feudalism, Capitalism, Socialism, and Communism. Under communalism, production was on used value basis not market value as obtain in capitalism. 

Capitalism 

Capitalism as political ideology or economic system refers to where individuals control the means of production and distribution.

In capitalism, there is a class conflict between the bourgeoisie and the proletariat. The bourgeoisie, otherwise refer to as the capitalists, are the owners of means of production and distribution. 

They employ the service of the proletariat, otherwise known as the labour, to produce goods and services. The labour or workers are paid wages and salaries which are not commensurate with their input. The proletariats live on the income earned from the bourgeoisies. The bourgeoisie enjoy the surplus value (profit) produced by the proletariat. This exploitation of the proletariat by the capitalists leads to inequality. 

Socialism 

Karl Marx is generally known as the father of socialism. Socialism, which was a protest against capitalism, was first practiced in the defunct USSR in 1917 when the Bolshevik party overthrew the government of the Menshevik party. Marx argues that the mode of production in material life determines the general character of the social, political and spiritual process of life. 

In other words, it is the substructure (economy) that determines the character of super structure (politics). 

Marx believes in material interpretation of history: dialectical historical materialism. Socialism is an economic system or political ideology in which the major means of production and distribution are owned and control by the state or government. It emphasizes collectivism as against individualism. 

Socialism, sometimes called central planned economy or collectivism, considers labour as the most essential productive force. Ujamaa was the brand of African socialism practiced in Tanzania by President Julius Nyerere. Ujamaa was concerned with villagization and communalization of agricultural production. 

Humanism was the brand of African socialism practiced by Kenneth Kaunda of Zambia. Titoism is the name given to a moderate form of communism as practiced in Yugoslavia.

Features of communism

1. Total ownership of means of production and distribution, by the state.

2. Absence of classes, ie classes society.

3. Centrally planned economy.

4. Authoritarian and Totalitarian government

Communism

Communism is a system which recognizes the existence of the state. It is also a communal organization of social existence on the basis of collective ownership of property (common ownership). 

It emphasizes the withering away of state. In other words, it is a stateless or classless society where the society is organized on the principle. "From each according to his capacity and to each according to his need.

In other words, in communism the distribution of goods and services, that is, the sharing principle is according to the needs and wants of the individual not minding their contributions.

All the means of production and distribution is collectively owned. The former USSR under Stalin and China (1949) claimed to have practiced communism. 

The thought and ideas upon which communism was founded were developed by Karl Marx and Frederick Engel in Communist Manifesto published in 1848. Karl Marx is however considered as father of communism. Mao-Tse-Tung was the founder of Chinese communism. 

Fascism

Facism is a way of organizing a society in which a government ruled by a dictator controls the lives of the people and in which people are not allowed to disagree with the government. Fascism originated in Italy. 

Fascism regime requires people to obey rules or laws giving no room for personal freedom(authoritarian) and controlling the people of a country in a very strict way with complete power that cannot be opposed (totalitarian).

Nazism

The body of political and economic doctrines held and put into effect by the Nazis in Germany from 1933 to 1945 including the totalitarian principle of government, predominance of especially Germanic groups assumed to be racially superior, and supremacy of the führer

Feudalism

Feudalism is the system of political organization prevailing in Europe from the 9th to about the 15th centuries having as its basis the relation of lord to vassal with all land held in fee and as chief characteristics homage, the service of tenants under arms and in court, wardship, and forfeiture. Serf is a member of a servile feudal class bound to the land and subject to the will of its owner.
