11. CONSTITUTION

The constitution of a country (or a state) is a legal document representing the principles and ways by which states are governed and how its government is supposed to work. It tells how the country’s leaders are to be chosen and how long they get to stay in office, how new laws are made and old laws are to be changed or removed based on law, what kind of people are allowed to vote and what other rights they are guaranteed, and how the constitution can be changed. During the military government of Murtala/Obasanjo appointed a 50 men Constitutional Drafting Committee which was headed by Chief Rotimi William. The report was later submitted to a 230 Member Constituent Assembly headed by Justice Udo Udoma for further debate and sanction.

Scope and features of constitution. 

1. The constitution defines the party system of a state. 

2. It contains the fundamental objectives and principle of a state 

3. It contains the rights of citizens. 

4. It stipulate the political structure of a state. 

5. It contains amendment process of the state. 

Functions of constitution 

 1. Political independence: constitution symbolizes sovereignty of a state

 2. Fundamental law: It serves as the law of a state and specifies the rules and regulation of state. 

 3. Organs of government: It stipulates the institutions of state and functions. 

 4. System of government: it specifies the structure and governmental system in practice.

 5. Human rights: it stipulates and protect the citizen's liberty 

 Factors to be considered in drawing a constitution

1. Preamble: The constitution must have a preamble; that is, an introduction stating the ideological principles of the constitution.

2. Human rights: The fundamental human rights of the citizens must be considered 

3. Organs of state: The functions and interaction among the organs of government must be stated. 

4. System of government: The system of government; for instance, presidential or parliamentary should be stated. 

5. Amendment procedure: The methods and procedure of amending the constitution has to be stated. 

Main Sources of Nigeria Constitution 

1. Equity: the principle of equity serves as a source of constitution. 

2. Constitutional conference: The results and recommendation of constitutional conference and drafting committee as well as constituent assembly are constitutional sources. 

3. Judicial Precedents (case laws): These are rulings of the courts that have found their ways into the constitution. Judicial rules and decisions can be used as the bases of another decision in the future, especially where the cases are similar.

4. Act of national assembly National assembly: the law or statute made by the legislature such as national assembly is a source of constitution in Nigeria. 

5. The citizens: the people are the primary source of constitution because sovereignty belongs to the people. 

7. Constitutions of other countries: A country's constitution formation may involve studying the constitution of other countries, and picking up best practices which are then incorporated into the new constitution. 

8. Customs and conventions: The customs and practices of the people can also be the source of a constitution. 

9. Previous constitutions: The previous constitutions of a state may serve as the source of a country’s new constitution. 

10. Decisions of a constituent Assembly: Another source of a constitution is the decision of a Constituent Assembly that has been put together to draft a constitution for a country. 

Functions of the Constitution

1. Constitution projects the form of government in the state (country).

2. It establishes the fundamental rights of its citizens.

3. It directs the states in making legislation.

4. It outlines the procedures for several functions, administrations, legislation, execution of the government machinery.

5. It enables the practice of separation of Powers.

6. It provides for the independence of each organ, i.e. legislative, executive and judiciary.

7. It upholds the sovereignty of the nation.

8. It acts as a check in maladministration and misuse of powers.

9. It promotes accountability of the government to the people of the country.

10. Constitution gives room for judicial review in case of laws violating the Supreme procedure established by law (i.e. Constitution).

Written Constitution 

A written constitution simply refers to a constitution that the rules are codified in a single document. They are usually rigid and federal in character and have a preamble. Nigeria, USA, Canada and Australia work with written constitution. San Marino developed the first codified constitution 1600. Nigeria first adopted a written constitution in 1954 when the federal system was established under Littleton constitution. 

Features and Importance of Written Constitution

These are the advantages and reasons the adoption of Written Constitution in a state. 

1. Easy reference: written constitution can easily be cited in time of dispute among individuals, groups and government.

2. Rigidity: it can be amended through a long process prevents manipulation by executive. 

3. Charity of rules: It has a clear expression of fundamental principles and rules of the state. 

4. Charity of human rights: It states clearly the human rights of the citizens and as such helps in effective protection rights of citizens in a state.

5. Stability of government: It states clearly the means of changing government and thus promoting stability. 

Disadvantages /Defects of Constitution 

1. Ambiguity of words: it contains words that may be clear and prone to different interpretations and meaning. 

2. Expensive: it requires huge financial and material resources are involved in amendment and production of constitution. 

3. Frequent litigation: It is prone disputes over roles among, various tiers and level of government. 

4. Unsuitable for illiterate citizens: The content of the constitution may not be known by the illiterate’s citizens in the society. 

Unwritten constitution  

Unwritten constitution simply means a constitution that the rules and regulations is not codified in a single document. Here, the rules and regulations are contained in several documents unlike written constitution that is written in one document. Some of the examples are the British and Israel constitution.

Features and importance of Unwritten constitution 

1. Several documentations: It is contained in no single legal documents. 

2. Cheap: The amendment process is easy and requires less material and financial resources. 

3. Easy amendment of the constitution: Its flexibility makes it easy to amend and suitable in time of emergency. 

4. Preservation of customs: It preserves the customs and tradition of the people. 

5. Response to change: it responds to changes and conditions in the society because it evolves with time. 

Demerits of unwritten constitution

1. Uneasy to reference: since the document is not in a single document, it will not be easy to reference.

2. Prone to tyranny and Dictatorship: this due to the fact that the constitution is uneasy to amend and therefore leads to tyranny.

3. Not suitable for a federal state: it is not practicable in a federal state with multi nationalities. 

4. Political instability: it can lead to instability in government because it can be easily manipulated. 

5. Lack of clear expression of human right:  the rights of the citizens are not clear enough making them unable to seek redress.

Rigid Constitution

A rigid constitution is a constitution that requires a laid down procedure for its amendment. This is obtainable in Nigeria. The amendment of the 1999 constitution of Nigeria required the support of two-third support of the members of the national assembly and two-third support of the state houses of assembly. This constitution is obtainable in other countries like USA, Switzerland, South Africa, Australia and Canada.

Features and Merits of Rigid Constitution

1. Enables the protection of Human rights: the constitution is clearly stated and uneasy to be manipulated.

2. Clarity and certainty of amendment procedure: the government are bound to follow a certain laid down procedure for amending the constitution.

3. suitable for a federal state: it can work well in a federal state with heterogenous population and diverse culture.

4. Easy Reference: rigid constitution can easily be cited in time of dispute among individuals, groups and government.

5. Prevention of arbitrary change: arbitrary changes are controlled with the laid down procedure for every constitutional change.

6. Stability and smooth Change in Government: rigid constitution makes change in the government constitution easy. 

Demerits of Rigid Constitution 

1. Unsuitable for emergency situation: It is not easily emergency situation when an urgent act of challenges because it is difficult to amend. 

2. Expensive to operate: It is expensive to operate because the amendment and production of copies of the constitution require huge material and financial resources. 

3. Frequent litigation: It is subject to frequent conflicts because some sections of it may contain words with different interpretations. 

4. Unsuitable for illiterates: It is usually written and may not be suitable in an illiterate society.

Flexible Constitution

Flexible is a constitution in which its amendment procedure is simple like passing ordinary law country like Britain, Finland, New Zealand and Italy have flexible constitution. 

Features and Merits of Flexible Constitution 

1. Easy amendment: A flexible Constitution is suitable for emergency situation because it can easily be amended. 

2. Suitable in unitary and non multi-cultural state: It is suitable in a non-pluralistic society; that is, a state with homogeneous population. 

3. Unwritten in nature: It is usually unwritten and eliminates the problem of difficult 

  interpretation and incidence of frequent court cases. 

4. Less expensive: it is less expensive to operate because it is not codified and the 

  amendment procedure involves no much resource. 

Demerits of Flexible Constitution

1. Easy manipulation: Due to its flexible nature, constitution may be misused by tyrants and dictators because it can be easily amended anytime for selfish purposes.

2. Non clarity of human right: there is no clarity of the rights of the citizens because it is usually an unwritten constitution. 

3. Lack of single document: There is no single document called constitution because the rules and regulations are not codified. 

4. Political instability: It may lead to instability/changes in government owing to frequent amendment.

5. Lack of easy reference: Reference cannot be easily made to sections of the constitution because it is not in a single document 

Unitary constitution 

A unitary constitution refers to a constitution that provides for a centralize structure of governance, and does not recognize or assign roles to the region, state or local government as they may be called. However, the central government can decongest its power by deconcentration and delegation of powers to the sub national units. Unitary constitution is good in a non-pluralistic society. It usually recognizes unicameralism; one legislative chamber. For example, France and British constitutions are unitary. 

Features of Unitary Constitution 

1. Unitary constitution are usually flexible in nature. 

2. It recognizes one strong central government. 

3. It does not assign roles to the regions or local authority. 

4. The constitution may not be supreme 

5. It is usually operated in a country with small territory and homogeneous culture 

Merits of Unitary Constitution why some State Operate Unitary Constitution 

1. Effective Response to changes: It responds to changes and conditions in the society because it 

  evolves with time. 

2. Less litigation: It is less prone to misinterpretation and as such reduces the incidence 

  of disputes among the tiers of government. 

3. Suitable for literates and illiterate (cultural preservation): The knowledge of the rules and regulation is widely known both by the literate and the illiterate ones in society because of its unwritten nature. 

4. Less expensive: The amendment process is easy and requires less material and financial resources.

5. Cultural preservation: It preserves the custom and tradition of the people. 

6: Political stability: It provides for strong and stable central government. 

Demerits of Unitary Constitution

1. Lack of easy reference: Reference cannot be easily made to the constitution because it may not be in a single document 

2. Lack of local freedom: It does not encourage local autonomy/self-government. 

3. Unsuitable for multi-ethnic society: It is not suitable for multi-cultural society with vast territory because it may not protect minority interest. 

4. Centralization of power: It over concentrate power at the central government and 

  makes it over powerful. This may lead to despotism. 

5. Non protection of minority rights: It does not protect the interest of the minority group because the majority ethnic groups always dominate in government. 

Federal Constitution 

A federal constitution provides for decentralization of powers among the various levels (tiers) of government (Federal, State and local government). The Federal government exercise exclusive powers, the state performs residual powers and both exercise concurrent powers. Federal constitution has been operated in USA, Nigeria, Canada and Switzerland. 

Features of federal constitution 

1. Written and rigid: It is in written form and cannot easily be changed. 

2. Bicameral legislature: It usually provides for a bicameral legislature, the upper md the lower chambers. 

3. Division Of power: The constitution decentralizes power among the levels of  government. 

4. Rigomus amendment: It has special procedure for amendment. This process is long. 

5. Preamble: It always has a preamble; that is, an introduction which states the intention and ideology of the makers. 

Merits of Federal Constitution

These are the reasons why some states adopt federal constitution

1. Clarity of citizens' rights: It states clearly the duties, rights and obligations of citizens; consequently, citizens can seek redress for the abuse of their rights. 

2.  local freedom: It encourages autonomy of local authorities and local political participation and thereby brings government to grassroots 

3. Easy reference: It is always written and codified in a single document and thus can easily be referred to by citizens and government 

4. Prevention of dictatorship: It is rigid and cannot be easily manipulated by dictators and tyrant for their selfish interest. 

5. Suitable for multi-ethnic society: Federal constitution is suitable for heterogeneous population where there are diverse cultures 

Demerits of Federal Constitution Why Some States Do Not Operate It

1. Unsuitable for emergency period: Federal constitution is not suitable for Emergency situation when there is an urgent need for quick amendment because of its rigid nature 

2. Expensive to operate: The constitution is expensive because the amendment Process and production of copies of the constitution require huge financial, material and human resources 

3. Frequent litigation: The constitution may lead to constant conflicts between the 

  federal government and the states and between states and states because of ambiguity. 

4. Duplication of roles: Federal constitution creates duplicated offices at state and federal levels and this may lead to conflicts as well as increase in the cost of administration 

Confederal Constitution 

Confederal constitution is a constitution that creates a loosed association of sovereign states in which the component states are stronger than the federal government in terms of the exercise of powers. 

Characteristics of Confederal Constitution 

1. Suitable for diverse population: The constitution is most suitable for a country with large population and diverse culture. 

2. Secession right: The constitution provides for the right of secession of component states. 

3. Easy reference: The constitution is always written and can be referred to by member states. 

4. Independent states: The constitution allows the component states to retain their sovereignty. 

5. Strong state: The constitution provides for strong component states and weak central authority. 

Merits of Confederal Constitution 

Why Some States Operate it

1. sovereign states: The constitution enables the component states to retain their independence. 

2. Powerful nation: Confederal constitution brings weak component states together to form a powerful nation. 

3. Mutual cooperation: It promotes mutual cooperation among component states in areas of security, economy, politics etc. 

4. Voluntary association: It makes it possible for a union to be formed by people of different cultural background. 

5. Protection of minority interest: Confederal constitution reduces fear of the minority group or state because each retains its identity. 

Demerits of confederal Constitution

1. Political instability: The constitution creates bounce of serious political instability by allowing tor the right of states to secede at their wills. 

2. Unfair development: it creates unfair development because some states are more blessed with resources. 

3. Feeble central government: The constitution undermines the authority of the central government by weakening the power of the at the center. 

4. Civil unrest: The power of component states to retain thief armed forces may lead to civil unrest and wars among the confederated units. 

Separation of Powers

Separation of powers means that the three arms(branches) of government are separated. The Legislative arm makes laws, the Executive arm carries out (executes) the laws, while the Judicial arm which includes the courts, decide if the law has been broken or maintained. 

Separation of powers is also called a system of checks and balances because the branches can check up on each other and if any of the branches get too strong, that branch will be balanced by the others.

It helps to ensure that the people are safe. The executive branch carries out the laws but cannot make laws to make themselves too powerful. Also, the judiciary is responsible for making sure that criminals are punished so that members of the government or legislature cannot ignore the law as the judiciary can check on them.

Checks and Balances Work

The system of checks and balances is an important part of the Constitution. With checks and balances, each of the three branches of government can limit the powers of the others. This way, no one branch becomes too powerful. Each branch “checks” the power of the other branches to make sure that the power is balanced between them. How does this system of checks and balances work?

The process of how laws are made is a good example of checks and balances in action. First, the legislative branch introduces and votes on a bill. The bill then goes to the executive branch, where the President decides whether he thinks the bill is good for the country. If so, he signs the bill, and it becomes a law.

If the President does not believe the bill is good for the country, he does not sign it. This is called a veto. But the legislative branch gets another chance. With enough votes, the legislative branch can override the executive branch’s veto, and the bill becomes a law.

Once a law is in place, the people of the country can test it through the court system, which is under the control of the judicial branch. If someone believes a law is unfair, a lawsuit can be filed. Lawyers then make arguments for and against the case, and a judge decides which side has presented the most convincing arguments. The side that loses can choose to appeal to a higher court, and may eventually reach the highest court of all, the Supreme Court.

If the legislative branch does not agree with the way in which the judicial branch has interpreted the law, they can introduce a new piece of legislation, and the process starts all over again.

Rule of law is a legal maxim that suggests that no one is above the law and governmental decisions must be made only by applying known legal and moral principles. The Rule of Law limits the powers of Government by judicial defense of laws and the Constitution which is based on recognized basic legal values, established in international law. The Rule of Law is meant to prevent dictatorship and to protect the rights of the people. It also promotes economic development in a development.

The rule of law means that all authority and power must come from an ultimate source of law.

Characteristics of the Rule of Law

1. Supremacy of the Law: The law is the highest reference point in every situation. This prevents manipulation by those in authority. No one is above the law

2. Certainty of the law: The people know what to do and what not to do as represented in the law.

3. Equality before the law: This means that the law must not be based on the class of the individuals involved. It also means that every person must be entitled to equal protection of the law.

4. Individual Rights to Personal Freedom: Everybody has the right to life, no person shall be subjected to torture or to any form of inhuman or degrading treatment and she or he also has the right to work, personal safety, education, property and the right to vote and to stand as a candidate for election.

5. Judicial Independence: Judicial independence is the concept that the judiciary should be independent from the other branches of government. That is, courts should not be subject to improper influence from the other branches of government or from private or partisan. This means all means of legislative or executive influence on judicial decision making must be disabled,  Judicial independence serves as a safeguard for the rights and privileges provided by a limited constitution and prevent executive and legislative encroachment upon those rights. It serves as a foundation for the rule of law and democracy.

Limitations and Exceptions to the Rule of Law

1. Delegated Legislation: The grant of discretionary powers to government officials to enable them perform their duties effectively can be used against the citizens. 

2. Diplomatic Immunity: Ambassadors and diplomats cannot be sued and prosecuted in countries where they are serving as they are treated as above the law of the host country, rather they can be repatriated.

3. Existence of Administrative Tribunals or Special Courts: These courts and tribunals use special procedures that are not observed in civil courts. Most of the judgments of such tribunals forbids individual rights of appeal.

4. Special Privileges: In reality there are some certain individuals that are immune against the law of the country. Such people include the President and the Vice President, Governors, members of the Parliament (National Assembly) and some other high-ranking government officials. Judges are immune from law while adjudicating in the court. This is to make them to carry out their duty without fear or favour.

5. State of Emergency and War: In some critical conditions the government might declare a state of emergency or when there is war, government usually invoke certain discretionary powers. These powers when exercised by the government usually denies individuals part of their fundamental human rights as people may be confined to their homes or restricted from carrying out certain activities or individuals may be forced to go and fight war to defend their country even if it is against their wish.

6. Poverty, Illiteracy and Ignorance: The high level of poverty and illiteracy in a country, especially developing countries do not allow them to pursue or sue anyone that infringed their rights.
