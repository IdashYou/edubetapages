4. ARMS OF GOVERNMENT

The legislature: 

This is the arm of government that its primary responsibility is the enactment of law for peace, order and good government. 

Functions of the Legislature 

1. Law making: The legislature enacts legislation/ law for the governance of state. 

2. Removal of the executive: The legislature can impeach the executive such as the president and governor on grounds of constitutional misconducts. 

3. Approval of appointment: The legislature undertakes the ratification/confirmation of executive appointees such as ministers, commissioners, ambassadors and high commissioners. These appointees in Nigeria are confirmed by the senate. 

4. Constitutional changes: The legislature is responsible for constitutional amendment in a state. 

5. Expression of public opinion: The legislature engages in articulation of peoples’ views on public matters. 

6. Harmonization of diverse opinion: The legislature engages in interest aggregation 

7. Checks and balances: The legislature carries out scrutiny and oversight over executive actions. 

8. Representation: The legislature serves as representative body of the people. 

9. Approval of public expenditure: The legislature is responsible for approval of budget/appropriation bill of a state. 

10. Future leaders: The legislature provides a training ground for future leaders of a state. 

11. judicial function: The legislature, for instance the House of Lord in Britain, performs judicial functions by serving as the court of appeal. Etc. 

The power of legislature can be checked or controlled through the following measures: 

1. The executive can veto (ref use to sign into law) the bill passed by the legislature 

2. The legislature is guided by the constitutional provisions in its legislative conducts 

3. The judiciary can review the actions of the legislature and declare them illegal e.t.c

Nigeria Law Making Body

In Nigeria the law-making body at the federal level is the National Assembly, which is divided into the Senate and House of Representatives. Representation of states in the Senate is based on equality, while that of the House of Representatives is based on population which determines the number of constituencies in a state. 

Under the 1999 Constitution, each state in Nigeria is represented by three senators. Election of members into the Senate is through the senatorial district but that of the House of Representatives is through federal constituencies. The federal legislature is bicameral while that of the state is unicameral: House of Assembly. The legislative body at local government level in Nigeria consists of the councilors and is known as local government council. 

In the First Republic of Nigeria, (1963 -1966) the federal body that made law was called parliament but in the Second Republic: (1979 1983) it was changed to National Assembly. The total number of senators under the 1999 Constitution is 109 while the House of Representatives has 360 members. 

Under the1960 Constitution, the federal legislature in Nigeria consisted of the British monarch, a Senate of 44 members with 12 members from each region and the House of Representatives consisting 312 members. The life of parliament under 1960 and 1963 constitutions of Nigeria were five years but under the 1979 and 1999 constitutions it is four years. 

The first Republic of Nigeria (1963 1966) had 56 Senators. In the second Republic (1979-1983) the total number of House of Representatives members was 450 while that of the Senate was 96. Each state in the Second Republic was represented by five Senators. In the aborted Third Republic, under General Babangida Transition to civil rule, the House of Representatives were made up of 593 members but Senate was made up of 91 members with three Senators from each state. 

In Britain, the Queen, the House of Lord and the House of Common constitute the law-making body. The House of the Lord is the Upper House and is presided over by the Lord Chancellor and the House of Common is the Lower House. The members of House of Lord are appointed not elected.

Types of legislature 

A bicameral legislature has dual chambers while unicameral legislature has one chamber. Bicameral legislature which is a common feature of federalism has some merits. Example of countries that practice bicameral legislature include: USA, Canada, Britain, Nigeria, and India while those of unicameral include: Greece, Turkey, Bulgaria, Israel, Denmark Newzealand and Spain. in the year 1960 the central legislature of Nigeria became bicameral. McPherson constitution of 1951 created two chambers regional legislatures known as the house of chiefs and the house of Assembly in the Northern and Western region. Coming then to independence constitution of 1960. Bicameral legislatures were adopted in both the federal and regional government including the Eastern region.

Merits of Bicameral Legislature 

1. Prevention of tyranny/despotisms 

2. Use of experienced politicians

3. Prevention of passage of harmful law

4. Equal representation

5. Increased Participation

Demerits of Bicameral Legislation

  1. Delay in law making
  2. Expensive to operate
  3. Conflict of power
  4. Non-elected senator

Merits of Unicameral Legislature

  1. Quick law making
  2. Lack of conflict
  3. Suitable for emergency
  4. Cheap to operate

Demerits of Unicameral Legislature

  1. Unequal representation of states
  2. Despotism
  3. Passage of harmful law
  4. Vulnerable to executive influence
  5. Non protection of minority interest

Delegated legislation 

Delegated legislation means legislation made by bodies other than legislature, but these legislations are sanctioned by the legislature.

Reasons for delegated legislation are as follows:

1. Pressure on the time and volume of work of parliament.

2. Technicalities of modern legislation

3. The need for legislative flexibility and thoroughness.

4. The need to cope with emergency situations

5. Suitability for experimentation

6. To ensure speedy legislative process.

The Executive 

The executive is the second main branch of government with the primary responsibility of implementing laws. 

Functions of the Executive 

  1. implementing of law: The executive is responsible for the enforcement of law made by the legislature.
  2. Policy formulation: The executive creates policies and program of government.
  3. Foreign affairs: they conduct foreign affair of the state and maintain friendly relation with international community.
  4. Granting of pardon
  5. Summon and termination of Parliament
  6. Ministerial appointment
  7. Drafting of bill
  8. Veto of bill: the executive can reject and veto bill passed by the legislature
  9. Provision of infrastructure
  10. Drafting of budget
  11. Maintenance of law and order

Judiciary

The judiciary is a branch of government in which judicial power is vested. Two Judicial Functions include;

Law making through interpretation: The judiciary engages in law making through constitutional interpretation (procedural). This type of law is called case law 

Interpretation of law: The judiciary adjudicates /interprets law and dispenses justice. 

Judicial Independence

Judicial independence is the concept that the judiciary should be independent from the other branches of government. That is, courts should not be subject to improper influence from the other branches of government or from private or partisan. This means all means of legislative or executive influence on judicial decision making must be disabled, Judicial independence serves as a safeguard for the rights and privileges provided by a limited constitution and prevent executive and legislative encroachment upon those rights. It serves as a foundation for the rule of law and democracy.

The final interpretation pf the provisions of a federal constitution is vested in the highest court of the land. The Judiciary interpret law, while legislature makes law. In Nigeria the supreme court is the highest court.

One of the main characteristics of a Federal System of Government is the existence of a court to interpret the constitution and resolve conflicts between the centre and the federating units.

NB: Judicial precedence are earlier judgements passed by higher courts such as Supreme Court. It is one of the major sources of constitution. 

A writ is also used to order a lower court or government agency to complete a duty to uphold the law or to correct an abuse of discretion.

Swearing in of member of the Houses of Assembly is the responsibility of the Judiciary 

Case-laws consists of judgments given by higher courts in interpreting the statutes or the provisions of the constitution, that is applicable in cases brought before them. Case-laws are part of common law can be called precedents and are binding on all courts in the same jurisdiction, to be followed as the law in similar cases. 

Exclusive list deals with matters in which Federal government alone can legislate. Concurrent list deals with matters which are shared between Federal and state government. Residual list deals with the left-over matters which are neither in Exclusive nor concurrent list

The legislative body of the United States of America is the Congress. According to Ade Fasuba (1972) '' All legislative powers herein granted shall be vested in a congress of the United States, which shall consist of a Senate and a House of Representative.

The upper house in most federal systems is created to prevent excesses of the executive. Bicameralism makes it difficult for the executive to dominate the two chambers.
