10. PARTY SYSTEM

Party system refers to a network of relationships through which parties interact and influence the political process. 

Types of Party System 

Multi-party or group system: A multi-party system is the type in which more than two political parties are allowed to operate and compete for political power in a state. For instance, in the first, second and fourth republic, Nigeria, operated a multi-party system. 

One Party System: This constitutionally allows for existence of only one Political party. This is obtainable mostly in a communist or socialist and fascist/totalitarian state. A state with a hegemonic political party has one dominant party.

Coalition government arises when no party has a majority in the parliament. 

Functions / Roles of Political Parties 

  1. Electoral contest
  2. Organisation and education of the electorate
  3. Continuity of policy
  4. Smooth transfer of power
  5. Recruitment and training of politicians
  6. Opposition government
  7. National unity

NB: National Party of Nigeria (NPN). NPN was the widest spread of, membership in Nigeria during the Second Republic and it was formed by former leaders of Northern People’s Congress (NPC).

 

PUTME Examples

1. One of the functions of political parties is to____.

A. make laws, B. declare a state of emergency, C. interpret the constitution, D. aggregate interest.

Option D (2006/2007, no 8).

 

2. The disadvantage of one-party system is that it____.

A. makes accountability difficult, B. negates freedom of association, C. emphasize political differences, D. delays decision making

Option B (2008/2009, no 1)

negates freedom of association

 

3. One of the distinctive features of one- party system is that____.

A. the ruling party is the only legal party, B. communism is banned

C. the communist party is the only Legal party, D. elections to the legislature are held at the party's conferences

Option A (2014/2015, no 1)

the ruling party is the only legal party in one-party system.

1. The political party serves as________

A. A platform upon which the rights and liberties of the people can be attained

B. The group aggregating the interest of all in the state

C. A platform for rebelling against military rule

D. A platform for interest representation

Option A. 2019

Explanation

Basically, the essence of political parties is to ensure that the interests, entitlements and aspirations of the people are realised through the emergence of a responsible government to lead the people.

9. A common feature of a multi-party system is that government is formed by

A. the major political party

B. all the registered political parties

C. a coalition of political parties

D. the party with the highest votes.

Option C. 2018

Explanation

In multi-party system the government is formed by a coalition of political parties.

When there are several political parties in a country, it then become difficult for one party to win a clear majority in parliament. The part that has won the scanty majority will be forced to form a coalition government with another part. e.g. NPN and NNP during the second Republic.
